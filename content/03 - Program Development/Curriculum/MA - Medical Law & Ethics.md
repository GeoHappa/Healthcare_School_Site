# Medical Law & Ethics - Curriculum Module

> **Program:** Clinical Medical Assistant
> **Module:** 1.4 - Medical Law & Ethics
> **Hours:** 8 didactic hours
> **Prerequisites:** Introduction to Healthcare

---

## Module Overview

This module provides foundational knowledge of the legal and ethical principles that govern healthcare practice. Students learn the legal framework within which medical assistants operate, patient rights, professional liability, and ethical decision-making.

---

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion of this module, students will be able to:

1. Differentiate between laws, ethics, and bioethics in healthcare
2. Explain the medical assistant's scope of practice in California
3. Identify types of legal liability affecting healthcare workers
4. Describe patient rights including informed consent and privacy
5. Apply ethical principles to healthcare scenarios
6. Recognize and respond to situations involving abuse or neglect
7. Understand documentation requirements for legal protection
8. Explain the importance of professional boundaries

## Outline with Time Allocations

### Unit 1: Introduction to Medical Law (2 hours)

**1.1 Sources of Healthcare Law (30 min)**
- Federal vs. state law
- Statutory law (legislation)
- Administrative law (regulations)
- Common law (court decisions)
- California-specific healthcare regulations

**1.2 The Legal System and Healthcare (30 min)**
- Civil vs. criminal law
- Tort law and negligence
- Contract law in healthcare
- How lawsuits proceed

**1.3 Scope of Practice (30 min)**
- Medical assistant scope in California
- Delegation and supervision requirements
- Tasks MAs can and cannot perform
- State-specific regulations (Business & Professions Code ยง2069)
- When to refuse an inappropriate task

**1.4 Credentialing and Certification (30 min)**
- Difference between licensure, certification, and registration
- CMA, RMA, CCMA credentials
- Maintaining credentials
- Continuing education requirements

---

### Unit 2: Professional Liability (2 hours)

**2.1 Types of Liability (30 min)**
- Personal liability
- Vicarious liability (respondeat superior)
- Corporate liability
- Standard of care

**2.2 Negligence and Malpractice (45 min)**
- Four elements of negligence:
  - Duty
  - Breach
  - Causation
  - Damages
- Medical malpractice claims
- MA involvement in malpractice cases
- Case studies and examples

**2.3 Risk Management (45 min)**
- Documentation as legal protection
- Incident reporting
- Error prevention
- Professional liability insurance
- What to do if served with legal papers

---

### Unit 3: Patient Rights (2 hours)

**3.1 Informed Consent (30 min)**
- Elements of valid consent
- Who can give consent
- Minors and consent (emancipated minors, mature minor doctrine)
- Emergency exceptions
- Documenting consent
- MA role in consent process

**3.2 Patient Self-Determination (30 min)**
- Advance directives
- Living wills
- Healthcare power of attorney
- Do Not Resuscitate (DNR) orders
- POLST (Physician Orders for Life-Sustaining Treatment)
- Patient Advocate role

**3.3 Confidentiality and Privacy (45 min)**
- Right to privacy
- Confidentiality obligations
- Exceptions to confidentiality:
  - Mandatory reporting (abuse, communicable diseases)
  - Court orders
  - Patient authorization
- Breach of confidentiality consequences

**3.4 Patient Bill of Rights (15 min)**
- California Patient's Rights
- Right to access records
- Right to refuse treatment
- Right to be informed

---

### Unit 4: Ethics in Healthcare (2 hours)

**4.1 Ethical Principles (30 min)**
- Autonomy (patient self-determination)
- Beneficence (doing good)
- Nonmaleficence (do no harm)
- Justice (fair treatment)
- Veracity (truthfulness)
- Fidelity (keeping promises)

**4.2 Ethical Decision-Making (30 min)**
- Ethical vs. legal obligations
- Ethical dilemma framework
- Role of ethics committees
- When ethics and law conflict
- Conscientious objection

**4.3 Bioethics Topics (30 min)**
- End-of-life issues
- Genetic testing and privacy
- Organ donation
- Resource allocation
- Research ethics

**4.4 Professional Ethics (30 min)**
- Professional boundaries
- Gifts and gratuities
- Social media and patient privacy
- Dating patients/families
- Personal relationships in the workplace
- Impaired colleague situations

## Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Scope of Practice
Maria, an MA, is asked by a physician to administer a medication via IV push. The office is busy, and the physician insists there's no time to wait for the nurse.
- **Discussion:** What should Maria do? What are the legal implications?

### Case Study 2: Confidentiality
A patient's employer calls asking about a workers' compensation injury. The employer states they need information for insurance purposes.
- **Discussion:** Can the MA release information? What documentation is needed?

### Case Study 3: Informed Consent
A 16-year-old patient comes for a pregnancy test without parental knowledge. She asks the MA not to tell her parents.
- **Discussion:** What are the MA's obligations? California minor consent laws?

### Case Study 4: Ethics in Practice
An MA notices that a coworker has been pocketing sample medications. When confronted, the coworker says they're for personal use due to financial difficulties.
- **Discussion:** What ethical principles apply? What should the MA do?

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Scope of Practice** | The range of responsibilities and activities a healthcare professional is permitted to perform |
| **Negligence** | Failure to exercise the care that a reasonably prudent person would exercise |
| **Malpractice** | Professional negligence by a healthcare provider |
| **Informed Consent** | Patient's voluntary agreement to treatment after understanding risks and alternatives |
| **Respondeat Superior** | "Let the master answer" - employer liability for employee actions |
| **Bioethics** | Study of ethical issues arising from biological and medical advances |
| **HIPAA** | Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (privacy regulations) |
| **Advance Directive** | Legal document expressing patient's wishes for future medical care |
