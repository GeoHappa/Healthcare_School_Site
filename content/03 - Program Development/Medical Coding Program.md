## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, graduates will be able to:
1. Apply ICD-10-CM codes for diagnoses
2. Apply CPT codes for procedures and services
3. Apply HCPCS Level II codes for supplies and equipment
4. Navigate medical records to extract coding information
5. Understand anatomy, physiology, and medical terminology
6. Apply coding guidelines and compliance regulations
7. Process insurance claims and understand reimbursement
8. Use electronic health records and coding software

## Certification Pathways

### AAPC Certifications (Outpatient Focus)

**CPC (Certified Professional Coder)**
- Most common entry-level credential
- 150 questions, 5 hours 40 minutes
- Open book (bring code books)
- Passing score: 70%
- Our students target: CPC

**CPC-A (Apprentice)**
- Awarded upon passing exam without experience
- Upgrade to CPC after 2 years experience

### AHIMA Certifications (Inpatient Focus)

**CCA (Certified Coding Associate)**
- Entry-level credential
- 100 questions, 2 hours
- Computer-based (codes provided)
- Good stepping stone to CCS

**CCS (Certified Coding Specialist)**
- Advanced credential
- Requires more experience
- Preferred by hospitals

### Our Recommendation
Prepare students for **CPC (AAPC)** as primary target:
- Most job postings request CPC
- Open-book format matches our training
- Clear pathway from CPC-A to CPC
