---

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, graduates will be able to:
1. Perform venipuncture using proper technique
2. Perform skin puncture (capillary) blood collection
3. Identify proper blood collection tubes and order of draw
4. Apply infection control and safety procedures
5. Handle, process, and transport specimens correctly
6. Communicate effectively with patients and healthcare team
7. Maintain accurate documentation and records
8. Recognize and respond to adverse patient reactions

## Clinical Training Sites

### Site Requirements
- CLIA-certified laboratory
- Adequate patient volume (50+ draws/day)
- Qualified supervisor (CPT I or higher)
- Signed affiliation agreement

### Ideal Partners
- Hospital laboratories
- Outpatient blood draw stations
- Urgent care centers
- Large medical group practices
- Blood donation centers

### Supervision Requirements
- On-site supervisor with CPT I, MLT, or MLS credential
- Direct observation of first 10 draws
- Sign-off on all competency verifications

## Instructor Requirements

### Qualifications
- CPT I or CPT II certification (or MLT/MLS)

- Teaching experience preferred

## Admissions Requirements

- High school diploma or GED
- 18 years of age or older
- Ability to read/write English
- Manual dexterity assessment
- No fear of blood or needles

### Pre-Externship Requirements
- Background check cleared
- Proof of immunizations (Hepatitis B, MMR, Varicella, Tdap)
- Negative TB test (within 1 year)
- CPR certification (or obtained during program)

## Certification Pathway

### Step 1: Complete Program
- Pass all didactic exams
- Complete 50 venipunctures + 10 skin punctures
- Obtain Certificate of Completion

### Step 2: Pass National Certification Exam
Accepted exams:
- **NHA CPT** - National Healthcareer Association
- **ASCP PBT** - American Society for Clinical Pathology
- **NCCT** - National Center for Competency Testing
- **AMT RPT** - American Medical Technologists

### Step 3: Apply to CDPH
Submit to Laboratory Field Services:
- Application for Phlebotomist Certification
- Certificate of Completion from approved program
- Statement of Phlebotomy Practical Training
- Proof of national certification
- Application fee (~$100)

### Step 4: Receive CPT I Certificate
- Valid for 2 years
- Renewal requires 6 CEUs
