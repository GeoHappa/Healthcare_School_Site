## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, graduates will be able to:
1. Perform clinical procedures including vital signs, injections, and specimen collection
2. Assist physicians with examinations and minor procedures
3. Manage administrative tasks including scheduling, billing, and EHR documentation
4. Apply medical terminology in clinical and administrative contexts
5. Demonstrate professional communication with patients and healthcare team
6. Practice infection control and safety protocols
7. Understand medical law, ethics, and HIPAA compliance

## Certification Pathways

### CMA (AAMA) - Certified Medical Assistant
- **Eligibility:** Graduates of CAAHEP or ABHES accredited programs
- **Exam:** 200 questions, 160 minutes
- **Passing Score:** 425/500
- **Renewal:** Every 60 months (60 CEUs)

### RMA (AMT) - Registered Medical Assistant
- **Eligibility:** Graduates of ABHES accredited programs or qualifying experience
- **Exam:** 210 questions
- **Renewal:** Every 3 years (30 CEUs)

### CCMA - California Certified Medical Assistant
- **Eligibility:** California-specific certification
- **Administered by:** California Certifying Board for Medical Assistants (CCBMA)
- **Good for:** Students before national accreditation achieved
