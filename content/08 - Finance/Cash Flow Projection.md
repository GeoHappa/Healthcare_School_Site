# Cash Flow Projection

## Overview

This document projects monthly cash flow for the first 18 months of operation, covering the pre-launch period through the first full year of enrollment. Cash flow projections are critical for ensuring adequate funding and identifying potential shortfalls.

## Contingency Plans

### If Cash Falls Below $20,000

1. **Immediate Actions**
   - [ ] Delay non-essential purchases
   - [ ] Accelerate AR collections
   - [ ] Review upcoming expenses

2. **Short-term Options**
   - [ ] Draw on line of credit
   - [ ] Request payment advances from new enrollments
   - [ ] Negotiate vendor payment extensions

3. **Longer-term Solutions**
   - [ ] Additional owner investment
   - [ ] Reduce operating hours/staffing
   - [ ] Accelerate marketing for next cohort

### If Revenue Shortfall Persists

1. **Reduce expenses**
   - Part-time instructor model
   - Reduce marketing (but risk lower enrollment)
   - Negotiate rent reduction

2. **Increase revenue**
   - Add short programs (CPR, certifications)
   - Evening/weekend cohorts
   - Corporate training contracts
