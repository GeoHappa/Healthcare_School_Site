# Folsom, CA - Market Analysis

## Executive Summary

Folsom represents a **high-opportunity market** for a healthcare training center due to its affluent, educated population, strong healthcare employment sector, and significant gaps in vocational training options within a 30-mile radius.

### Healthcare Workforce Demand

**California Nursing Shortage:**
- 25,168 FTE RN shortage in 2024
- Sacramento region designated as shortage area
- Northern & Sierra regions have most significant shortages

**Medical Assistant Demand (Sacramento):**
- 270 job postings per month
- 10% "unmet demand" - employers can't fill positions
- 15% national growth projected 2023-2033

**Drivers of Demand:**
- Aging population (Baby Boomers)
- Healthcare industry is 20% of Sacramento regional payroll
- 100+ biotech and medical device companies in region

## Target Student Profiles

### Profile 1: Career Changer (Primary Target)
- Age: 25-45
- Currently employed but unfulfilled
- Household income supports tuition payment
- Seeking stable healthcare career
- Values: Job security, helping others, work-life balance

### Profile 2: Recent High School Graduate
- Age: 18-22
- Not pursuing 4-year college
- Parents may help with tuition
- Wants to enter workforce quickly
- Values: Fast track to employment, hands-on learning

### Profile 3: Displaced Tech Worker
- Age: 30-50
- Affected by Intel/tech layoffs
- Has savings or severance
- Seeking career pivot
- Values: Transferable skills, job stability

### Profile 4: Workforce Development Referral
- Age: 20-55
- Unemployed/underemployed
- Eligible for WIOA funding
- May have barriers to employment
- Values: Affordable training, support services

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Affluent market that can afford tuition
- Highly educated population values credentials
- Underserved by existing vocational schools
- Growing healthcare employment sector
- Strong local employers for job placement

### Weaknesses
- Higher commercial real estate costs
- Competition from Sacramento schools (15-20 mi)
- No established brand recognition
- Accreditation takes 18-24 months

### Opportunities
- Intel layoffs creating reskilling demand
- Samsung arrival bringing new workers
- CA nursing shortage driving policy support
- Remote medical coding appeals to tech-savvy residents
- Partnership potential with Mercy, Kaiser, Dignity

### Threats
- Community college expansion (free/low-cost)
- Economic downturn affecting enrollment
- Regulatory changes (BPPE, accreditation)
- Established competitors expanding to area

---

## Recommendations

1. **Launch with Medical Assistant + Phlebotomy** - Zero competition, high demand
2. **Add Medical Coding** - Appeals to displaced tech workers, remote-friendly
3. **Target career changers 25-45** - Can afford tuition, motivated
4. **Partner with Mercy/Kaiser early** - Externship sites + job placement
5. **Plan for ABHES accreditation** - CMA eligibility is major differentiator
