# Medical Assistant Instructor

---

## Position Overview

**Title:** Medical Assistant Program Instructor
**Department:** Academics
**Reports To:** Program Director
**Status:** Full-Time or Part-Time (specify)
**Salary Range:** $55,000 - $70,000 annually (full-time) / $35-50/hour (part-time)

---

## Position Summary

The Medical Assistant Instructor is responsible for delivering comprehensive instruction in the Medical Assistant program, including both didactic (classroom) and clinical laboratory components. This position prepares students for entry-level employment as medical assistants and for national certification examinations.

---

## BPPE Minimum Qualifications

Per California Education Code and BPPE regulations, instructors must meet specific qualification requirements. Medical Assistant instructors must meet ONE of the following credential pathways:

### Credential Pathway Options

**Option A: Certified Medical Assistant**
| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| Certification | Current RMA (AMT), CMA (AAMA), NCMA (NCCT), or CCMA (NHA) |
| Clinical Experience | 3 years as practicing Medical Assistant |
| Education | High school diploma minimum; Associate's preferred |

**Option B: Licensed Healthcare Professional**
| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| License | Current CA RN, LVN, or PA license |
| Clinical Experience | 3 years in ambulatory care/clinical setting |
| Education | Degree required for licensure |

**Option C: Healthcare Educator**
| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| Education | Bachelor's degree in healthcare field |
| Teaching Experience | 2 years teaching healthcare subjects |
| Clinical Experience | 3 years in clinical healthcare setting |

### Additional Requirements (All Options)
- [ ] Current CPR/BLS certification (Healthcare Provider level)
- [ ] No disciplinary actions on professional licenses
- [ ] Able to pass background check
- [ ] TB clearance

## Essential Duties and Responsibilities

### Classroom Instruction (50%)
- Deliver engaging lectures covering all MA curriculum topics
- Facilitate classroom discussions and activities
- Administer and grade examinations
- Maintain accurate attendance and grade records
- Provide timely feedback on student performance
- Create and update lesson plans aligned with course objectives

### Skills Lab Instruction (30%)
- Demonstrate clinical and administrative procedures
- Supervise student practice of clinical skills
- Evaluate student competency in hands-on skills
- Maintain lab equipment and supplies inventory
- Ensure lab safety and infection control compliance
- Document student skill proficiency

### Curriculum Topics
The instructor must be competent to teach all of the following:

**Administrative Skills**
- [ ] Medical office procedures
- [ ] Appointment scheduling
- [ ] Medical records management
- [ ] Insurance and billing basics
- [ ] Electronic health records (EHR)
- [ ] HIPAA compliance

**Clinical Skills**
- [ ] Vital signs measurement
- [ ] Patient intake and history
- [ ] Phlebotomy and specimen collection
- [ ] Injections and medication administration
- [ ] EKG/ECG performance
- [ ] Point-of-care testing
- [ ] Sterile technique
- [ ] Minor surgical assistance
- [ ] Infection control
- [ ] Medical terminology

**Professional Skills**
- [ ] Medical law and ethics
- [ ] Patient communication
- [ ] Professional behavior
- [ ] Emergency procedures

### Student Support (15%)
- Provide academic tutoring and remediation
- Monitor student progress and identify at-risk students
- Communicate concerns to Program Director
- Assist with certification exam preparation
- Support externship readiness

### Administrative Duties (5%)
- Attend faculty meetings
- Participate in curriculum review
- Complete required training
- Maintain professional development
- Document instruction per BPPE requirements

## Sample Interview Questions

### Experience & Credentials
1. Tell me about your medical assisting career and what led you to teaching.
2. What certifications do you currently hold? When do they expire?
3. Describe your clinical experience in ambulatory care settings.

### Teaching Ability
4. How would you teach venipuncture to a student who is nervous?
5. How do you handle students who learn at different paces?
6. What strategies do you use to keep students engaged during lecture?

### Clinical Knowledge
7. Walk me through how you would demonstrate proper vital signs technique.
8. What are the key points you emphasize when teaching injection administration?
9. How do you stay current with changes in medical assisting practice?

### Practical Demonstration
10. Please demonstrate how you would teach a student to take a blood pressure.
11. Show us how you would explain EKG lead placement.

### Situational
12. A student fails a clinical competency check-off. How do you handle remediation?
13. You notice a student struggling with medical terminology. What do you do?
