# EKG Technician Instructor

---

## Position Overview

**Title:** EKG Technician Program Instructor
**Department:** Academics
**Reports To:** Program Director
**Status:** Part-Time (typically)
**Salary Range:** $35-50/hour

---

## Position Summary

The EKG Technician Instructor is responsible for delivering instruction in electrocardiography, including EKG/ECG theory, lead placement, rhythm interpretation, and equipment operation. This position prepares students for entry-level employment as EKG technicians and for national certification examinations such as CET (Certified EKG Technician).

---

## BPPE Minimum Qualifications

Per BPPE regulations, instructors must demonstrate expertise through credentials and experience:

### Required Credentials - Must Meet ONE Pathway

**Option A: Certified EKG/Cardiac Technician**
| Requirement | Minimum | Details |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| Certification | CET (NHA), CCT (NHA), or CRAT | Current, in good standing |
| Experience | 3 years performing EKGs | Verified employment |
| Education | High school diploma | Associate's preferred |

**Option B: Licensed Healthcare Professional**
| Requirement | Minimum | Details |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| License | CA RN, LVN, or Paramedic | Current, in good standing |
| Experience | 3 years in cardiology or performing EKGs | Verified employment |
| EKG Competency | Demonstrated proficiency | Skills verification |

**Option C: Cardiovascular Technologist**
| Requirement | Minimum | Details |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| Credential | RCVT, RCIS, or CCT (CCI) | Current, in good standing |
| Experience | 3 years cardiac diagnostics | Verified employment |

### Preferred Qualifications
| Qualification | Details |
|---------------|---------|
| Multiple Certifications | CET + additional cardiac credentials |
| Teaching Experience | Prior vocational or healthcare instruction |
| Cardiology Experience | Cardiology office, cath lab, or cardiac unit |
| Advanced Rhythm Interpretation | Holter, stress testing, telemetry |
| Medical Assistant Certification | Can also teach MA program EKG component |
| ACLS/BLS Instructor | Current instructor certification |

## Essential Duties and Responsibilities

### Classroom Instruction (50%)
- Deliver lectures on cardiac anatomy and physiology
- Teach EKG theory and electrical conduction
- Instruct rhythm recognition and interpretation
- Explain normal and abnormal findings
- Administer written examinations
- Maintain accurate attendance and grade records

### Curriculum Topics

**Cardiac Anatomy & Physiology**
- [ ] Heart structure and chambers
- [ ] Coronary circulation
- [ ] Cardiac valves
- [ ] Electrical conduction system
- [ ] SA node, AV node, Bundle of His, Purkinje fibers
- [ ] Cardiac cycle

**EKG Fundamentals**
- [ ] History of electrocardiography
- [ ] EKG equipment and supplies
- [ ] Lead systems (3-lead, 5-lead, 12-lead)
- [ ] Lead placement and positioning
- [ ] Einthoven's triangle
- [ ] Augmented and precordial leads
- [ ] Troubleshooting artifacts

**Waveform Analysis**
- [ ] P wave
- [ ] QRS complex
- [ ] T wave
- [ ] U wave
- [ ] PR interval
- [ ] QT interval
- [ ] ST segment
- [ ] Calculating heart rate
- [ ] Determining rhythm regularity
- [ ] Axis determination (basic)

**Rhythm Interpretation**
- [ ] Normal sinus rhythm
- [ ] Sinus bradycardia/tachycardia
- [ ] Sinus arrhythmia
- [ ] Atrial rhythms (PAC, atrial flutter, atrial fibrillation)
- [ ] Junctional rhythms
- [ ] Ventricular rhythms (PVC, V-tach, V-fib)
- [ ] Heart blocks (1st, 2nd, 3rd degree)
- [ ] Bundle branch blocks
- [ ] Pacemaker rhythms

**Clinical Abnormalities**
- [ ] Myocardial infarction patterns
- [ ] Ischemia indicators
- [ ] Hypertrophy patterns
- [ ] Electrolyte imbalances
- [ ] Medication effects

### Skills Lab Instruction (40%)
- Demonstrate proper 12-lead EKG acquisition
- Supervise student practice on classmates (simulated patient)
- Evaluate lead placement accuracy
- Review student-acquired tracings
- Teach artifact recognition and correction
- Demonstrate equipment maintenance
- Practice patient interaction and communication

### Skills Competencies to Teach
| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Patient preparation | Positioning, skin prep, privacy |
| Lead placement (12-lead) | All 10 electrodes correctly positioned |
| Lead placement (3-lead/5-lead) | Telemetry monitoring setup |
| EKG acquisition | Clean, artifact-free tracing |
| Tracing quality check | Identify and correct issues |
| Rhythm strip interpretation | Basic interpretation at bedside |
| Documentation | Proper labeling and mounting |
| Equipment care | Cleaning, maintenance, troubleshooting |

### Administrative Duties (10%)
- Maintain student competency records
- Prepare students for certification exams
- Attend faculty meetings
- Maintain professional credentials
- Order supplies as needed

## Sample Interview Questions

### Credentials & Experience
1. What certifications do you hold related to EKG/cardiac monitoring?
2. Describe your experience performing EKGs. What settings have you worked in?
3. How many EKGs would you estimate you've performed?
4. Have you worked in cardiology, telemetry, or cardiac cath lab?

### Technical Knowledge
5. Walk me through the proper placement of all 12-lead electrodes.
6. What causes artifact on an EKG tracing and how do you correct it?
7. Explain the electrical conduction system of the heart.
8. How do you calculate heart rate from an EKG strip?

### Rhythm Interpretation
9. Look at these 10 rhythm strips and identify each rhythm.
10. What findings on an EKG would suggest acute MI?
11. Differentiate between atrial flutter and atrial fibrillation.
12. What are the different types of heart block?

### Teaching Ability
13. How would you teach a student who keeps getting artifact on their tracings?
14. How do you make rhythm interpretation easy to understand?
15. A student places the V leads incorrectly. How do you address this?

### Practical Demonstration
16. Please demonstrate 12-lead placement on our volunteer.
17. Analyze these 5 tracings and explain your interpretation process.

## Rhythm Interpretation Test for Candidates

Administer a practical rhythm interpretation test:

### Test Format
| Section | Strips | Time | Passing |
|---------|--------|------|---------|
| Basic rhythms | 10 | 10 min | 90% |
| Atrial rhythms | 5 | 5 min | 80% |
| Ventricular rhythms | 5 | 5 min | 80% |
| Heart blocks | 5 | 5 min | 80% |
| Mixed/complex | 5 | 10 min | 80% |
| **Total** | 30 | 35 min | 85% overall |

### Required Rhythm Recognition
Candidate must correctly identify:
- [ ] Normal sinus rhythm
- [ ] Sinus bradycardia
- [ ] Sinus tachycardia
- [ ] Atrial fibrillation
- [ ] Atrial flutter
- [ ] SVT
- [ ] Ventricular tachycardia
- [ ] Ventricular fibrillation
- [ ] PVCs (unifocal, multifocal, couplets)
- [ ] 1st degree AV block
- [ ] 2nd degree AV block (Type I and II)
- [ ] 3rd degree AV block
- [ ] Asystole
- [ ] Pacemaker rhythm
