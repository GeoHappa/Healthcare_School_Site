# Phlebotomy Instructor

---

## Position Overview

**Title:** Phlebotomy Technician Program Instructor
**Department:** Academics
**Reports To:** Program Director
**Status:** Part-Time (typically) or Full-Time
**Salary Range:** $40-55/hour (part-time) / $50,000 - $65,000 annually (full-time)

---

## Position Summary

The Phlebotomy Instructor is responsible for delivering instruction in the Phlebotomy Technician program in compliance with both BPPE regulations and California Department of Public Health - Laboratory Field Services (CDPH-LFS) requirements. This position prepares students for CPT I (Certified Phlebotomy Technician I) certification through the State of California.

---

## Regulatory Overview

**IMPORTANT:** Phlebotomy training in California is regulated by TWO agencies:

| Agency | Jurisdiction | Key Requirements |
|--------|--------------|------------------|
| **BPPE** | School approval | Instructor qualifications, catalog, enrollment |
| **CDPH-LFS** | Program approval | Curriculum content, clinical hours, student draws |

The phlebotomy program must meet requirements from BOTH agencies.

Per BPPE regulations, instructors must demonstrate expertise in their subject area:

## CDPH-LFS Requirements

The California Department of Public Health has specific requirements for phlebotomy training programs:

### Instructor Must Be Qualified To Teach
| Component | Minimum Hours | Content |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| Didactic | 40 hours | Theory, anatomy, procedures |
| Clinical/Practical | 40 hours | Supervised practice |
| **Venipunctures** | n/a | 50 successful draws |
| **Skin Punctures** | n/a | 10 successful draws |

### Required Knowledge Areas
Per Title 17, California Code of Regulations:

- [ ] Anatomy and physiology (circulatory system)
- [ ] Medical terminology
- [ ] Infection control and safety
- [ ] Venipuncture techniques and equipment
- [ ] Skin puncture techniques
- [ ] Specimen collection and handling
- [ ] Order of draw
- [ ] Pre-analytical variables
- [ ] Patient identification
- [ ] Special collections (blood cultures, glucose tolerance)
- [ ] Legal and ethical issues
- [ ] Quality assurance

## Essential Duties and Responsibilities

### Classroom Instruction (40%)
- Deliver didactic instruction covering all CDPH-required topics
- Facilitate interactive learning activities
- Administer written examinations
- Maintain accurate attendance and grade records
- Ensure curriculum meets CDPH minimum requirements

### Skills Lab Instruction (35%)
- Demonstrate proper venipuncture and skin puncture techniques
- Supervise student practice on training arms
- Evaluate student competency progressively
- Maintain lab supplies and equipment
- Ensure proper safety and infection control
- Document all student practice attempts

### Clinical Externship Supervision (15%)
- Coordinate with clinical site supervisors
- Monitor student progress at clinical sites
- Verify student draw counts (50 venipunctures, 10 skin punctures)
- Address any issues at clinical sites
- Complete required clinical documentation
- Conduct site visits as needed

### Administrative Duties (10%)
- Maintain student competency records (critical for certification)
- Prepare students for CDPH certification application
- Submit program reports as required
- Attend faculty meetings
- Participate in curriculum review
- Maintain professional development

## Working Conditions

- Classroom and laboratory environment
- Standing during skills demonstrations
- Exposure to simulated and actual blood collection
- Must be comfortable with blood and needles
- Schedule typically aligns with program hours (40-hour program + clinical)

### Typical Schedule (Example: 4-week program)
| Week | Content | Hours |
|------|---------|-------|
| Week 1 | Didactic + Lab intro | 20 hours |
| Week 2 | Didactic + Lab practice | 20 hours |
| Week 3 | Lab practice + Clinical | 24 hours |
| Week 4 | Clinical completion | 30 hours |

---

## Physical Requirements

- Stand for up to 4 hours continuously
- Demonstrate venipuncture and skin puncture procedures
- Manual dexterity for blood collection
- Visual acuity for vein assessment and procedure observation
- Lift supplies up to 25 pounds

## Sample Interview Questions

### Credentials & Experience
1. When did you obtain your California CPT certification? What level?
2. Describe your phlebotomy experience. Where have you worked?
3. Approximately how many venipunctures have you performed in your career?
4. Have you supervised or trained other phlebotomists?

### Technical Knowledge
5. Walk me through your venipuncture technique step-by-step.
6. What is the correct order of draw and why does it matter?
7. How do you handle a patient who is difficult to draw?
8. Explain the pre-analytical factors that can affect specimen quality.

### Teaching Ability
9. How would you teach a student who is afraid of needles?
10. A student keeps missing veins. How do you help them improve?
11. How do you evaluate when a student is ready for clinical externship?

### Regulatory Knowledge
12. What are the CDPH requirements for phlebotomy certification in California?
13. How many successful draws must students complete before certification?
14. What documentation must be maintained for each student?

### Practical Demonstration
15. Please demonstrate venipuncture on our training arm while explaining your technique.
