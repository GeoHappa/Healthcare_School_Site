# Staffing Plan

## Overview

Building the right team is critical to success. This plan outlines staffing needs from launch through Year 3, with job descriptions, qualifications, and compensation benchmarks.

---

## Organizational Structure

### Launch Phase (Year 1)
```
Owner/Director
├── Program Director (may be owner)
├── Lead Instructor (MA)
├── Part-time Instructor (Coding)
└── Admin/Admissions Coordinator
```

### Growth Phase (Year 2-3)
```
Owner/Director
├── Program Director
│   ├── MA Lead Instructor
│   ├── Coding Lead Instructor
│   ├── Phlebotomy Instructor (PT)
│   └── EKG Instructor (PT)
├── Director of Admissions
│   └── Admissions Representative
├── Career Services Coordinator
└── Administrative Assistant
```

## Position Descriptions

### Program Director

**Role:** Oversee all educational programs, ensure quality and compliance

**Responsibilities:**
- Curriculum development and oversight
- Instructor supervision and evaluation
- Student outcomes tracking
- Accreditation preparation and maintenance
- BPPE compliance
- Clinical site relationship management
- Advisory board coordination

**Qualifications:**
- Bachelor's degree (Master's preferred)
- RN, CMA, or equivalent clinical credential
- 5+ years healthcare experience
- 2+ years teaching or administrative experience
- Knowledge of BPPE and accreditation requirements

**Compensation:** $65,000 - $85,000/year

---

### Lead MA Instructor

**Role:** Deliver Medical Assistant curriculum, lab instruction

**Responsibilities:**
- Teach didactic and lab courses
- Develop and update lesson plans
- Assess student competencies
- Supervise externship placements
- Maintain lab equipment and supplies
- Participate in curriculum review

**Qualifications:**
- CMA (AAMA), RMA (AMT), or equivalent credential
- 3+ years clinical MA experience
- 1+ year teaching experience (or willingness to train)
- Current CPR/BLS certification
- Strong communication skills

**Compensation:** $50,000 - $65,000/year

---

### Medical Coding Instructor

**Role:** Deliver Medical Coding curriculum

**Responsibilities:**
- Teach ICD-10-CM, CPT, HCPCS coding
- Develop practice exercises and assessments
- Prepare students for certification exams
- Maintain current coding knowledge
- Industry networking for job placement

**Qualifications:**
- CPC, CCS, or equivalent credential
- 3+ years coding experience (physician or facility)
- Teaching experience preferred
- Current AAPC or AHIMA membership
- Proficiency with coding software

**Compensation:** $50,000 - $65,000/year (full-time) or $40-$60/hour (part-time)

---

### Phlebotomy Instructor (Part-Time)

**Role:** Deliver Phlebotomy program

**Responsibilities:**
- Teach 80-hour phlebotomy program
- Supervise lab practice sessions
- Coordinate clinical training sites
- Verify student competencies
- Prepare students for certification

**Qualifications:**
- CPT I or CPT II certification
- MLT or MLS preferred
- 3+ years phlebotomy experience
- Teaching experience preferred

**Compensation:** $45-$60/hour (part-time)

---

### Admissions Coordinator

**Role:** Recruit and enroll students

**Responsibilities:**
- Respond to inquiries (phone, email, walk-in)
- Conduct campus tours
- Explain programs and financial options
- Process enrollment paperwork
- Track lead pipeline
- Meet enrollment targets
- Represent school at community events

**Qualifications:**
- Associate degree or equivalent experience
- 2+ years sales or customer service experience
- Education industry experience preferred
- Excellent communication skills
- CRM/database proficiency

**Compensation:** $40,000 - $55,000/year + enrollment bonuses

---

### Career Services Coordinator

**Role:** Support student job placement

**Responsibilities:**
- Build employer relationships
- Post job opportunities
- Conduct resume and interview workshops
- Track placement outcomes
- Coordinate externship sites
- Alumni engagement

**Qualifications:**
- Bachelor's degree preferred
- 2+ years career services or HR experience
- Healthcare industry knowledge helpful
- Strong networking skills

**Compensation:** $45,000 - $55,000/year

---

### Administrative Assistant

**Role:** General office support

**Responsibilities:**
- Answer phones and greet visitors
- Maintain student records
- Process paperwork and filing
- Support instructors with materials
- Order supplies
- General office management

**Qualifications:**
- High school diploma (Associate preferred)
- 2+ years office experience
- Proficiency in MS Office
- Organized and detail-oriented

**Compensation:** $35,000 - $45,000/year

---

### Medical Advisor (Required)

**Role:** Provide medical oversight for clinical programs

**Responsibilities:**
- Review and approve clinical curriculum
- Available for consultation on medical matters
- Sign off on program compliance documents
- Attend advisory board meetings

**Qualifications:**
- Licensed Physician (MD/DO) or Podiatrist
- Active California license
- Familiarity with medical education

**Compensation:** $5,000 - $15,000/year (stipend/consulting)

## Hiring Process

### Sourcing Candidates
1. **Indeed/LinkedIn** - General job boards
2. **AAMA/AAPC Job Boards** - Healthcare-specific
3. **Local colleges** - Adjunct instructor pools
4. **Professional networks** - Referrals from clinical sites
5. **Healthcare facilities** - Experienced clinicians interested in teaching

### Interview Process
1. **Phone screen** (15-20 min)
2. **In-person interview** with Director
3. **Teaching demonstration** (for instructors)
4. **Reference checks** (3 professional references)
5. **Background check** (required for all)
6. **Credential verification** (certifications, licenses)

### Onboarding
- [ ] Complete employment paperwork
- [ ] Review employee handbook
- [ ] BPPE compliance training
- [ ] FERPA training (student privacy)
- [ ] Tour facility and meet team
- [ ] Review curriculum and expectations
- [ ] Shadow existing instructors (if available)

## Performance Management

### Instructor Evaluation Criteria
- Student outcomes (completion, certification pass rates)
- Student evaluations
- Curriculum adherence
- Professional development
- Attendance and reliability
- Collaboration with team

### Administrative Evaluation Criteria
- Enrollment targets (Admissions)
- Placement rates (Career Services)
- Compliance and accuracy (Admin)
- Student/employer satisfaction
- Process improvement

### Review Schedule
- 90-day review (new hires)
- Annual performance review
- Ongoing feedback and coaching

## Employee Benefits

### Benefits Package (Target)
- Health insurance (employer pays 50-75%)
- Dental and vision (optional)
- 401(k) with match (after Year 2)
- Paid time off (2 weeks starting)
- Sick leave (per CA law)
- Professional development allowance
- Tuition discount for family members

### Not Offered Initially
- Pension
- Life insurance
- Long-term disability
(Can add as school grows and stabilizes)
