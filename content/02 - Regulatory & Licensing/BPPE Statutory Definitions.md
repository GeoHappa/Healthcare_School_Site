# BPPE Statutory Definitions

> **Reference Guide:** Complete glossary of legal definitions from the California Private Postsecondary Education Act of 2009 (CEC §§94800-94950) and implementing regulations (5 CCR §§70000-76245).

---

## Overview

Understanding these statutory definitions is essential for BPPE compliance. These terms have specific legal meanings that differ from common usage and are referenced throughout the California Education Code and Code of Regulations.

---

## Core Institutional Definitions

### Academic Year
**Citation:** CEC §94812

An academic year consists of:
- Minimum **30 weeks** of instructional time
- **24 semester units** or **36 quarter units**, OR
- **900 clock hours** of instruction

This definition affects financial aid eligibility, program length calculations, and reporting requirements.

---

### Accredited Institution
**Citation:** CEC §94813

An institution that holds current accreditation from an accrediting agency recognized by the U.S. Department of Education (USDE). Accreditation must be:
- Institutional (covers entire institution), not just programmatic
- Current and in good standing
- From a USDE-recognized agency

**Recognized Institutional Accreditors:**
| Regional | National |
|----------|----------|
| WASC Senior College (WSCUC) | ACCSC |
| Higher Learning Commission (HLC) | ACCET |
| Middle States (MSCHE) | DEAC |
| New England (NECHE) | COE |
| Northwest (NWCCU) | ABHES |
| Southern Association (SACSCOC) | TRACS |
| ACCJC (Community Colleges) | |

---

### Accrediting Agency
**Citation:** CEC §94814

An agency recognized by the U.S. Department of Education to evaluate and accredit postsecondary institutions or programs. Recognition must be current—agencies that lose USDE recognition no longer qualify.

---

### Approval to Operate
**Citation:** CEC §94817

The authorization issued by BPPE that permits an institution to offer postsecondary educational programs in California. This is the fundamental requirement for any private postsecondary institution.

**Key Points:**
- Required BEFORE enrolling students or advertising programs
- Valid for 5 years
- Two pathways: Accredited ($750) and Non-Accredited ($5,000)
- Non-accredited degree programs receive provisional approval only

---

### Institution / Private Postsecondary Educational Institution
**Citation:** CEC §94858, §94843

A private entity that:
1. Has **physical presence** in California, AND
2. Offers postsecondary education to the public **for a charge**

This includes both for-profit and nonprofit entities but excludes public institutions, certain religious institutions, and exempt organizations.

---

### To Operate
**Citation:** CEC §94869

To "operate" means either:
- Maintaining a place of business or physical facility in California for instruction, OR
- Enrolling California residents via distance education

Both activities require BPPE approval even without a physical campus.

---

### Physical Presence
**Citation:** CEC §94801.7

Activities that establish physical presence in California:
- Operating a place of business or facility
- Employing California residents for instruction
- Conducting in-person instruction anywhere in the state
- Maintaining administrative offices
- Offering clinical rotations or externships at California sites

**Note:** Out-of-state institutions offering only distance education to California residents may register rather than obtain full approval.

---

## Educational Program Definitions

### Educational Program
**Citation:** CEC §94837

A planned sequence of courses or subjects leading to:
- A degree, diploma, or certificate, OR
- Preparation for an occupation or profession

**Exclusions:**
- Single courses of 32 hours or less are NOT educational programs
- Avocational courses are exempt

---

### Degree
**Citation:** CEC §94830

A credential at or above the associate's level:
- Associate's degree
- Bachelor's degree
- Master's degree
- Doctoral degree (including professional doctorates)

Degree programs have additional requirements including mandatory accreditation plans for non-accredited institutions.

---

### Diploma / Certificate
**Citation:** CEC §94832

A credential below the associate's degree level, awarded upon completion of a vocational or professional program. Most healthcare training programs (MA, Phlebotomy, Coding, EKG) fall into this category.

---

### Distance Education
**Citation:** CEC §94834

Education in which:
- Instruction is transmitted to a location separate from the institution
- The instructor and student are in different physical locations
- Interaction may occur synchronously or asynchronously

**Includes:** Online courses, correspondence courses, hybrid programs

**Special Requirements:**
- Distance education enrollment agreements must include specific cancellation disclosures
- Students have additional cancellation rights before first lesson transmission
- Institutions must demonstrate capacity for student interaction and assessment

---

### Avocational Education
**Citation:** CEC §94818

Education intended for personal entertainment, pleasure, or enjoyment purposes rather than vocational preparation. Avocational programs are **exempt** from BPPE oversight.

**Examples of Avocational:**
- Recreational art classes
- Personal fitness instruction
- Hobby courses

**NOT Avocational:**
- Programs leading to employment
- Programs with career services claims
- Programs with placement rate disclosures

---

### Continuing Education
**Citation:** CEC §94827

Instruction designed for persons who have completed an educational program and need additional training to:
- Maintain professional licensure, OR
- Update skills in their profession

Continuing education that does NOT lead to a degree may be exempt from BPPE oversight under CEC §94874(d)(2).

---

## Ownership and Control Definitions

### Change in Control
**Citation:** CEC §94822

Occurs when any person acquires **25% or more** ownership or control interest in an institution. Triggers substantive change requirements and BPPE notification/approval.

**"Substantial control" definition:** Authority to direct or cause the institution to expend $5,000 or more in any calendar year.

---

### Change in Ownership
**Citation:** CEC §94823

Occurs when any person acquires more than **50%** interest in an institution. This is a major substantive change requiring:
- Prior BPPE approval
- New ownership disclosure
- Potential new application requirements

---

### Owner
**Citation:** 5 CCR §71130

Any person who:
- Owns 25% or more of the institution, OR
- Exercises substantial control over the institution

All owners must be disclosed with:
- Full legal name
- Physical home address
- Social Security Number or Federal EIN
- Percentage of ownership
- Nature of interest

---

## Student-Related Definitions

### California Resident (for STRF purposes)
**Citation:** 5 CCR §76000

For Student Tuition Recovery Fund eligibility, a California resident is a person who:
- Resided in California at the time of enrollment, OR
- Enrolled in a residency program while in California, OR
- Received lessons at an address in California

---

### Institutional Charges
**Citation:** CEC §94844

All charges paid directly to the institution for an educational program, including:
- Tuition
- Registration fees
- Equipment fees
- Lab fees and supplies
- Textbooks (if purchased through institution)
- STRF assessments
- Any other charges collected by the institution

**Excludes:** Third-party charges (transportation, childcare, living expenses)

---

### Full-Time Study
**Citation:** 5 CCR §70000(n)

Academic workload consisting of:
- 24 semester units in 30 weeks, OR
- 36 quarter units in 30 weeks, OR
- 900 clock hours in 30 weeks

---

## Approval-Related Definitions

### Provisional Approval
**Citation:** 5 CCR §70000(y); CEC §94885.5

Authorization granted to non-accredited institutions seeking to offer degree programs. Provisional approval:
- Lasts up to **5 years**
- Limits institution to **2 degree programs** maximum
- Requires an accreditation plan with milestone deadlines
- Requires specific student disclosures about provisional status

**Failure to meet accreditation deadlines results in automatic suspension.**

---

### Duly Qualified Faculty
**Citation:** 5 CCR §70000(j); §71720

Faculty meeting BPPE qualification standards:

**For Degree Programs:**
- Terminal degree in field, OR
- Master's degree in field plus professional experience

**For Non-Degree Programs (Certificate/Diploma):**
- 3 years experience in subject area, OR
- Relevant professional credential/license, OR
- Combination of education and experience demonstrating competency

---

### Qualifying Institution (for STRF)
**Citation:** 5 CCR §76000(g)

An institution that:
- Holds approval under CEC §94886 (non-accredited), OR
- Holds approval under CEC §94890 (accredited), OR
- Holds out-of-state registration under CEC §94801.5

All qualifying institutions must collect STRF assessments (when rate > $0) and submit quarterly reports.

---

## Closure-Related Definitions

### Teach-Out
**Citation:** CEC §94866

Arrangements made for students to complete their educational programs when an institution or program closes. Teach-out plans may include:
- Completion at the closing institution before closure date
- Transfer agreements with other approved institutions
- Refund of all charges if no teach-out is available

---

### Out-of-State Institution
**Citation:** CEC §94850.5

A private entity that:
- Is headquartered outside California
- Has no physical presence in California
- Offers distance education to California residents

Out-of-state institutions must register with BPPE ($1,500 for 5 years) unless exempt.

---

## Nonprofit Definitions

### Nonprofit Corporation
**Citation:** CEC §94850.2

An institution organized under IRC §501(c)(3) with tax-deductible contribution status. Nonprofit status affects:
- Ownership disclosure requirements
- Governing board requirements
- Declaration signature requirements
