## Purpose

This policy establishes standards of conduct for all students to maintain a professional, safe, and respectful learning environment.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all students while:
- On school premises
- Participating in school-related activities
- At externship/clinical sites
- Representing the school in any capacity

---

## Standards of Conduct

### Professional Behavior

As future healthcare professionals, students are expected to:
- Treat all persons with respect and dignity
- Communicate in a professional manner
- Maintain honesty and integrity
- Respect diverse backgrounds and perspectives
- Demonstrate reliability and accountability
- Represent the school positively

### Classroom Expectations

Students must:
- Arrive on time and prepared for class
- Participate actively in learning activities
- Follow instructor directions
- Complete assignments by due dates
- Maintain attention during instruction
- Respect learning environment of others

### Laboratory Expectations

Students must:
- Follow all safety protocols
- Handle equipment properly
- Maintain cleanliness and organization
- Report safety concerns immediately
- Practice skills responsibly
- Treat simulation equipment with care

### Clinical/Externship Expectations

Students must:
- Follow all site policies and procedures
- Maintain patient confidentiality (HIPAA)
- Arrive in proper uniform/attire
- Demonstrate professional demeanor
- Accept supervision and feedback
- Report incidents immediately

---

## Dress Code

### Classroom Attire

- Scrubs or professional attire
- Closed-toe, closed-heel shoes
- School ID badge (when provided)
- Clean, neat appearance
- Hair secured away from face

### Clinical/Externship Attire

- School-designated scrubs (clean, pressed)
- White or black leather shoes
- School ID badge displayed
- Watch with second hand
- Minimal jewelry (stud earrings only)
- No artificial nails
- No visible tattoos (per site policy)
- Natural hair colors

### Prohibited Items

- Open-toe or open-heel shoes
- Shorts or revealing clothing
- Hats (except religious head coverings)
- Strong perfume or cologne
- Excessive jewelry
- Visible body piercings (except ears)

---

## Electronic Device Policy

### Cell Phones and Personal Devices

- Must be silenced during class
- No calls or texting during instruction
- May be used during scheduled breaks
- Recording requires written permission
- No devices during examinations

### Computer/Internet Use

- School computers for educational purposes only
- No unauthorized software installation
- No accessing inappropriate content
- Respect network usage policies
- Log off after each session

### Social Media

- No posting of school/clinical information
- No photos of patients, students, or staff without consent
- Do not represent personal views as school positions
- Inappropriate posts may result in discipline

---

## Prohibited Conduct

### Major Violations

The following may result in immediate dismissal:

1. **Violence/Threats**
   - Physical assault or battery
   - Threatening behavior
   - Intimidation or bullying
   - Fighting

2. **Weapons**
   - Possession of weapons on school property
   - Includes firearms, knives, other weapons

3. **Substance Abuse**
   - Possession or use of illegal drugs
   - Being under the influence at school
   - Possession or use of alcohol on premises
   - Misuse of prescription medications

4. **Harassment/Discrimination**
   - Sexual harassment
   - Discrimination based on protected characteristics
   - Creating hostile environment
   - Retaliation against complainants

5. **HIPAA Violations**
   - Breaching patient confidentiality
   - Unauthorized access to records
   - Sharing protected health information

6. **Academic Dishonesty**
   - Cheating on examinations
   - Plagiarism
   - Falsifying records or documents
   - Forging signatures

7. **Property Violations**
   - Theft
   - Vandalism
   - Destruction of property
   - Unauthorized use of resources

8. **Safety Violations**
   - Reckless behavior endangering others
   - Disabling safety equipment
   - Creating hazardous conditions

### Minor Violations

The following may result in progressive discipline:

- Disruptive behavior in class
- Repeated tardiness
- Dress code violations
- Cell phone use during class
- Failure to follow instructions
- Disrespectful language
- Sleeping in class
- Eating/drinking in prohibited areas

## Disciplinary Process

### Progressive Discipline

| Step | Action | Documentation |
|------|--------|---------------|
| 1 | Verbal Warning | Documented in file |
| 2 | Written Warning | Signed acknowledgment |
| 3 | Probation | Formal probation letter |
| 4 | Suspension | Written notification |
| 5 | Dismissal | Formal dismissal letter |

*Note: Severe violations may skip steps*

### Investigation Process

1. Incident reported to School Director
2. Preliminary review within 24 hours
3. Investigation conducted (interviews, evidence)
4. Student informed of allegations
5. Student given opportunity to respond
6. Decision made within 10 business days
7. Written notification of outcome

### Student Rights During Process

- Presumed not responsible until determined
- Informed of allegations in writing
- Opportunity to present information
- May have advisor present (non-participating)
- Receive written decision with rationale
- Right to appeal

## Sanctions

### Available Sanctions

| Sanction | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Warning | Formal notice of policy violation |
| Probation | Period of monitoring; restrictions may apply |
| Restitution | Payment for damages |
| Service | Community service assignment |
| Suspension | Temporary removal from school |
| Dismissal | Permanent separation from school |

### Factors Considered

- Severity of violation
- Prior disciplinary history
- Student's acceptance of responsibility
- Impact on community
- Potential for rehabilitation

---

## Appeals

### Grounds for Appeal

Students may appeal disciplinary decisions based on:
- Procedural error affecting outcome
- New evidence not previously available
- Sanction disproportionate to violation

### Appeal Process

1. Submit written appeal within 5 business days
2. Appeal reviewed by School Director (or designee if Director made original decision)
3. Decision within 10 business days
4. Appeal decision is final

---

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report

Students, staff, or faculty may report violations by:
- Speaking with any staff member
- Contacting the School Director
- Submitting written report
- Email: [EMAIL ADDRESS]

### Confidentiality

- Reports handled confidentially
- Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- Anonymous reports accepted but may limit investigation

### Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone reporting violations or participating in investigations is prohibited and will result in disciplinary action.

- Documented in student file
- Maintained confidentially
- Retained per BPPE requirements
