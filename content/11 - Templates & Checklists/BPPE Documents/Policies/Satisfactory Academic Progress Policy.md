## Purpose

This policy defines the standards students must maintain to make Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) and remain enrolled in their program.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all students enrolled in all programs at [SCHOOL NAME].

---

## SAP Standards

### Qualitative Standard (Grade)

Students must maintain a **cumulative grade point average (CGPA) of 2.0** or higher.

### Quantitative Standard (Pace)

Students must complete a minimum of **67% of attempted clock/credit hours**.

**Pace Calculation:**
Pace = (Cumulative Hours Completed รท Cumulative Hours Attempted) ร 100

### Maximum Time Frame

Students must complete their program within **150%** of the published program length.

| Program | Normal Length | Maximum Time |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| Medical Assistant | 800 hours | 1,200 hours |
| Phlebotomy | 94 hours | 141 hours |
| Medical Coding | 460 hours | 690 hours |
| EKG Technician | 50 hours | 75 hours |

## SAP Evaluation Points

Academic progress is formally evaluated at:

| Evaluation Point | Checkpoint |
|------------------|------------|
| First Evaluation | 25% of program completion |
| Second Evaluation | 50% of program completion |
| Third Evaluation | 75% of program completion |
| Final Evaluation | Program completion |

Students are notified in writing of their SAP status after each evaluation.

---

## SAP Status Categories

### Good Standing

Students meeting all SAP standards:
- CGPA of 2.0 or higher
- Pace of 67% or higher
- On track to complete within 150% maximum time

### Academic Warning

Students failing to meet SAP standards at first evaluation:
- Notified in writing
- Meet with Program Director
- Develop Academic Improvement Plan
- One evaluation period to meet standards
- May continue enrollment during warning

### Academic Probation

Students failing to meet SAP standards after warning period:
- Notified in writing
- Enhanced monitoring (weekly progress reviews)
- Mandatory tutoring/support services
- Must meet standards by next evaluation
- May continue enrollment with conditions

### Academic Suspension

Students failing to meet SAP standards after probation:
- Enrollment suspended
- May not attend classes
- May appeal (see Appeals section)
- If reinstated, placed on probation

### Academic Dismissal

Students who:
- Fail to meet SAP after appeal is denied
- Fail same course twice
- Cannot mathematically complete within 150%
- Violate terms of probation

---

## SAP Notification Process

When a student fails to meet SAP:

1. **Written Notice** (within 5 business days of evaluation)
   - Current GPA and pace
   - SAP requirements
   - Status determination
   - Next steps

2. **Required Meeting** (within 3 business days of notice)
   - Review academic performance
   - Identify barriers to success
   - Develop improvement plan

3. **Academic Improvement Plan**
   - Specific, measurable goals
   - Timeline for improvement
   - Required support activities
   - Follow-up schedule

## Course Repetitions

### Impact on SAP

- Repeated course hours count toward maximum timeframe
- Only most recent grade counts toward GPA
- Both attempts count toward pace calculation

### Repetition Limits

- Maximum one repeat per failed course
- Second failure = dismissal from program
- Additional fees may apply for repeats

---

## Incompletes

### Impact on SAP

- Hours count as attempted but not completed
- No quality points until grade assigned
- Must be resolved within 2 weeks

### Failure to Complete

- Grade converts to F
- Impacts both GPA and pace

---

## Withdrawals

### Impact on SAP

- Hours count as attempted
- Hours do not count as completed
- No quality points assigned
- Negatively impacts pace calculation

---

## Transfer Credits

### Impact on SAP

- Count as hours completed
- Count toward program length
- Do not count toward GPA
- May reduce maximum time frame proportionally

---

## Appeals Process

### Grounds for Appeal

Students may appeal SAP determinations based on:
- Death of immediate family member
- Serious illness or injury (self or immediate family)
- Other significant extenuating circumstances

### Appeal Requirements

1. **Written Appeal** (within 5 business days)
   - Explain circumstances
   - Describe what has changed
   - Provide plan for success

2. **Documentation**
   - Medical records
   - Death certificate
   - Court documents
   - Other supporting evidence

3. **Academic Plan**
   - Specific improvement steps
   - Timeline for meeting SAP
   - Commitment to requirements

### Appeal Review

- Appeals reviewed by Appeals Committee
- Decision within 10 business days
- Student notified in writing
- Decision is final

### If Appeal Approved

- Student placed on Academic Probation
- Must follow approved Academic Plan
- Weekly progress monitoring
- Must meet SAP by next evaluation

### If Appeal Denied

- Student is dismissed from program
- May apply for reinstatement after 6 months
- Reinstatement not guaranteed

---

## Reinstatement

### Eligibility

Dismissed students may apply for reinstatement:
- After minimum 6-month waiting period
- If circumstances have changed
- If able to demonstrate readiness

### Reinstatement Process

1. Submit written reinstatement request
2. Meet with Program Director
3. Provide documentation of changed circumstances
4. Complete reinstatement interview
5. Approval at discretion of School Director

### Conditions of Reinstatement

- Placed on Academic Probation
- Must follow Academic Improvement Plan
- May have to repeat failed courses
- Subject to immediate dismissal if SAP not met
