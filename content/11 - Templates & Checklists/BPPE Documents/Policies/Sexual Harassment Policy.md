# Sexual Harassment Policy

---

## Policy Statement

[SCHOOL NAME] is committed to providing an educational environment free from sexual harassment. Sexual harassment of any kind is prohibited and will not be tolerated.

This policy applies to:
- All students
- Faculty and staff
- Clinical site interactions
- School-related activities

---

## Definition of Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment includes unwelcome conduct of a sexual nature that:
- Is made as a condition of education or employment
- Is used as a basis for academic or employment decisions
- Creates a hostile, intimidating, or offensive environment
- Interferes with academic performance or participation

---

## Types of Sexual Harassment

### Quid Pro Quo

When submission to sexual conduct is:
- Explicitly or implicitly a condition of academic success
- Used as basis for decisions affecting the student
- Required for grades, recommendations, or opportunities

### Hostile Environment

When conduct:
- Is severe or pervasive
- Unreasonably interferes with education
- Creates intimidating or offensive environment
- Would be offensive to a reasonable person

---

## Examples of Prohibited Conduct

### Verbal

- Sexual comments, jokes, or innuendos
- Requests for sexual favors
- Comments about someone's body
- Sexual rumors or gossip
- Repeated unwelcome requests for dates
- Threats related to sexual conduct

### Non-Verbal

- Sexual gestures
- Displaying sexual images
- Sending sexual messages or images
- Leering or staring
- Following or stalking

### Physical

- Unwelcome touching
- Blocking someone's path
- Sexual assault
- Any unwanted physical contact of sexual nature

### Electronic/Digital

- Sending sexual texts or emails
- Sharing sexual images
- Online harassment
- Cyberstalking
- Posting sexual content about someone

---

## Consent

### What is Consent

Consent is:
- Freely given
- Reversible (can be withdrawn)
- Informed
- Enthusiastic
- Specific

### What is NOT Consent

- Silence or lack of resistance
- Previous consent
- Consent to one activity (doesn't apply to others)
- Consent given under influence of drugs/alcohol
- Consent given under pressure or coercion

## Confidentiality

### Privacy Protection

- Reports handled confidentially to extent possible
- Information shared only with those who need to know
- Investigation conducted discreetly
- Records maintained securely

### Limits on Confidentiality

School may need to take action that limits full confidentiality:
- To protect the community
- To comply with legal obligations
- To conduct an adequate investigation

### Anonymous Reports

- Anonymous reports accepted
- Harder to investigate fully
- May limit response options
- Still reviewed and acted upon when possible

---

## Investigation Process

### Step 1: Initial Report
- Report received and documented
- Preliminary assessment conducted
- Interim measures considered

### Step 2: Formal Investigation
- Both parties notified
- Interviews conducted
- Evidence gathered
- Witnesses interviewed

### Step 3: Determination
- Findings made based on preponderance of evidence
- Both parties notified of outcome
- Sanctions determined if violation found

### Step 4: Appeal (if applicable)
- Either party may appeal
- Grounds for appeal specified
- Appeal reviewed by designated official

### Timeline

- Investigation typically completed within 60 days
- Complex cases may take longer
- Parties informed of delays

---

## Interim Measures

During investigation, measures may include:
- No-contact orders
- Schedule changes
- Escort services
- Housing changes (if applicable)
- Academic accommodations
- Clinical site reassignment

Available regardless of whether formal complaint is filed.

---

## Sanctions

### For Students

If found responsible:
- Warning
- Probation
- Suspension
- Dismissal from program
- Transcript notation
- No-contact order

### For Employees

Handled per employment policies:
- Corrective action
- Suspension
- Termination

### Factors Considered

- Severity of conduct
- Pattern of behavior
- Impact on complainant
- Response and remediation efforts

---

## Retaliation Prohibited

### What is Retaliation

Any adverse action against someone for:
- Making a report
- Participating in investigation
- Supporting a complainant
- Opposing harassment

### Examples

- Academic punishment
- Threats or intimidation
- Exclusion from activities
- Spreading rumors
- Negative treatment

### Reporting Retaliation

Report retaliation immediately to Title IX Coordinator.
