# Confidentiality Policy

---

## Overview

[SCHOOL NAME] is committed to protecting confidential information, including student records, patient information encountered during clinical training, and proprietary school information.

---

## Types of Confidential Information

### Student Information (FERPA)

Protected student information includes:
- Academic records and grades
- Financial information
- Personal identifiers (SSN, DOB)
- Health records
- Disciplinary records
- Enrollment status

See [[FERPA Policy]] for complete details.

### Patient Information (HIPAA)

During clinical training, students encounter Protected Health Information (PHI):
- Patient names
- Medical record numbers
- Diagnoses and treatments
- Dates of service
- Contact information
- Any identifying health information

### School Proprietary Information

Confidential school information includes:
- Exam content
- Curriculum materials
- Business information
- Personnel matters

## FERPA Compliance

### Student Privacy

[SCHOOL NAME] protects student records under FERPA:
- Records not disclosed without consent
- Exceptions defined by law
- Students control their information
- Directory information opt-out available

### Staff Obligations

School employees must:
- Access records only when needed
- Not share information inappropriately
- Follow proper disclosure procedures
- Report any breaches

---

## HIPAA Compliance

### Student Responsibilities

When in clinical settings, students must:
- Protect all patient information
- Not discuss patients outside clinical need
- Not photograph patients or records
- Not access records without authorization
- Not share patient stories (even without names)
- Follow site confidentiality policies

### What Constitutes a Violation

| Violation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Inappropriate access | Looking at records of non-assigned patients |
| Unauthorized disclosure | Telling family about patients |
| Social media | Posting about clinical experiences with identifiable info |
| Casual conversation | Discussing patients in public areas |
| Photography | Taking pictures at clinical site |
| Removing information | Taking copies of records |

### Consequences of HIPAA Violations

| Level | Consequence |
|-------|-------------|
| Minor inadvertent | Warning and education |
| Negligent | Removal from site, program probation |
| Deliberate | Dismissal from program |
| Criminal | Potential legal penalties |

## Social Media Policy

### Prohibited Postings

Never post:
- Patient information (even without names)
- Photos from clinical sites
- Complaints about patients
- Identifiable clinical details
- School exam content
- Other students' information

### What's Acceptable

- General statements about your program
- Career information (without specifics)
- School achievements (general)
- Non-confidential networking

### Remember

- "De-identified" information may still be identifiable
- Screenshots spread quickly
- Employers check social media
- Violations have serious consequences

## Confidentiality Agreement

### Student Agreement

All students sign a confidentiality agreement covering:
- Student record confidentiality
- Patient information protection
- Exam security
- Proprietary materials
- Social media restrictions

### Agreement Timing

Signed:
- At enrollment (general confidentiality)
- Before externship (clinical confidentiality)

### Continued Obligation

Confidentiality obligations:
- Continue after leaving the school
- Continue after graduation
- Are legally binding
- Apply indefinitely for patient information

## Reporting Breaches

### If You Witness a Breach

Report immediately to:
- Clinical Coordinator (if at clinical site)
- Program Director
- Instructor
- Site supervisor

### What to Report

- Unauthorized access to records
- Inappropriate discussions
- Social media violations
- Lost or stolen information
- Any suspected breach

### Protection from Retaliation

Students who report in good faith are protected from retaliation.

## Consequences of Violations

### Academic Consequences

| Severity | Consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| Minor first offense | Warning, education |
| Moderate offense | Probation, clinical removal |
| Serious offense | Dismissal from program |
| HIPAA violation | Immediate clinical removal, possible dismissal |

### Professional Consequences

Violations may affect:
- References from school and sites
- Certification eligibility
- Future employment
- Professional reputation

### Legal Consequences

Serious violations may result in:
- Civil penalties under HIPAA
- Criminal prosecution
- Lawsuits

## Protecting Information

### Physical Security

- Don't leave records unattended
- Store documents securely
- Shred sensitive documents
- Return borrowed materials

### Electronic Security

- Use strong passwords
- Lock computers when away
- Don't share login credentials
- Log out after use
- Be aware of screen visibility

### Verbal Security

- Don't discuss confidential matters in public
- Be aware of who can hear
- Use private spaces for sensitive discussions
- Don't share information unnecessarily
