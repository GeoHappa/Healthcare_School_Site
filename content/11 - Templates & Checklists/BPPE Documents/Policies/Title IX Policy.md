# Title IX Policy

---

## Overview

Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972 is a federal law that prohibits discrimination on the basis of sex in any education program or activity receiving federal financial assistance.

---

## Policy Statement

[SCHOOL NAME] does not discriminate on the basis of sex in its education programs and activities. This includes:
- Admissions
- Employment
- Academic programs
- Student services
- Athletics (if applicable)
- All school-related activities

## What Title IX Covers

### Protected from Discrimination

Title IX protects against discrimination based on:

- Prospective students
- Employees

## Formal Complaint Process

### Filing a Formal Complaint

To initiate investigation:
- Submit written complaint to Title IX Coordinator
- Describe the alleged conduct
- Request investigation

### Investigation Process

1. **Notice:** Both parties notified
2. **Investigation:** Interviews, evidence gathering
3. **Review:** Parties can review evidence
4. **Hearing:** Live hearing with questioning
5. **Determination:** Written decision
6. **Appeal:** Available to both parties

### Standard of Evidence

Decisions made by **preponderance of the evidence** (more likely than not).

### Timeline

- Initial response: 3-5 business days
- Investigation: 30-60 days (typical)
- Decision: Within 7 days of hearing
- Appeal: 10 days to file

## Emergency Removal

A respondent may be removed on emergency basis if:
- Immediate threat to health or safety
- Individualized safety analysis conducted
- Respondent given notice and opportunity to respond

## Rights of Parties

### Both Parties Have Right To:

- Be treated equitably
- Have presumption of non-responsibility (respondent)
- Have advisor of choice (including attorney)
- Review evidence
- Be notified of outcome
- Appeal the decision

### Additional Rights

- Timely resolution
- Privacy to extent possible
- No contact with other party
- Updates on process status

## Appeals

### Grounds for Appeal

Either party may appeal based on:
- Procedural irregularity affecting outcome
- New evidence not reasonably available
- Title IX official had conflict of interest

### Appeal Process

1. Submit written appeal within 10 days
2. Other party notified
3. Both parties may submit statements
4. Appeal officer reviews
5. Written decision issued

## Pregnancy and Parenting

### Protected Activities

Title IX protects pregnant and parenting students:
- Cannot be excluded from programs
- Entitled to reasonable accommodations
- Excused absences for medical appointments
- Same treatment as other medical conditions

### Accommodations May Include

- Larger desk or seating
- Breaks during class
- Elevator access
- Modified physical activities
- Extended time for assignments
- Make-up opportunities

### How to Request

Contact Title IX Coordinator or Student Services to discuss accommodations.
