# Non-Discrimination Policy

---

## Policy Statement

[SCHOOL NAME] is committed to providing an educational environment free from discrimination. We do not discriminate on the basis of any protected characteristic in admissions, education, employment, or any school-related activity.

---

## Protected Characteristics

[SCHOOL NAME] prohibits discrimination based on:

- Race
- Color
- National origin
- Ancestry
- Ethnicity
- Religion
- Creed
- Age
- Sex
- Gender
- Gender identity
- Gender expression
- Sexual orientation
- Marital status
- Pregnancy
- Childbirth or related conditions
- Physical disability
- Mental disability
- Medical condition
- Genetic information
- Military or veteran status
- Citizenship status (within legal limits)
- Any other characteristic protected by law

## What Constitutes Discrimination

### Discriminatory Actions Include

- Denying admission based on protected characteristic
- Different treatment in academic matters
- Harassment based on protected status
- Creating hostile environment
- Retaliation for reporting discrimination
- Failing to provide reasonable accommodations

### Examples

| Area | Discriminatory Example |
|------|------------------------|
| Admissions | Rejecting applicant due to religion |
| Classroom | Making derogatory comments about ethnicity |
| Grading | Lower grades based on gender |
| Clinical | Refusing placement due to disability |
| Services | Denying access due to sexual orientation |

## Reporting Discrimination

### Where to Report

| Issue Type | Primary Contact |
|------------|-----------------|
| General discrimination | School Director |
| Sexual harassment | Title IX Coordinator |
| Disability discrimination | ADA Coordinator |
| Student concerns | Student Services |
| Employment concerns | School Director |

### How to Report

Reports may be made:
- In person
- By phone
- By email
- In writing
- Through grievance procedure

### What to Include

- Description of the incident
- Names of those involved
- Dates, times, locations
- Witnesses (if any)
- Impact on you
- Desired resolution

---

## Investigation Process

### Steps

1. **Report received** - Documented and acknowledged
2. **Initial assessment** - Determine scope and process
3. **Investigation** - Interviews and evidence gathering
4. **Determination** - Finding made based on evidence
5. **Action** - Corrective action if violation found
6. **Appeal** - Available to parties

### Timeline

- Acknowledgment: Within 2 business days
- Investigation: Typically 30-60 days
- Decision: Within 10 days of investigation completion

---

## Corrective Actions

### If Discrimination Found

Actions may include:
- Education and training
- Warning or reprimand
- Change in policies or practices
- Disciplinary action
- Termination of employment/enrollment

### Remedies for Affected Individuals

May include:
- Academic adjustments
- Schedule changes
- Retroactive grade changes
- Other appropriate relief

---

## Retaliation Prohibited

### What is Retaliation

Adverse action against someone for:
- Reporting discrimination
- Participating in investigation
- Supporting a complainant
- Opposing discriminatory practices

### Examples of Retaliation

- Academic punishment
- Hostile treatment
- Exclusion from activities
- Negative references
- Threats or intimidation

### Reporting Retaliation

Report retaliation immediately - it is a separate violation.
