## Purpose

This procedure provides a fair and efficient process for students to address concerns, complaints, and grievances related to their educational experience.

---

## Scope

This procedure applies to all currently enrolled students and covers complaints related to:
- Academic matters
- Administrative decisions
- Policies and procedures
- Treatment by staff or faculty
- School services
- Learning environment

---

## Definitions

### Grievance
A formal complaint about a school decision, policy, procedure, or treatment that the student believes is unfair, improper, or in violation of established rules.

### Complainant
The student filing the grievance.

### Respondent
The individual(s) or department against whom the grievance is filed.

### Grievance Officer
The school official designated to coordinate the grievance process (typically the School Director or designee).

---

## Informal Resolution (Step 1)

### Process

Many concerns can be resolved informally through direct communication.

1. **Identify the Issue**
   - Clarify your concern
   - Gather relevant information/documentation

2. **Speak with the Appropriate Person**
   - Academic concerns → Instructor or Program Director
   - Administrative concerns → Student Services
   - Financial concerns → Business Office

3. **Attempt Resolution**
   - Discuss the issue calmly and professionally
   - Listen to the other perspective
   - Seek a mutually acceptable solution

### Timeline
- Should be initiated within 10 business days of the incident
- Resolution attempted within 5 business days

### Outcome
If resolved, no further action needed. If unresolved, proceed to Step 2.

---

## Written Grievance (Step 2)

### When to Use
If informal resolution is unsuccessful or inappropriate given the nature of the complaint.

### Process

1. **Obtain Grievance Form**
   - Available from Student Services
   - May also submit typed letter

2. **Complete the Form Including:**
   - Your name and contact information
   - Date of incident(s)
   - Description of the grievance
   - Names of individuals involved
   - Previous attempts at resolution
   - Desired outcome/remedy
   - Your signature and date

3. **Submit to Program Director**
   - Submit within 10 business days of Step 1
   - Keep a copy for your records

### Timeline
- Program Director responds in writing within 10 business days
- Response includes decision and rationale

---

## Appeal to School Director (Step 3)

### When to Use
If dissatisfied with the Program Director's decision.

### Process

1. **Submit Written Appeal**
   - Within 5 business days of Step 2 decision
   - Include original grievance and response
   - State specific reasons for appeal
   - Provide any additional documentation

2. **School Director Review**
   - Reviews all documentation
   - May meet with parties
   - May conduct additional investigation

3. **Decision**
   - Written response within 10 business days
   - Decision includes rationale
   - School Director's decision is final for internal process

## Grievance Documentation

### Grievance Form Content

**Section 1: Student Information**
- Name
- Student ID
- Program
- Contact information

**Section 2: Grievance Details**
- Date of incident
- Location
- Persons involved
- Detailed description (who, what, when, where)
- Impact on student

**Section 3: Previous Resolution Attempts**
- Dates of informal discussions
- Persons contacted
- Outcomes

**Section 4: Desired Outcome**
- Specific remedy sought
- Why this is appropriate

**Section 5: Certification**
- Statement of truthfulness
- Signature and date

## Record Keeping

### Documentation Maintained
- Original grievance form
- All correspondence
- Investigation notes
- Witness statements
- Decision letters
- Appeal documentation

### Retention
- Grievance records maintained for 5 years
- Stored confidentially in separate file
- Access limited to need-to-know basis

## Rights of the Complainant

Throughout the grievance process, students have the right to:
- Be treated fairly and respectfully
- Present relevant information and witnesses
- Receive timely responses
- Have a support person present (non-participating)
- Be free from retaliation
- Receive written decisions
- Appeal decisions

---

## Non-Retaliation

### Policy
No student shall be subject to retaliation for filing a grievance, participating in a grievance investigation, or supporting another student's grievance.

### Prohibited Actions
- Academic penalties
- Negative treatment by staff
- Intimidation or harassment
- Threats or coercion

### Reporting Retaliation
Report suspected retaliation immediately to the School Director. Substantiated retaliation will result in disciplinary action.

---

## Special Circumstances

### Grievances Against the School Director
- Submit directly to school owner/board
- Alternative designee will investigate

### Grievances Involving Discrimination/Harassment
- May be addressed under separate Title IX/harassment policy
- Immediate interim measures may be taken
- Specialized investigation procedures apply

### Emergency Situations
- Immediate safety concerns addressed first
- Expedited process may be used
- Interim measures may be implemented
