## Purpose

In compliance with the Drug-Free Schools and Communities Act (DFSCA) and California law, [SCHOOL NAME] is committed to maintaining a drug-free and alcohol-free learning environment.

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All students
- All employees
- All visitors
- All school premises and activities
- All externship/clinical sites

---

## Policy Statement

[SCHOOL NAME] prohibits the unlawful manufacture, distribution, dispensation, possession, or use of controlled substances and alcohol on school premises or as part of any school-sponsored activity.

---

## Standards of Conduct

### Prohibited Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited:

1. **Controlled Substances**
   - Possession of illegal drugs
   - Use of illegal drugs
   - Sale or distribution of illegal drugs
   - Manufacturing of illegal drugs
   - Being under the influence of illegal drugs

2. **Alcohol**
   - Possession of alcohol on school premises
   - Consumption of alcohol on school premises
   - Being under the influence while at school
   - Distribution of alcohol to minors

3. **Prescription Medications**
   - Use of prescription medications not prescribed to you
   - Distribution of prescription medications
   - Abuse of prescription medications

4. **Clinical/Externship Sites**
   - All clinical sites have zero-tolerance policies
   - Students must comply with site policies
   - May be subject to drug testing

## Legal Sanctions

### Federal Penalties

The federal Controlled Substances Act provides penalties up to:
- 1st offense: Up to 1 year imprisonment and/or $1,000-$10,000 fine
- 2nd offense: Up to 2 years imprisonment and/or $2,500-$250,000 fine
- 3+ offenses: Up to 3 years imprisonment and/or $5,000-$250,000 fine

Trafficking offenses carry substantially higher penalties, including mandatory minimum sentences.

### California Penalties

Under California Health and Safety Code, penalties include:
- Possession: Up to 1 year in jail and/or fines
- Possession for sale: 2-4 years in state prison
- Sale or transportation: 3-9 years in state prison
- Felony convictions may result in loss of professional licensure eligibility

### Alcohol Offenses (Under 21)

- First offense: $250 fine and 24-32 hours community service
- Second offense: $500 fine and 36-48 hours community service
- May result in driver's license suspension

## Health Risks

### Alcohol Abuse

Short-term effects:
- Impaired judgment and coordination
- Slowed reaction times
- Risk of alcohol poisoning

Long-term effects:
- Liver disease
- Heart problems
- Brain damage
- Increased cancer risk
- Addiction

### Drug Abuse

Effects vary by substance but may include:
- Overdose and death
- Addiction
- Brain damage
- Heart problems
- Mental health disorders
- Infectious disease (hepatitis, HIV)
- Impaired judgment leading to accidents

### Impact on Healthcare Careers

Substance abuse can:
- Prevent certification/licensure
- Result in loss of existing credentials
- Create barriers to employment
- Endanger patient safety
- Lead to criminal record
