## Purpose

This policy establishes the grounds for student dismissal from [SCHOOL NAME] and the procedures for reinstatement.

## Grounds for Dismissal

A student may be dismissed from [SCHOOL NAME] for any of the following reasons:

### Academic Dismissal
- Failure to maintain [[Satisfactory Academic Progress Policy|Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)]] after the appeal process
- GPA below 2.0 (70%) for two consecutive evaluation periods
- Failure to complete program within 150% of normal time frame

### Attendance Dismissal
- Per the [[Attendance Policy]], attendance below 80% after warning and probation
- Excessive unexcused absences (more than 10% of program hours)
- Failure to return from approved leave of absence

### Conduct Dismissal
- Violation of the [[Student Conduct Policy]]
- Academic dishonesty (cheating, plagiarism)
- Falsification of records or applications
- Threatening behavior toward students, staff, or faculty
- Possession or use of drugs/alcohol on campus (see [[Drug-Free School Policy]])
- Theft or destruction of property
- Violation of clinical site policies during externship

### Financial Dismissal
- Failure to meet financial obligations per signed payment plan
- Default on payment agreement for more than 60 days
- Failure to resolve outstanding balance after notice

---

## Dismissal Procedures

### Step 1: Documentation
The Program Director or designee will document:
- Specific violation or grounds for dismissal
- Previous warnings issued
- Any relevant evidence
- Date and nature of incident(s)

### Step 2: Notification
The student will receive written notification including:
- Specific reason(s) for dismissal
- Effective date of dismissal
- Right to appeal
- Appeal deadline (10 business days from notification)
- Instructions for returning school property

### Step 3: Student Conference
Before dismissal is finalized, the student will be offered an opportunity to meet with the Program Director to:
- Discuss the circumstances
- Present any mitigating factors
- Understand the appeal process

### Step 4: Final Determination
The Program Director will issue a final dismissal letter including:
- Confirmation of dismissal
- Last date of attendance
- Status of tuition account
- Refund calculation (if applicable) per [[Refund Policy]]
- Transcript request information
- Reinstatement eligibility

---

## Appeal Process

Students may appeal dismissal by following the [[Grievance Procedure]]:

1. Submit written appeal within 10 business days
2. Include explanation and any supporting documentation
3. Appeal reviewed by Appeals Committee
4. Decision issued within 15 business days
5. Decision is final

---

## Reinstatement Eligibility

### When Reinstatement May Be Considered

| Dismissal Type | Eligibility | Waiting Period |
|----------------|-------------|----------------|
| Academic (SAP) | Yes | One term (or 3 months) |
| Attendance | Yes | 6 months |
| Conduct (minor) | Case-by-case | 6-12 months |
| Conduct (major) | Usually no | N/A |
| Financial | Yes (when resolved) | When paid in full |

### When Reinstatement Is NOT Permitted

- Dismissal for violence or threats
- Dismissal for sexual harassment
- Dismissal for academic dishonesty (repeated)
- Criminal conduct on campus
- Actions that endangered others

---

## Reinstatement Procedures

### Step 1: Application for Reinstatement
Submit written request to the Program Director including:
- [ ] Reinstatement application form
- [ ] Letter explaining circumstances leading to dismissal
- [ ] Description of actions taken to address issues
- [ ] Evidence of readiness to return (if applicable)
- [ ] Payment of any outstanding balance

### Step 2: Reinstatement Review
The Reinstatement Committee will evaluate:
- Nature of original dismissal
- Time elapsed since dismissal
- Student's efforts toward remediation
- Likelihood of successful completion
- Available space in program

### Step 3: Reinstatement Decision
Possible outcomes:
- **Approved**: Student may re-enroll with conditions
- **Approved with Probation**: Must meet specific requirements
- **Denied**: Student is not eligible for reinstatement
- **Deferred**: Review again after additional time/action

### Step 4: Conditions of Reinstatement
Reinstated students must typically:
- Sign new enrollment agreement
- Agree to any academic or conduct probation terms
- Complete any remedial requirements
- Maintain specified GPA or attendance
- Meet financial obligations

---

## Re-Entry into Program

### Academic Standing Upon Return
- Previous passing grades are retained
- Failed courses must be repeated
- SAP status recalculated from return date
- Maximum time frame calculation continues from original start

### Financial Considerations
- New payment plan may be required
- Previous balance must be paid
- STRF assessment applies to new enrollment
- Financial aid eligibility must be reconfirmed

### Scheduling
- Subject to space availability
- May need to wait for new cohort start
- Make-up work may be required

---

## Records Retention

- Dismissal documentation: 5 years minimum
- Appeal records: 5 years
- Reinstatement applications: Permanent (if approved)
- Denied applications: 5 years
