# Clinical Externship Policy

---

## Overview

This policy establishes requirements and guidelines for students participating in clinical externship experiences at [SCHOOL NAME]. Externship is a critical component of healthcare training programs, providing hands-on experience in real healthcare settings.

| Program | Clinical Component | Hours Required |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|
| Medical Assistant | Externship | 160 hours |

| EKG Technician | Clinical Observation | 10 hours |
| Medical Coding | None | N/A |

---

## Externship Purpose

### Learning Objectives

Clinical externships allow students to:
- Apply classroom knowledge in real settings
- Develop professional competencies
- Build workplace skills
- Gain supervised practical experience
- Prepare for employment

### Not Employment

**Important:** Externship is an educational experience:
- Students are not employees of clinical sites
- Not entitled to wages or benefits
- Supervised by site and school personnel
- Focus is on learning, not production

## Site Placement

### How Sites Are Selected

Factors considered:
1. Program requirements and objectives
2. Site availability and capacity
3. Student schedule and transportation
4. Geographic proximity
5. Site-student match
6. Learning opportunities available

### Student Role in Placement

- Submit placement preferences (not guaranteed)
- Complete required paperwork on time
- Communicate schedule constraints
- Accept assigned placement

**Note:** Students may not arrange their own placements without approval.

See [[Externship Placement]] for detailed process.

## Attendance Requirements

### Attendance Policy

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Required hours | 100% must be completed |
| Tardiness | Not acceptable |
| Absences | Must be made up |
| Notification | Site AND school must be notified |

### Absence Notification

If you must be absent:
1. Contact site supervisor BEFORE shift
2. Contact Clinical Coordinator same day
3. Document reason
4. Arrange make-up time

### Consequences

- Missed hours extend program completion
- Excessive absences may result in site removal
- Pattern of absences may result in program failure

---

## Supervision Requirements

### Direct Supervision

Students must work under supervision:
- Qualified healthcare professional on-site
- Available for guidance and questions
- Observes student procedures
- Provides feedback and instruction

### Scope of Activities

Students may perform:
- Procedures learned and demonstrated in lab
- Tasks within their program curriculum
- Activities approved by supervisor

Students may NOT:
- Perform procedures not yet learned
- Work independently (unless approved)
- Replace regular site staff
- Make clinical decisions independently

## Dress Code

### Standard Requirements

- Clean, pressed scrubs (program-specified color)
- Closed-toe, non-slip shoes

### HIPAA Compliance

- Protect patient health information

- Not photograph or record in clinical areas
- Follow site confidentiality policies
- Report any breaches immediately

### Violations

HIPAA violations may result in:
- Site removal
- Program dismissal
- Civil/criminal penalties
- Professional consequences

## Safety

### Infection Control

Follow all:

- Standard precautions
- PPE requirements

## Problems at Clinical Site

### Types of Problems

Report to Clinical Coordinator if you experience:
- Inadequate supervision
- Harassment or discrimination
- Unsafe conditions
- Excessive duties unrelated to learning
- Scheduling issues
- Other concerns

### How to Report

1. Contact Clinical Coordinator immediately
2. Document the situation
3. Do not confront site staff angrily
4. Follow instructions from Coordinator

### Site Removal

Students may be removed from site for:
- Unprofessional conduct
- Safety violations
- Attendance issues
- Performance concerns
- Site request

After removal:
- Meet with Clinical Coordinator
- Determine if reassignment possible
- May result in program failure
