## Passing Grade Requirements

### Minimum Standards

- **Minimum passing grade:** 70% (C) or higher
- **Minimum GPA for graduation:** 2.0
- **All required courses:** Must be passed with C or higher
- **Externship:** Must receive grade of P (Pass)

### Failed Courses

Students who fail a course must:
1. Repeat the course in its entirety
2. Pay associated repeat fees
3. Complete within maximum timeframe
4. May only repeat a course once

## Examination Policies

### Written Examinations

- Scheduled in advance on course syllabus
- No electronic devices permitted
- Students must remain seated until finished
- No talking or communication during exams
- Submit exam materials before leaving

### Practical Examinations

- Skills must be demonstrated to instructor
- May have multiple attempts per competency
- Must meet all critical criteria to pass
- Unsafe practices = automatic failure

### Final Examinations

- Comprehensive of course material
- Required for course completion
- May include written and practical components

---

## Make-Up Examination Policy

### Eligibility

Make-up exams permitted for:
- Documented illness
- Family emergency
- Prior approved absence

### Procedure

1. Contact instructor within 24 hours of missed exam
2. Provide documentation for absence
3. Schedule make-up within one week
4. Alternative exam may be administered

### Conditions

- No make-up for unexcused absences (grade of 0)
- Maximum of 2 make-up exams per course
- Make-up exams may differ from original

---

## Retake Policy

### Examination Retakes

- Students may retake one failed exam per course
- Maximum score on retake: 75%
- Retake must occur within one week
- Only one retake attempt permitted

### Skills Retakes

- Failed skills require remediation first
- Must demonstrate competency to instructor
- No maximum score limitation
- Multiple attempts may be permitted at instructor discretion

---

## Incomplete Grades (I)

### Eligibility

Incomplete grades may be granted when:
- Student has passing grade to date
- Extenuating circumstances prevent completion
- Instructor and Program Director approve

### Requirements

1. Student must request incomplete in writing
2. Complete Incomplete Grade Agreement form
3. Specify work to be completed
4. Agree to completion deadline (maximum 2 weeks)

### Resolution

- Complete required work by deadline
- Grade calculated based on completed work
- Failure to complete = grade of F
- Extensions require Director approval

## Grade Point Average (GPA)

### Calculation

GPA = Total Grade Points รท Total Credit Hours Attempted

**Example:**
| Course | Grade | Grade Points | Hours | Quality Points |
|--------|-------|--------------|-------|----------------|
| Course A | A | 4.0 | 3 | 12.0 |
| Course B | B | 3.0 | 3 | 9.0 |
| Course C | C | 2.0 | 2 | 4.0 |
| **Total** | | | **8** | **25.0** |

GPA = 25.0 รท 8 = **3.125**

### GPA Requirements

| Requirement | Minimum GPA |
|-------------|-------------|
| Good Standing | 2.0 |
| Academic Warning | Below 2.0 |
| Academic Probation | Below 2.0 after warning |
| Graduation | 2.0 |

## Grade Appeals

### Grounds for Appeal

Students may appeal grades based on:
- Calculation error
- Inconsistent application of grading criteria
- Procedural violation
- Unfair treatment

### Appeal Process

**Step 1:** Meet with instructor within 5 business days
- Discuss grade concern
- Attempt informal resolution

**Step 2:** Written appeal to Program Director
- Submit within 5 business days of Step 1
- Include specific grounds for appeal
- Provide supporting documentation

**Step 3:** Director Review
- Director investigates within 10 business days
- May meet with student and instructor
- Issues written decision

**Step 4:** Final Appeal to School Director
- Submit within 5 business days of Step 3
- School Director decision is final

## Academic Integrity

### Standards

All work must be the student's own. Academic dishonesty includes:
- Cheating on examinations
- Copying from other students
- Using unauthorized materials
- Plagiarism
- Falsifying documents
- Sharing exam information

### Consequences

| Offense | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| First | Zero on assignment/exam; written warning |
| Second | Failing grade (F) for the course |
| Third | Dismissal from program |
