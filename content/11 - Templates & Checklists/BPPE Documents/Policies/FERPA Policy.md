## Student Rights Under FERPA

### Right to Inspect and Review Records

Students have the right to inspect and review their education records within 45 days of request.

**Procedure:**
1. Submit written request to Registrar
2. Identify the records you wish to inspect
3. Registrar will make arrangements and notify you
4. Review records in person during business hours

### Right to Request Amendment

Students have the right to request amendment of records they believe are inaccurate, misleading, or in violation of privacy rights.

**Procedure:**
1. Submit written request to Registrar
2. Identify the record and explain why it should be changed
3. School will notify you of decision within 30 days
4. If denied, you may request a hearing
5. You may add a statement to your file

### Right to Consent to Disclosure

Students have the right to consent before the school discloses personally identifiable information from records, except as authorized by law.

### Right to File a Complaint

Students have the right to file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education concerning failures to comply with FERPA.

**Contact:**
Family Policy Compliance Office
U.S. Department of Education
400 Maryland Avenue, SW
Washington, DC 20202-8520

## Definition of Education Records

### Included

Education records are any records directly related to a student that are maintained by the school, including:
- Enrollment documents
- Academic transcripts
- Attendance records
- Financial records
- Externship evaluations
- Disciplinary records

### Excluded

The following are NOT education records:
- Sole possession records (personal notes kept by staff)
- Law enforcement records
- Employment records (when employment is not contingent on student status)
- Medical/treatment records
- Alumni records (created after attendance)

---

## Directory Information

### Definition

Directory information is information generally not considered harmful or an invasion of privacy if disclosed.

### Designated Directory Information

[SCHOOL NAME] designates the following as directory information:
- Student name
- Address (local and permanent)
- Telephone number
- Email address
- Date and place of birth
- Program of study
- Enrollment status (full/part-time)
- Dates of attendance
- Degrees and certificates awarded
- Awards and honors
- Photograph
- Most recent school attended

### Opting Out

Students may request that directory information not be disclosed by:
1. Submitting written request to Registrar
2. Within 14 days of enrollment
3. Request remains in effect until revoked in writing

## Disclosure Without Consent

FERPA permits disclosure without consent in the following circumstances:

### School Officials with Legitimate Educational Interest

- School officials include: administrators, faculty, staff, contractors, and others performing institutional services
- Legitimate educational interest exists if official needs access to fulfill professional responsibilities

### Other Institutions

- Upon request from another school where student seeks to enroll
- Student will be notified unless notification is waived

### Financial Aid

- In connection with financial aid application, receipt, or repayment

### Accrediting Organizations

- To organizations conducting educational studies or accreditation activities

### Legal Compliance

- In response to lawful subpoena or court order
- School will make reasonable effort to notify student in advance

### Health or Safety Emergency

- To appropriate parties when necessary to protect health or safety
- Must document threat and persons notified

### State and Federal Authorities

- To specified officials for audit, evaluation, or enforcement purposes

### Victims of Crime

- Results of disciplinary proceedings may be disclosed to alleged victims of crimes of violence

## Procedures for Disclosure

### Requests for Records

1. **Written Request Required**
   - State the purpose of disclosure
   - Identify specific records needed
   - Provide authorization if required

2. **Review by Registrar**
   - Verify identity of requester
   - Confirm authorization or permitted disclosure
   - Redact non-disclosable information

3. **Documentation**
   - Maintain record of all disclosures
   - Document date, recipient, purpose
   - Available to student for review

### Student Consent

When consent is required, it must:
- Specify records to be disclosed
- State purpose of disclosure
- Identify recipient
- Be signed and dated by student

## Security of Records

### Physical Records

- Stored in locked files
- Access limited to authorized personnel
- Destroyed securely when no longer needed

### Electronic Records

- Password protected systems
- Access controls by role
- Regular backup procedures
- Secure disposal of electronic media

### Staff Training

- All staff trained on FERPA requirements
- Annual refresher training
- New hire orientation includes FERPA

## FERPA Rights Notification

**Annual Notification of FERPA Rights**

The Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) affords students certain rights with respect to their education records:

1. **Right to Inspect and Review**
   You have the right to inspect and review your education records within 45 days of the date [SCHOOL NAME] receives your request.

2. **Right to Request Amendment**
   You have the right to request the amendment of your education records that you believe are inaccurate, misleading, or in violation of your privacy rights.

3. **Right to Consent to Disclosure**
   You have the right to consent to disclosures of personally identifiable information contained in your education records, except to the extent that FERPA authorizes disclosure without consent.

4. **Right to File a Complaint**
   You have the right to file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education concerning alleged failures by [SCHOOL NAME] to comply with FERPA.
