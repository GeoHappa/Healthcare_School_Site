## Purpose

Regular attendance is essential for academic success and professional development. This policy establishes attendance expectations and procedures for all students.

## Scope

## Policy Statement

Students are expected to attend all scheduled class sessions, laboratory sessions, and externship hours. Consistent attendance is critical for:
- Mastering program content
- Developing professional habits
- Meeting program completion requirements
- Preparing for healthcare employment

## Attendance Requirements

### Minimum Attendance Standard

| Component | Minimum Requirement |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Overall Program | 80% attendance |
| Individual Courses | 80% attendance |
| Externship/Clinical | 100% of assigned hours |

### Tracking Attendance

- Students must sign in at the beginning of each class
- Students must sign out at the end of each class
- Attendance is recorded daily by the instructor
- Students may verify their attendance record with the Registrar

## Definitions

### Absence
Any missed class session or portion thereof.

### Excused Absence
An absence due to documented circumstances beyond the student's control:
- Medical illness (with doctor's note)
- Medical emergency (self or immediate family)
- Death of immediate family member
- Court-ordered appearance
- Military duty
- Approved school business

### Unexcused Absence
An absence without acceptable documentation:
- Personal appointments
- Transportation problems
- Work schedule conflicts
- Failure to notify school
- Undocumented illness

### Tardy
- Arriving more than 10 minutes after class start time
- Leaving more than 10 minutes before class end time
- Three (3) tardies = one (1) unexcused absence

### No Call/No Show
Failure to attend class without prior notification.

## Absence Reporting Procedure

1. **Notify the school BEFORE class begins**
   - Call: [PHONE NUMBER]
   - Email: [EMAIL ADDRESS]

2. **Leave a message including:**
   - Your name
   - Program/class
   - Reason for absence
   - Expected return date

3. **Provide documentation upon return**
   - Submit within 3 business days
   - Documentation subject to verification

4. **Make up missed work**
   - Contact instructor within 24 hours of return
   - Complete make-up within one week

## Consequences of Poor Attendance

### Warning Levels

| Attendance Level | Action |
|------------------|--------|
| 85% or below | Verbal warning from instructor |
| 80% or below | Written warning from Program Director |
| 75% or below | Academic probation |
| 70% or below | Subject to program dismissal |

### Attendance Improvement Plan

Students falling below 80% attendance will:
1. Meet with Program Director
2. Develop an Attendance Improvement Plan
3. Be monitored weekly
4. Have one evaluation period to improve

### Dismissal

Students may be dismissed for:
- Falling below 70% attendance
- Three (3) consecutive absences without notification
- Failure to improve after Attendance Improvement Plan
- Pattern of unexcused absences

---

## Make-Up Policy

### Classroom Hours
- Make-up hours available by appointment
- Maximum make-up: 10% of program hours
- Must be completed before program end
- Make-up time is at instructor's discretion

### Examinations
- Missed exams must be made up within one week
- Scheduling requires instructor approval
- Alternative exam may be administered

### Laboratory Skills
- Missed lab skills must be demonstrated
- Open lab time available for practice
- Skills must be evaluated by instructor

### Externship Hours
- Externship hours CANNOT be made up
- All assigned hours must be completed
- Excessive absences may require reassignment or extended externship

---

## Leave of Absence

### Eligibility
Students may request a leave of absence for:
- Medical reasons (documentation required)
- Military duty
- Family emergency
- Other extenuating circumstances

### Procedure
1. Submit written request to Program Director
2. Include reason and expected duration
3. Provide supporting documentation
4. Receive written approval before leave begins

### Terms
- Maximum leave: 180 days
- Must return by scheduled date
- Failure to return = withdrawal
- Re-enrollment subject to availability

---

## Appeals

Students may appeal attendance-related decisions by:
1. Submitting written appeal within 5 business days
2. Explaining extenuating circumstances
3. Providing supporting documentation
4. Requesting meeting with School Director
