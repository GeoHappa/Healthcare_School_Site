# ADA Accommodations Policy

---

## Overview

[SCHOOL NAME] is committed to providing equal access to education for students with disabilities in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

---

## Policy Statement

[SCHOOL NAME] provides reasonable accommodations to qualified students with documented disabilities. Accommodations are designed to provide equal access, not to fundamentally alter program requirements or provide unfair advantage.

---

## Who is Eligible

### Qualified Individual with Disability

A person who:
- Has a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more major life activities
- Has a record of such impairment, OR
- Is regarded as having such impairment

AND

- Meets the academic and technical standards for admission
- Can perform essential program functions with or without accommodation

### Major Life Activities Include

- Learning
- Reading
- Concentrating
- Thinking
- Communicating
- Seeing
- Hearing
- Walking
- Breathing
- Working

---

## Types of Accommodations

### Academic Accommodations

| Accommodation | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Extended test time | Typically 1.5x or 2x |
| Separate testing location | Reduced distraction environment |
| Breaks during exams | Scheduled rest periods |
| Alternative format materials | Large print, audio, electronic |
| Note-taking assistance | Peer notes, recording permission |
| Preferential seating | Front row, near door, etc. |
| Use of assistive technology | Screen readers, speech-to-text |
| Reduced course load | Extended program length |

### Physical Accommodations

| Accommodation | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Accessible classrooms | Wheelchair accessible spaces |
| Elevator access | Building accessibility |
| Adjustable furniture | Desks, chairs |
| Reserved parking | Close to building |
| Modified lab equipment | Accessible equipment |

### Clinical/Externship Accommodations

Accommodations in clinical settings must:
- Not compromise patient safety
- Be coordinated with clinical site
- Maintain essential competencies
- Be discussed early in planning

## Request Process

### Step 1: Self-Identify

Students must self-identify to request accommodations:
- Disability Services contact
- Complete intake form
- Disclose disability and limitations

### Step 2: Provide Documentation

Submit documentation that:
- Is from qualified professional
- States the disability diagnosis
- Describes functional limitations
- Recommends specific accommodations
- Is recent (within 3-5 years for most conditions)

### Step 3: Interactive Meeting

Meet with Disability Services to:
- Discuss limitations and needs
- Review documentation
- Explore accommodation options
- Determine appropriate accommodations

### Step 4: Receive Accommodation Letter

If approved:
- Written accommodation letter provided
- Student shares with instructors
- Accommodations implemented
- Ongoing communication maintained

---

## Documentation Requirements

### Required Elements

Documentation should include:
- Diagnosing professional credentials
- Date of evaluation
- Specific diagnosis
- Description of how disability affects you
- Functional limitations in educational setting
- Recommended accommodations

### By Disability Type

| Disability Type | Documentation Source |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Learning disability | Psychoeducational evaluation |
| ADHD | Psychological/psychiatric evaluation |
| Physical disability | Physician letter |
| Mental health | Psychiatrist/psychologist |
| Chronic health | Treating physician |
| Visual/hearing | Specialist evaluation |

### Documentation Not Accepted

- IEP/504 plans alone (may supplement)
- Prescriptions without explanation
- Documentation without credentials
- Very old documentation (case by case)

## Interactive Process

### What It Means

The interactive process is collaborative discussion to:
- Understand your limitations
- Identify barriers in the program
- Explore possible accommodations
- Determine effective accommodations
- Find solutions that work

### Your Participation

You should:
- Describe how disability affects you
- Explain what helps you succeed
- Consider suggestions offered
- Be open to alternatives
- Communicate ongoing needs

### Outcome

Together we determine:
- What accommodations are appropriate
- How accommodations will be implemented
- Timeline for reassessment
- Process for concerns

## Essential Functions

### What Are Essential Functions

Core activities required to complete the program:
- Clinical skills and competencies
- Safety requirements
- Professional standards
- Licensing/certification requirements

### Cannot Be Accommodated

Accommodations cannot:
- Eliminate essential functions
- Fundamentally alter the program
- Compromise patient safety
- Lower academic standards
- Create undue burden

### Program-Specific Requirements

| Program | Essential Functions Include |
|---------|----------------------------|
| Medical Assistant | Patient care, injections, lifting |
| Phlebotomy | Venipuncture, fine motor skills |
| Medical Coding | Analytical skills, computer use |
| EKG | Lead placement, patient interaction |

## Confidentiality

### Privacy Protection

- Disability information is confidential
- Shared only with those who need to know
- Instructors receive accommodation letter only (not diagnosis)
- Records maintained separately from academic file

### What Instructors Are Told

- That you have approved accommodations
- What accommodations to provide
- NOT your diagnosis or disability details

---

## Implementing Accommodations

### Student Responsibilities

1. Request accommodations each term (if required)
2. Provide accommodation letter to instructors
3. Discuss implementation with instructors
4. Use accommodations appropriately
5. Report problems promptly

### Instructor Responsibilities

1. Implement approved accommodations
2. Maintain confidentiality
3. Contact Disability Services with questions
4. Report concerns through proper channels

### Timeline

- Request accommodations early
- Allow time for documentation review
- Test accommodations need advance notice
- Some accommodations take time to arrange

---

## Externship Accommodations

### Special Considerations

Clinical accommodations require:
- Early planning
- Site coordination
- Safety analysis
- Sometimes creative solutions

### Process

1. Discuss needs with Disability Services and Clinical Coordinator
2. Identify appropriate site
3. Coordinate with site (disclosure at your discretion)
4. Implement accommodations
5. Monitor and adjust as needed

### Limitations

Some accommodations may not be possible in all clinical settings due to:
- Patient safety requirements
- Site capabilities
- Essential function requirements

---

## Temporary Conditions

### Short-Term Disabilities

For temporary conditions (broken arm, surgery recovery):
- Contact Student Services
- Provide medical documentation
- Discuss temporary accommodations
- Limited duration arrangement

### Process

May be handled through:
- Disability Services (if meeting criteria)
- Student Services (informal arrangement)
- Medical leave of absence

---

## Service Animals

### Policy

Service animals are permitted:
- Dogs trained to perform specific tasks
- Related to person's disability
- Under handler's control

### Requirements

- Animal must be trained
- Must be housebroken
- Must be controlled
- Handler responsible for care

### Not Service Animals

- Emotional support animals (different process)
- Pets
- Animals in training

## Grievances

### If Accommodations Denied

If you disagree with accommodation decision:
1. Discuss with Disability Services
2. Request review by Program Director
3. Appeal to School Director
4. External complaint to OCR (if unresolved)

### External Complaints

Office for Civil Rights (OCR)
1-800-421-3481
www.ed.gov/ocr
