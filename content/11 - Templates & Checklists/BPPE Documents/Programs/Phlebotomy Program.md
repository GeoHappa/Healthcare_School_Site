## Program Overview

The Phlebotomy Technician program prepares students for entry-level positions as phlebotomists, specializing in blood collection for laboratory analysis, transfusion, donation, or research.

## California Certification Requirement

> **IMPORTANT:** In California, phlebotomists MUST be certified by the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) to perform venipuncture in licensed healthcare facilities.

This program meets CDPH requirements for CPT I (Certified Phlebotomy Technician I) eligibility.

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, graduates will be able to:

### Technical Skills
- Perform venipuncture using multiple techniques
- Perform capillary/skin puncture
- Select appropriate collection equipment
- Identify correct order of draw
- Apply proper tourniquet technique
- Handle and process specimens correctly
- Recognize and avoid complications

### Safety Skills
- Apply standard precautions
- Properly dispose of sharps
- Respond to accidental exposure
- Maintain clean work environment
- Use personal protective equipment

### Professional Skills
- Verify patient identification
- Communicate effectively with patients
- Calm anxious patients
- Maintain patient confidentiality
- Document procedures accurately
- Work within scope of practice

## Certification Process

### Step 1: Complete Program
- Finish all didactic hours
- Complete clinical requirements
- Receive certificate of completion

### Step 2: Pass National Certification Exam
CDPH-approved certifying agencies:

| Agency | Exam | Cost |
|--------|------|------|
| ASCP | PBT | ~$135 |
| NCCT | NCPT | ~$115 |
| AMT | RPT | ~$130 |
| NHA | CPT | ~$125 |

### Step 3: Apply to CDPH
- Submit application and fee ($100)
- Provide proof of training
- Provide certification exam results
- Receive CPT I certificate

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I work while waiting for CDPH certification?**
A: Limited. You may work under direct supervision or in non-licensed settings, but licensed facilities require CPT I certification.

**Q: How long is CPT I certification valid?**
A: CPT I certification is valid for 2 years and must be renewed.

**Q: Can I take the exam before completing clinical?**
A: No, you must complete both didactic and clinical requirements before taking a national exam.
