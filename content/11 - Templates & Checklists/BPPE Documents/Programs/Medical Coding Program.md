## Program Overview

The Medical Coding Specialist program prepares students for careers in healthcare revenue cycle, translating medical documentation into standardized codes for billing, reimbursement, and data analysis.

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, graduates will be able to:

### Coding Skills
- Assign accurate ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes
- Assign accurate CPT procedure codes
- Apply HCPCS Level II codes appropriately
- Abstract information from medical records
- Apply coding guidelines and conventions
- Identify coding errors and discrepancies

### Reimbursement Knowledge
- Understand healthcare payment systems
- Apply Medicare/Medicaid guidelines
- Process insurance claims
- Identify compliance issues
- Understand coding audits

### Professional Skills
- Maintain patient confidentiality (HIPAA)
- Work independently with accuracy
- Meet production and quality standards
- Use coding software and encoders
- Communicate with providers about documentation

## Certification Information

Graduates are prepared for national certification exams:

### Primary Certification (AAPC)

| Credential | Description | Exam Cost |
|------------|-------------|-----------|
| CPC | Certified Professional Coder | $399 |

**AAPC Requirements:**
- Pass exam (100 multiple choice, 5 hr 40 min)
- Membership required ($175/year)
- Receive CPC-A (Apprentice) initially
- CPC after 2 years experience or alternate pathway

### Alternative Certification (AHIMA)

| Credential | Description | Exam Cost |
|------------|-------------|-----------|
| CCA | Certified Coding Associate | $299 |

**AHIMA Requirements:**
- Pass exam (100 questions, 2 hours)
- Membership recommended

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need medical experience to become a coder?**
A: No, but medical terminology and anatomy knowledge is essential (included in the program).

**Q: How long before I can work remotely?**
A: Most remote positions require 1-2 years of experience. Entry-level is often on-site.

**Q: What is the CPC-A?**
A: The Apprentice designation indicates you are newly certified. It converts to CPC after 2 years of coding experience.

**Q: Is typing speed important?**
A: Yes, proficient typing (35+ WPM) helps with productivity.
