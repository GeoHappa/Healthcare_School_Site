## Program Overview

The Medical Assistant program prepares students for entry-level positions in healthcare settings, performing both administrative and clinical duties under the supervision of physicians and other healthcare providers.

## Learning Objectives

Upon completion, graduates will be able to:

### Clinical Skills
- Measure and record vital signs accurately
- Prepare patients for examinations
- Perform venipuncture and capillary puncture
- Collect and process specimens
- Perform electrocardiography (EKG)
- Administer medications as directed
- Assist with minor surgical procedures
- Apply principles of aseptic technique
- Perform CLIA-waived laboratory tests

### Administrative Skills
- Schedule patient appointments
- Maintain medical records (paper and electronic)
- Process insurance claims
- Verify insurance eligibility
- Handle telephone communications
- Manage front office operations

### Professional Skills
- Demonstrate professional conduct
- Communicate effectively with patients
- Apply HIPAA regulations
- Work within scope of practice
- Practice ethical decision-making

## Externship Information

### Requirements Before Externship
- Complete all classroom hours
- Pass all courses with 70% or higher
- Current CPR/BLS certification
- Background check cleared
- Drug screen cleared (if required)
- Immunizations current
- TB test negative

### Externship Sites
Students may be placed at:
- Physician offices
- Medical clinics
- Urgent care centers
- Specialty practices
- Hospital outpatient clinics

### Externship Competencies
Students must demonstrate competency in:
- [ ] Patient intake and vital signs
- [ ] Medication administration assistance
- [ ] Specimen collection
- [ ] EKG performance
- [ ] Sterilization and aseptic technique
- [ ] Medical records management
- [ ] Appointment scheduling
- [ ] Insurance verification

## Certification Information

Graduates are eligible to sit for national certification exams:

| Certification | Organization | Website |
|---------------|--------------|---------|
| NCMA | NCCT | ncctinc.com |
| CCMA | NHA | nhanow.com |
| RMA | AMT | americanmedtech.org |
| CMA (AAMA) | AAMA | aama-ntl.org* |

*CMA (AAMA) requires graduation from CAAHEP or ABHES accredited program

> **Note:** Certification is NOT required to work as a Medical Assistant in California. However, certification may enhance employment opportunities and demonstrate professional competency.
