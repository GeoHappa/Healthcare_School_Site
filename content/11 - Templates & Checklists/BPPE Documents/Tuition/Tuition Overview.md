## Understanding Your Investment

Investing in healthcare education opens doors to stable, rewarding careers in a growing field. This page provides transparent information about all costs associated with your training so you can make an informed decision and plan your finances accordingly.

All costs are disclosed in your [[Enrollment Agreement]] before you sign. California law requires schools to provide complete cost information upfront with no hidden fees.

## Fee Descriptions

### Registration Fee
- **Amount:** $_______ (maximum $250 per BPPE)
- **Purpose:** Administrative processing, enrollment
- **Refundable:** Non-refundable after enrollment

### Tuition
- **Covers:** Instruction, lab access, basic supplies
- **Payment:** Per payment plan selected
- **Refundable:** Pro-rata per [[Refund Policy]]

### Books and Supplies
- **Includes:** Textbooks, workbooks, basic supplies
- **Provided:** At start of program
- **Refundable:** Only if returned unused, in original condition

### Equipment
- **Includes:** Program-specific tools (stethoscope, scrubs, etc.)
- **Provided:** Before clinical/externship
- **Refundable:** Only if returned unused, in original condition

### STRF Assessment
- **Amount:** $2.50 per $1,000 of institutional charges (current rate)
- **Purpose:** Student Tuition Recovery Fund
- **Refundable:** Non-refundable
- See [[STRF Disclosure]] for details

## Financial Assistance Options

### WIOA Funding
The Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA) may cover full tuition for eligible students. See [[WIOA Information]] for details.

**Eligibility factors include:**
- Employment status (unemployed or underemployed)
- Income level
- Barriers to employment
- Veteran status

### Payment Plans
Flexible payment plans are available to help manage costs:
- No interest charged on payment plans
- Monthly payments based on program length
- Automatic payment options available
- See [[Payment Plans]] for options

### Employer Sponsorship
Some employers offer tuition assistance for employees seeking healthcare credentials. Ask your HR department about:
- Tuition reimbursement programs
- Professional development funds
- Upskilling initiatives

## Cost Comparison

When comparing school costs, consider the **total cost of attendance**, which includes:

| Cost Type | What to Ask |
|-----------|-------------|
| Tuition | Is the quoted price all-inclusive? |
| Registration | Is this fee applied toward tuition? |
| Books/Materials | Are textbooks included or extra? |
| Equipment | Are scrubs, stethoscope, etc. provided? |
| Certification Exam | Is exam prep or exam fee included? |
| Background Check | Who pays for this requirement? |

## Refund Policy Summary

California law protects students with specific refund rights. Key points:

- **Before classes start:** Full refund minus registration fee (max $250)
- **During program:** Pro-rata refund based on percentage completed
- **See [[Refund Policy]] for complete details**
