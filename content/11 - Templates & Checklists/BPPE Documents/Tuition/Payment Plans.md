# Payment Plans

---

## Overview

[SCHOOL NAME] offers flexible payment options to help students manage the cost of their education. All payment plans are interest-free when payments are made on time.

---

## Payment Options

### Option 1: Full Payment

**Benefits:**
- Simplest option
- No monthly tracking
- Potential discount: [X%] off tuition

**Details:**
- Full amount due at enrollment
- Payment by cash, check, credit card, or money order
- Receipt provided for records

---

### Option 2: Two-Payment Plan

**Structure:**
| Payment | Timing | Percentage |
|---------|--------|------------|
| First Payment | At enrollment | 50% |
| Second Payment | Program midpoint | 50% |

**Example (Medical Assistant - $15,000 total):**
| Payment | Amount | Due Date |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Payment 1 | $7,500 | Enrollment |
| Payment 2 | $7,500 | Week [X] |

---

### Option 3: Monthly Payment Plan

**Structure:**
- Down payment at enrollment
- Equal monthly installments
- Due on the 1st of each month

**Requirements:**
- Minimum down payment: 20% of total cost
- Automatic payment authorization required
- Maximum term: Program length + 1 month

**Example (Medical Assistant - $15,000 total):**
| Payment | Amount | Due Date |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Down Payment | $3,000 (20%) | Enrollment |
| Month 1-6 | $2,000/month | 1st of each month |

## Payment Plan Agreement

All payment plans require a signed Payment Plan Agreement including:

- Total amount due
- Payment schedule with due dates
- Late payment policy
- Default consequences
- Automatic payment authorization (if applicable)

See [[../Forms/Payment Plan Agreement]] for the form.

---

## Late Payment Policy

### Grace Period
- 5 calendar days after due date
- No penalty during grace period

### Late Fee
- $25 late fee after grace period
- Applied to each late payment
- Maximum late fees: $100 per program

### Collection Process

| Days Late | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| 6-14 days | Late fee applied, reminder sent |
| 15-30 days | Phone contact, written notice |
| 31-60 days | Final notice, payment plan review |
| 61+ days | Account default, collection action |

---

## Default Policy

An account is considered in default when:
- Payment is 60+ days past due
- Three or more payments are missed
- Student stops attending without arrangements

**Consequences of Default:**
- Suspension from classes
- Withholding of transcripts/diploma
- Report to collection agency
- Impact on credit history
- Legal action for balance owed

---

## Hardship Provisions

Students experiencing financial hardship may request:

### Payment Deferral
- Temporary postponement of payments
- Maximum 30 days
- One deferral per program
- Request in writing to Business Office

### Payment Plan Modification
- Restructure remaining balance
- Extend payment term
- Requires Director approval

### Emergency Assistance
- Limited emergency funds may be available
- Case-by-case basis
- Contact Student Services

---

## Refund of Overpayment

If a student overpays:
- Credit applied to account first
- Refund issued within 30 days if no balance
- Request in writing for refund

### Employer Tuition Assistance
- Direct billing available
