# WIOA Information

---

## What is WIOA?

The **Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act (WIOA)** is a federal law that provides funding for job training and employment services through local workforce development boards. WIOA-funded training can significantly reduce or eliminate tuition costs for eligible students.

---

## Benefits for Students

### Financial Benefits
- **Full or partial tuition coverage** for eligible participants
- **Support services** including transportation, childcare, and work supplies
- **Credential attainment** support and incentives
- **No repayment required** - WIOA funding is not a loan

### Career Support
- Career counseling and job search assistance
- Resume and interview preparation
- Job placement services
- Follow-up services after employment

---

## Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for WIOA funding, students typically must meet the following criteria:

### General Requirements
| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Age | 18 years or older (Adult Program) |
| Work Authorization | U.S. citizen or authorized to work |
| Selective Service | Males must be registered (if applicable) |
| Employment Status | Unemployed, underemployed, or seeking career change |

### Priority Groups
WIOA prioritizes services for:
- Public assistance recipients (CalWORKs, SNAP, SSI)
- Low-income individuals
- Individuals with barriers to employment
- Veterans and eligible spouses
- Individuals with disabilities
- Ex-offenders
- Homeless individuals
- Long-term unemployed

### Step 1: Contact Your Local America's Job Center
Find your local center at: [CalJOBS.ca.gov](https://www.caljobs.ca.gov)

**Folsom/Rancho Cordova:**
- America's Job Center of California
- (916) 255-3255

### Step 2: Complete Eligibility Determination
- Bring required documents (ID, income verification, etc.)
- Meet with a career counselor
- Complete assessment and career planning

### Step 3: Receive Approval
- Obtain an Individual Training Account (ITA)
- Submit ITA to school admissions
- Enroll in approved program

---

## Required Documentation

Applicants should bring the following to their WIOA appointment:

### Identity Documents
- Government-issued photo ID
- Social Security card
- Birth certificate or passport (for citizenship verification)

### Income Verification
- Last 6 months of pay stubs (if employed)
- Tax returns from past 2 years
- Public assistance documentation
- Unemployment benefits letter

### Other Documents
- High school diploma or GED
- Selective Service registration (males 18-25)
- DD-214 (veterans)
- Proof of address (utility bill, lease)

## ETPL - Eligible Training Provider List

Schools must be on the California **Eligible Training Provider List (ETPL)** for students to use WIOA funding. Being ETPL-approved means:

- Programs meet state quality standards
- Completion and employment outcomes are tracked
- Students can use Individual Training Accounts (ITAs)

### Checking ETPL Status
Verify school/program ETPL status at:
- [CalJOBS ETPL Search](https://www.caljobs.ca.gov/vosnet/Default.aspx)
- Contact local workforce board

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I work while receiving WIOA training?
Yes, many participants work part-time while attending training. Discuss scheduling options with your career counselor.

### What if I don't qualify for WIOA?
Alternative funding options include:
- [[Payment Plans]]
- Employer-sponsored training
- Private scholarships
- Personal financing

### Does WIOA cover all costs?
WIOA typically covers tuition. Additional costs (books, supplies, certification exams) may or may not be covered depending on available funding and your training plan.

### What happens if I drop out?
Dropping out may affect future WIOA eligibility. If you're struggling, contact your career counselor immediately to discuss options.

## School Responsibilities

### For WIOA-Funded Students
- Verify ITA authorization before enrollment
- Track attendance and progress
- Report completion and outcomes to workforce board
- Maintain ETPL eligibility

### Reporting Requirements
- Monthly/quarterly progress reports
- Credential attainment documentation
- Employment outcomes at 2nd and 4th quarter post-completion
