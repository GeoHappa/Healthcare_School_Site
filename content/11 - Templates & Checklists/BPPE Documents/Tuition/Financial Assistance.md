## Overview

[SCHOOL NAME] is committed to making healthcare education accessible. While we do not participate in federal financial aid programs (Title IV), several options are available to help you finance your education.

## Available Funding Options

### WIOA (Workforce Innovation and Opportunity Act)

The most common funding source for our students. WIOA can cover full tuition for eligible individuals.

**Eligibility:**
- Unemployed or underemployed
- Low-income individuals
- Veterans and eligible spouses
- Public assistance recipients
- Individuals with barriers to employment

**How to Apply:**
1. Contact your local America's Job Center
2. Complete eligibility determination
3. Obtain an Individual Training Account (ITA)
4. Submit ITA to school admissions

See [[WIOA Information]] for complete details.

---

### Payment Plans

Interest-free payment plans allow you to spread costs over the program duration.

**Features:**
- No credit check required
- No interest charged
- Flexible payment schedules
- Automatic payment options

**Options:**
- Pay in full (discount may apply)
- Two-payment plan
- Monthly installments

See [[Payment Plans]] for specific terms.

---

### Employer Sponsorship

Many employers offer tuition assistance for employees seeking healthcare credentials.

**Common Programs:**
- Tuition reimbursement
- Professional development funds
- Career advancement programs
- Upskilling initiatives

**How to Use:**
1. Check with your HR department
2. Request a tuition sponsorship letter from admissions
3. Provide enrollment verification to employer
4. Follow employer's reimbursement process

*We can provide enrollment verification letters and invoices for employer billing.*

### Veterans Benefits

If approved as a VA-eligible institution, veterans and dependents may use:

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| GI Bill (Chapter 33) | Post-9/11 education benefits |
| Montgomery GI Bill | Active duty or selected reserve |
| Vocational Rehab (Chapter 31) | Veterans with service-connected disabilities |
| VRRAP | Veteran Rapid Retraining |

**Current VA Status:** [Approved/Pending/Not Applicable]

## Important Disclosures

- Federal work-study

### No Institutional Loans

All students pay a non-refundable Student Tuition Recovery Fund (STRF) assessment. See [[STRF Disclosure]].

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I don't qualify for WIOA?**
A: Payment plans are available to all students regardless of income or employment status.

**Q: Can I combine funding sources?**
A: Yes. For example, WIOA may cover tuition while you pay for additional costs out of pocket.

**Q: What if my employer only reimburses after completion?**
A: You can use a payment plan during the program, then pay off the balance upon reimbursement.

**Q: How long is WIOA approval valid?**
A: ITAs typically must be used within 90 days. Start the process 4-6 weeks before your intended start date.
