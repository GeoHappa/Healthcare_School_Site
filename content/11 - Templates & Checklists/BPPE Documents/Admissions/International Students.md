## Foreign Education Credentials

### High School Equivalency

If you completed secondary education outside the U.S.:

**Option 1: Credential Evaluation**
- Obtain evaluation from approved agency
- Must show equivalency to U.S. high school
- Official evaluation required

**Approved Evaluation Agencies:**
- World Education Services (WES)
- Educational Credential Evaluators (ECE)
- Foundation for International Services (FIS)
- Other NACES member organizations

**Cost:** Approximately $100-200 (student responsibility)

**Option 2: GED (if applicable)**
- Take GED in the United States
- Demonstrates high school equivalency

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I enroll on a tourist visa?**
A: No. Tourist/visitor visas (B1/B2) do not permit enrollment in vocational training.

**Q: Do you provide visa sponsorship?**
A: [Answer based on SEVP status - typically No for unaccredited schools]

**Q: Can I work after graduation?**
A: Work eligibility depends on your immigration status, not your education. Consult an immigration attorney.

**Q: Will you evaluate my foreign credentials?**
A: No. You must obtain an independent evaluation from an approved agency.

**Q: Do you have ESL classes?**
A: No. English proficiency is required for enrollment. We do not offer ESL training.

**Q: Can DACA recipients enroll?**
A: Yes, with valid Employment Authorization Document (EAD).
