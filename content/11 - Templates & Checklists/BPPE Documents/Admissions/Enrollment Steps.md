## Step 2: Campus Tour and Admissions Interview

### Campus Tour
During your tour you will see:
- Classrooms and lab facilities
- Equipment and learning resources
- Student services areas
- General campus environment

### Admissions Interview
The interview covers:
- Your educational and career goals
- Program requirements
- Schedule compatibility
- Financial planning
- Your questions

### What to Bring
- Government-issued photo ID
- High school diploma or GED (if available)
- Questions you want answered

### Interview is NOT
- A test of your knowledge
- Intimidating or stressful
- Binding in any way

## Step 4: Verify Eligibility

### General Requirements
All applicants must meet these requirements:

| Requirement | Document Needed |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Age 18 or older | Government-issued ID |
| High school diploma or GED | Diploma, transcript, or GED certificate |
| Valid identification | Driver's license, passport, or state ID |
| English proficiency | Demonstrated in interview |

### Program-Specific Requirements
Some programs have additional requirements:
- Background check eligibility (MA, Phlebotomy, EKG)

## Step 6: Enrollment Appointment

### Before Your Appointment
Gather all required documents:
- [ ] Government-issued photo ID
- [ ] High school diploma or GED certificate
- [ ] Social Security card (or number)
- [ ] Payment or funding documentation
- [ ] Immunization records (if available)

### During Your Appointment

**Review and Sign:**
1. Enrollment Agreement (line by line review)
2. STRF Disclosure acknowledgment
3. Pre-enrollment disclosures
4. Payment plan agreement (if applicable)
5. Technology and conduct policies

**Complete:**
1. Student information form
2. Emergency contact form
3. Photo for student ID (optional)
4. Technology access setup

**Receive:**
1. Copy of all signed documents
2. School Catalog
3. Student ID
4. Class schedule
5. First day instructions

### Time Required
- Approximately 1-2 hours
- Review is thorough - this is for your protection
- Ask questions at any time

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does enrollment take?**
A: The appointment takes 1-2 hours. The entire process from inquiry to enrollment can take 1-7 days.

**Q: Can I start classes the same day I enroll?**
A: Typically no. Classes start on scheduled dates. You'll receive your start date at enrollment.

**Q: What if I don't have my high school diploma?**
A: You can provide official transcripts or GED certificate. If you need to obtain a GED, we can provide resources.

**Q: Can I enroll if I have a criminal record?**
A: Possibly. Discuss your situation with Admissions. Some convictions may affect externship placement.

**Q: What if I need to delay my start?**
A: Contact Admissions to discuss moving to a later start date.
