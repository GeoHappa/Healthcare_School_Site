# Re-Enrollment Policy

---

## Overview

This policy outlines the process and requirements for students who have previously withdrawn or been dismissed and wish to return to [SCHOOL NAME].

---

## Who Needs to Re-Enroll

Re-enrollment is required for:
- Students who voluntarily withdrew
- Students dismissed for academic reasons
- Students dismissed for non-academic reasons
- Students who exceeded maximum time frame
- Students who did not return from Leave of Absence
- Students whose enrollment was cancelled

---

## Re-Enrollment Eligibility

### General Eligibility

| Category | May Re-Enroll? | Conditions |
|----------|---------------|------------|
| Voluntary withdrawal | Yes | Subject to review |
| Academic dismissal | Maybe | After waiting period + appeal |
| Conduct dismissal | Maybe | At Director's discretion |
| Non-payment dismissal | Yes | Upon resolving balance |
| LOA non-return | Yes | Subject to review |
| Time frame exceeded | Yes | New enrollment required |

### Waiting Periods

| Reason for Separation | Minimum Wait |
|----------------------|--------------|
| Voluntary withdrawal | None (may apply immediately) |
| Academic dismissal | 90 days or one term |
| Conduct dismissal | 6 months minimum |
| Multiple dismissals | May not be eligible |

## Re-Enrollment Process

### Step 1: Contact Admissions

Request re-enrollment by:
- Phone: [PHONE]
- Email: [EMAIL]
- In person at campus

### Step 2: Submit Re-Enrollment Application

Complete and submit:
- Re-Enrollment Application Form
- Personal statement explaining:
  - Reason for leaving
  - What has changed
  - Plans for success
  - Commitment to completion

### Step 3: Gather Required Documents

**All Applicants:**
- Current government-issued ID
- Updated contact information
- Previous student ID or enrollment information

**If Applicable:**
- Financial clearance (if previous balance)
- Academic improvement plan (if academic dismissal)
- Documentation of changed circumstances
- Updated health clearance documents

### Step 4: Re-Enrollment Interview

Meet with Program Director to:
- Review academic history
- Discuss circumstances of departure
- Evaluate readiness to return
- Develop success plan
- Determine re-entry point

### Step 5: Receive Decision

Decision provided within 10 business days:
- **Approved** - Proceed with enrollment
- **Approved with Conditions** - Return with specific requirements
- **Denied** - Not eligible at this time
- **Deferred** - May apply again after additional time

## Academic Standing Upon Return

### Previous Academic Record

| Situation | Standing Upon Return |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Left in good standing | Good standing |
| Left on warning | Warning status |
| Academic dismissal (appeal granted) | Probation |
| New enrollment (time frame exceeded) | Good standing |

### Credit for Previous Coursework

**May Receive Credit If:**
- Courses completed with C or better
- Curriculum unchanged
- Within [5] years of original completion
- Skills competencies still valid

**May Need to Repeat If:**
- Curriculum substantially changed
- Extended time since completion
- Skills competencies expired
- At Director's discretion

## Conditions for Re-Enrollment

### Standard Conditions

All re-enrolling students must:
- Sign new enrollment agreement
- Acknowledge current policies
- Complete orientation (if changed)
- Meet current admission requirements

### Academic Probation Return

Students returning after academic dismissal:
- Return on Academic Probation
- Must meet with advisor weekly
- Complete Academic Improvement Plan
- May have limited course load
- Subject to stricter monitoring

### Conduct-Related Return

Students returning after conduct issues:
- May have conditions on behavior
- Subject to immediate dismissal if issues recur
- May require documentation of resolution

## Re-Entry Point

### Where You Start

Determined by:
- Previous coursework completed
- Time elapsed since completion
- Curriculum changes
- Skills competency status

### Options

| Situation | Re-Entry Point |
|-----------|---------------|
| Left recently, good standing | Continue where left off |
| Left mid-course | Start of that course |
| Extended time elapsed | May need to restart |
| Curriculum changed | Determined by Director |
| Skills expired | Skills must be re-demonstrated |

## Health and Background Updates

### Clinical Programs

If returning to a clinical program:
- Updated TB test (within 12 months)
- Current immunizations
- New background check (if expired)
- New drug screen (if required)
- Current CPR certification

### Timeline

Begin gathering these documents early:
- Background checks: 1-2 weeks
- Drug screens: 1-3 days
- TB tests: 2-3 days (or 2 weeks for 2-step)
- Immunization verification: Varies

---

## Denial of Re-Enrollment

### Re-Enrollment May Be Denied If:

- Previous conduct violation was severe
- Academic record shows repeated failures
- Balance owed and not resolved
- Circumstances that led to departure unchanged
- Program no longer offered
- Other factors indicating likely failure

### Appeal of Denial

If re-enrollment is denied:
1. Submit written appeal within 10 business days
2. Include additional information or documentation
3. School Director reviews appeal
4. Decision is final

---

## Statute of Limitations

### Record Retention

- Student records maintained per retention policy
- After many years, records may be archived
- Contact Registrar for historical records

### Extended Absence (5+ Years)

Students returning after 5+ years:
- May need to start as new student
- Previous credits may not apply
- Curriculum likely changed
- New admission process required

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How soon can I re-enroll after withdrawing?**
A: You may apply immediately for voluntary withdrawal. Academic dismissal requires a waiting period.

**Q: Do I have to pay for courses I already completed?**
A: Generally, no. You pay for remaining coursework. Previous payments are applied.

**Q: Will my previous grades be on my transcript?**
A: Yes. All coursework remains on your transcript. New coursework is added.

**Q: Can I transfer to a different program?**
A: Yes, subject to meeting admission requirements for the new program.

**Q: What if my externship site is no longer available?**
A: We will work to place you at an appropriate clinical site.
