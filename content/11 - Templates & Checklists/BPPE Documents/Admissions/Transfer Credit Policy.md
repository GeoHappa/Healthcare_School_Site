# Transfer Credit Policy

---

## Overview

[SCHOOL NAME] may accept transfer credits from other institutions under specific conditions. This policy outlines the requirements and procedures for requesting transfer credit.

---

## Transfer Credit Eligibility

### Maximum Transferable Credits
- Maximum **25%** of total program hours may be transferred
- Applies to all programs

| Program | Total Hours | Max Transfer |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Medical Assistant | 800 hours | 200 hours |
| Phlebotomy | 94 hours | 23 hours |
| Medical Coding | 460 hours | 115 hours |
| EKG Technician | 50 hours | 12 hours |

---

## Requirements for Transfer Credit

### Institution Requirements
Credits must be from:
- Accredited institution (recognized by U.S. Department of Education), OR
- BPPE-approved institution, OR
- Institution approved by equivalent state agency

### Course Requirements
Courses must be:
- Equivalent in content and scope to [SCHOOL NAME] courses
- Completed with grade of "C" (70%) or higher
- Completed within **5 years** of enrollment date
- Documented with official transcripts

### Documentation Required
1. **Official Transcript** - Sent directly from previous institution
2. **Course Descriptions** - Syllabus or catalog descriptions
3. **Clock/Credit Hour Verification** - Documentation of hours completed

---

## Courses NOT Eligible for Transfer

The following cannot be transferred:
- Clinical/externship hours (must be completed at [SCHOOL NAME])
- Courses with grade below "C"
- Courses older than 5 years
- Non-credit continuing education
- Courses from unaccredited institutions (without proper evaluation)

---

## Transfer Credit Evaluation Process

### Step 1: Submit Request
- Complete Transfer Credit Request Form
- Submit official transcripts
- Provide course descriptions/syllabi

### Step 2: Review
- Program Director evaluates course equivalency
- Verification of accreditation status
- Assessment of currency (within 5 years)

### Step 3: Decision
- Written notification within 10 business days
- Credits awarded or denial with explanation
- Appeal process available

### Step 4: Record Update
- Accepted credits posted to student record
- Program length adjusted accordingly
- Tuition adjusted if applicable

---

## Transfer Credit Appeal

If transfer credit is denied:
1. Submit written appeal within 10 business days
2. Provide additional documentation supporting equivalency
3. School Director reviews within 10 business days
4. Decision is final

---

## Effect on Program

### Program Length
- Accepted transfer credits reduce program hours
- Maximum time frame adjusted proportionally
- Must complete remaining hours at [SCHOOL NAME]

### Tuition
- Tuition may be adjusted based on transferred hours
- See [[../Tuition/Tuition Adjustments]] for details
- No refund for credits not used

### Financial Aid
- Transfer credits affect satisfactory academic progress calculations
- Transferred hours count as attempted and completed
- May affect eligibility for certain funding

## Transferability of Credits from [SCHOOL NAME]

### Notice to Students

> **NOTICE CONCERNING TRANSFERABILITY OF CREDITS AND CREDENTIALS EARNED AT OUR INSTITUTION**
>
> The transferability of credits you earn at [SCHOOL NAME] is at the complete discretion of an institution to which you may seek to transfer. Acceptance of the diploma or certificate you earn in your program is also at the complete discretion of the institution to which you may seek to transfer.
>
> If the credits or diploma/certificate that you earn at this institution are not accepted at the institution to which you seek to transfer, you may be required to repeat some or all of your coursework at that institution. For this reason, you should make certain that your attendance at this institution will meet your educational goals. This may include contacting an institution to which you may seek to transfer after attending [SCHOOL NAME] to determine if your credits or diploma/certificate will transfer.

### Potential Transfer Destinations
Students interested in continuing education may consider:
- Community colleges (with assessment)
- Universities (generally require additional coursework)
- Other vocational institutions (case-by-case basis)

*[SCHOOL NAME] does not guarantee transferability to any institution.*

## Prior Learning Assessment

### Experience-Based Credit

Students may request evaluation of:
- Prior work experience in healthcare
- Military training and experience
- Professional certifications
- Continuing education courses

### Requirements
- Documented evidence of learning
- Demonstrated competency
- Relevance to program curriculum
- Program Director approval

### Evaluation Methods
- Portfolio review
- Skills demonstration
- Written examination
- Employer verification

### Limitations
- Maximum 10% of program hours
- No externship credit through PLA
- Subject to same time limitations (5 years)
