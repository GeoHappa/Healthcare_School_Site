# Open Lab Hours

---

## Overview

Open Lab provides students additional time to practice clinical skills outside of scheduled class time. The lab is supervised by an instructor or qualified assistant who can answer questions and provide guidance.

---

## Purpose

Open Lab allows students to:
- Practice skills learned in class
- Prepare for skills checkoffs
- Remediate after unsuccessful skill attempts
- Build confidence before externship
- Get additional help with challenging techniques

## Lab Rules

### General Rules
1. **Sign in/out** - Log attendance at each visit
2. **Clean up** - Return equipment and clean station
3. **Respect equipment** - Report any damage immediately
4. **No food or drink** - Water bottles OK in designated area
5. **Professional behavior** - Maintain professional environment
6. **Phone silence** - Phones on silent, no calls in lab

### Safety Rules
1. Wear appropriate PPE when practicing
2. Dispose of sharps properly (even practice sharps)
3. Follow infection control procedures
4. Report spills or accidents immediately
5. No horseplay

### Equipment Use
1. Use equipment only as trained
2. Do not remove equipment from lab
3. Report malfunctions to supervisor
4. Return supplies to proper location
5. Do not waste disposable supplies

## Supervision

### Supervisor Role
The Open Lab supervisor is available to:
- Answer questions
- Observe technique and provide feedback
- Help troubleshoot problems
- Ensure safety compliance
- Sign off practice hours (if required)

### Supervisor is NOT Available To
- Provide lecture content
- Teach new skills not yet covered in class
- Perform official skills checkoffs

## Practice Guidelines

### Effective Practice

**Before Lab:**
1. Review procedure in textbook/notes
2. Watch instructional videos
3. Identify specific skills to practice
4. Set practice goals

**During Lab:**
1. Practice deliberately (focus on technique)
2. Ask for feedback from supervisor
3. Practice until confident, not just once
4. Use checklists to ensure steps are complete

**After Lab:**
1. Review what you practiced
2. Note areas needing more work
3. Plan next practice session

### Recommended Practice Time

| Skill | Recommended Practice |
|-------|---------------------|
| Venipuncture | 2+ hours before checkoff |
| Injections | 1-2 hours before checkoff |
| EKG | 1-2 hours before checkoff |
| Vital Signs | 1 hour before checkoff |

## Peer Practice

### Guidelines for Practicing on Peers
When practicing on fellow students:
- Obtain verbal consent
- Respect boundaries
- Practice on training equipment first
- Supervisor must be present for certain skills
- Report any injuries immediately

### Skills Requiring Manikins/Arms Only
- Injections (always on pads)
- Venipuncture (on arms unless specific live draw session)

## Tips for Success

1. **Come prepared** - Know what you want to practice
2. **Use checklists** - Practice exactly as you'll be tested
3. **Practice to mastery** - Not just until "good enough"
4. **Ask questions** - Supervisor is there to help
5. **Help peers** - Teaching reinforces learning
6. **Come regularly** - Consistent practice beats cramming

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I have to come to Open Lab?**
A: It's optional for most students, but highly recommended. Required for students on academic improvement plans.

**Q: Can I bring a friend who isn't a student?**
A: No, only enrolled students may use the lab.

**Q: What if I need help and the supervisor is busy?**
A: Sign up for the question queue. Work on independent practice while waiting.

**Q: Can I use Open Lab for studying (not practicing)?**
A: Yes, but priority goes to students actively practicing skills. Study areas are also available outside the lab.
