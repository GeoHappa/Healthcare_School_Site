## Your Right to Access Records

### FERPA Rights

Under FERPA, you have the right to:
- Inspect your education records
- Request copies of records
- Request amendment of inaccurate records
- Control disclosure of records

See [[FERPA Rights]] for complete information.

### How to Request Access

**Step 1:** Submit written request to Registrar
**Step 2:** Specify records you wish to view
**Step 3:** School responds within 45 days
**Step 4:** Schedule appointment to view records

## Updating Your Records

### Contact Information

Keep these current:
- Mailing address
- Phone number
- Email address
- Emergency contacts

**How to Update:**
- Notify Student Services
- Complete Change of Information Form
- Provide documentation (for name changes)

### Name Changes

To change your name on records:
1. Provide legal documentation (marriage certificate, court order)
2. Complete Name Change Form
3. Submit to Registrar
4. Updated on all future documents

### Correcting Errors

If you find an error:
1. Notify Registrar in writing
2. Identify the error specifically
3. Provide correct information
4. Include documentation if applicable

## Confidentiality

### Who Can Access Your Records

**Without Your Consent:**
- School officials with legitimate educational interest
- Auditors and accreditors
- Financial aid officials
- Legal requirements (subpoena, court order)
- Health/safety emergencies

**With Your Consent:**
- Employers
- Other schools
- Parents (with written consent)
- Anyone you authorize

### Protecting Your Information

We protect your records through:
- Secure storage (physical and electronic)
- Access controls
- Staff training
- Disposal procedures

## Record Retention

### How Long Records Are Kept

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|-------------|------------------|
| Transcripts | Permanent |
| Enrollment agreements | 5 years after last attendance |
| Financial records | 5 years |
| Attendance records | 5 years |
| Grade documentation | 5 years |
| Clinical documentation | 5 years |
| Disciplinary records | 5 years |

### After Retention Period

- Records may be destroyed
- Destruction is secure (shredding, digital wiping)
- Transcripts always maintained

## Directory Information

### What is Directory Information

Basic information that may be disclosed without consent:
- Name
- Program of study
- Dates of attendance
- Enrollment status
- Credentials earned
- Honors/awards

### Opting Out

You may restrict directory information disclosure:
- Submit FERPA Opt-Out Form
- Effective until you change it
- Does not affect required disclosures

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How do I get a copy of my transcript?**
A: Complete a Transcript Request Form and pay the fee. See [[Transcripts]].

**Q: Can my parents access my records?**
A: Only with your written consent (you're an adult student).

**Q: How long do you keep records?**
A: Transcripts are permanent. Other records kept 5 years after last attendance.

**Q: Can I get records from years ago?**
A: Transcripts are always available. Other records depend on retention period.

**Q: What if I find an error in my records?**
A: Submit a written correction request to the Registrar with documentation.
