## Scheduling an Appointment

### How to Schedule
1. Contact Student Services or instructor
2. Specify reason for advising
3. Provide available times
4. Confirmation sent within 24 hours

### Appointment Types

| Type | Duration | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| Quick Check | 15 min | Single question or concern |
| Standard | 30 min | Progress review, planning |
| Extended | 60 min | Complex issues, improvement plans |

### Preparation
Bring to your appointment:
- Current grade information
- Specific questions or concerns
- Calendar for scheduling
- Any relevant documents

## Academic Improvement Planning

For students needing additional support, advisors help develop:

### Improvement Plan Components
1. **Assessment** - Identify specific challenges
2. **Goals** - Set measurable objectives
3. **Strategies** - Action steps to improve
4. **Resources** - Support services to utilize
5. **Timeline** - Milestones and check-ins
6. **Monitoring** - Progress tracking method

### Sample Goals
- Raise quiz average from 68% to 75% in 2 weeks
- Improve attendance to 90%+ this month
- Complete 4 hours of tutoring weekly
- Practice skills in Open Lab twice weekly

To get the most from advising:

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I meet with an advisor?**
A: At minimum, once before externship and once before graduation. More often if you have concerns.

**Q: Can I change advisors?**
A: Advising is typically provided by Student Services and your Program Director. If you have concerns, speak with the School Director.

**Q: Is advising mandatory?**
A: It's optional for students in good standing, but mandatory for those on warning or probation.

**Q: What if I disagree with my advisor's guidance?**
A: You can seek a second opinion from the Program Director or discuss concerns through the grievance process.
