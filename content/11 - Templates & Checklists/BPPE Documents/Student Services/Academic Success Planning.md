# Academic Success Planning

---

## Overview

Academic Success Planning at [SCHOOL NAME] provides structured support for students to develop strategies, set goals, and overcome challenges to achieve academic success.

---

## What is Success Planning?

Academic success planning involves:
- Assessing current academic standing
- Identifying strengths and challenges
- Setting realistic goals
- Developing action plans
- Monitoring progress
- Adjusting strategies as needed

---

## When Success Planning is Used

### Proactive Planning

Available to all students who want to:
- Maximize their academic performance
- Develop better study habits
- Plan for challenging courses
- Balance school with other responsibilities

### Required Planning

May be required when:
- GPA falls below 2.0
- Placed on [[Academic Warning]] or [[Academic Probation]]
- Returning from [[Leave of Absence]]
- [[Reinstatement Process|Reinstating]] after dismissal
- Experiencing academic difficulties

---

## Success Planning Process

### Step 1: Initial Assessment

Review of:
- Current grades and GPA
- Attendance record
- Study habits and time management
- Learning challenges
- External factors affecting academics

### Step 2: Goal Setting

Develop SMART goals:
- **S**pecific - What exactly will you achieve?
- **M**easurable - How will you track progress?
- **A**chievable - Is this realistic?
- **R**elevant - Does this align with your program goals?
- **T**ime-bound - When will you achieve this?

### Step 3: Action Plan Development

Create specific strategies:

| Goal | Actions | Resources | Timeline |
|------|---------|-----------|----------|
| Improve test scores | Attend tutoring, use study guides | [[Tutoring Services]] | Weekly |
| Better attendance | Set multiple alarms, arrange backup transport | Student Services | Daily |
| Complete assignments | Schedule study blocks, use planner | [[Study Resources]] | Weekly |

### Step 4: Implementation

Put the plan into action:
- Follow action steps consistently
- Use identified resources
- Track progress weekly
- Note what works and what doesn't

### Step 5: Progress Monitoring

Regular check-ins to:
- Review progress toward goals
- Celebrate successes
- Identify continuing challenges
- Adjust strategies as needed

## Study Strategies

### Time Management

- Create weekly schedule
- Block study time daily
- Prioritize difficult subjects
- Avoid procrastination
- Take regular breaks

### Active Learning

- Take notes during class
- Review notes same day
- Create study guides
- Practice skills regularly
- Teach concepts to others

### Test Preparation

- Review material regularly (not just before exams)
- Use practice tests
- Understand formats and expectations
- Get adequate sleep
- Arrive early and prepared

### Managing Challenges

| Challenge | Strategies |
|-----------|------------|
| Work-school balance | Communicate with employer, plan ahead |
| Family responsibilities | Create support network, schedule flexibility |
| Learning difficulties | Request [[ADA Accommodations Policy|accommodations]], use tutoring |
| Stress/anxiety | Use stress management techniques, seek support |

## When You're Struggling

### Warning Signs

- Missing multiple classes
- Falling behind on assignments
- Failing grades on exams
- Feeling overwhelmed
- Considering dropping out

### What to Do

1. **Don't wait** - Seek help immediately
2. **Talk to someone** - Advisor, instructor, Student Services
3. **Be honest** - About challenges you're facing
4. **Ask for help** - Use available resources
5. **Adjust your plan** - Modify strategies as needed
