## Common Healthcare Interview Questions

### About You
- "Tell me about yourself."
- "Why did you choose healthcare?"
- "Why this specific role?"
- "What are your strengths and weaknesses?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

### Clinical/Technical
- "Describe your clinical training experience."
- "How many venipunctures did you perform?"
- "What EMR systems have you used?"
- "Walk me through [procedure]."
- "How do you handle difficult sticks?"

### Situational/Behavioral
- "Tell me about a time you dealt with a difficult patient."
- "How do you handle stressful situations?"
- "Describe a time you made a mistake. How did you handle it?"
- "How do you prioritize multiple tasks?"
- "Give an example of working as part of a team."

### Compliance/Ethics
- "How do you protect patient confidentiality?"
- "What would you do if you saw a coworker violating HIPAA?"
- "How do you handle a patient who refuses treatment?"
- "What does professionalism mean to you?"

## STAR Method for Behavioral Questions

Use the STAR method to structure your answers:

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **S**ituation | Set the scene | "During my clinical rotation..." |
| **T**ask | Explain your role | "I was responsible for..." |
| **A**ction | Describe what you did | "I approached the situation by..." |
| **R**esult | Share the outcome | "As a result, the patient..." |

### Example STAR Answer

**Question:** "Tell me about a time you dealt with a difficult patient."

**Answer:**
- **Situation:** "During my externship at [Clinic], an elderly patient was anxious about having blood drawn."
- **Task:** "As the phlebotomy extern, I needed to complete the draw while ensuring patient comfort."
- **Action:** "I took extra time to explain each step, used distraction techniques, and made sure she felt in control by letting her choose which arm."
- **Result:** "The patient relaxed, I completed a successful draw, and she thanked me for my patience. The supervisor noted my bedside manner in my evaluation."

## Professional Presentation

### Dress Code for Interviews

**Recommended:**
- Clean, pressed scrubs OR business casual
- Closed-toe shoes
- Minimal jewelry
- Natural makeup
- Clean, neat hair
- No strong fragrances

**Avoid:**
- Casual clothing (jeans, t-shirts)
- Excessive jewelry or piercings
- Strong perfume/cologne
- Wrinkled or stained clothing
- Open-toe shoes

### Body Language

**Positive Signals:**
- Firm handshake
- Eye contact
- Good posture
- Genuine smile
- Nodding to show understanding
- Facing interviewer

**Avoid:**
- Crossed arms
- Looking at phone
- Fidgeting
- Slouching
- Avoiding eye contact
- Interrupting

## Before the Interview

### Research the Employer
- Company website and mission
- Services offered
- Recent news
- Size and locations
- Patient population

### Prepare Your Materials
- Multiple copies of resume
- List of references
- Certifications/licenses (copies)
- Portfolio (if applicable)
- Notepad and pen

### Plan Logistics
- Know exact location
- Arrive 10-15 minutes early
- Have contact phone number
- Plan parking
- Allow extra travel time

### Prepare Questions to Ask
- "What does a typical day look like?"
- "How is the training/orientation structured?"
- "What qualities are you looking for in this role?"
- "What are the opportunities for growth?"
- "What do you enjoy about working here?"

---

## During the Interview

### First Impressions
- Arrive early (10-15 minutes)
- Be polite to everyone you meet
- Turn off phone completely
- Firm handshake, eye contact, smile

### During Questions
- Listen carefully to each question
- Take a moment to think before answering
- Be honest and specific
- Use examples from training/clinical experience
- Keep answers focused (1-2 minutes)

### Closing
- Ask your prepared questions
- Express enthusiasm for the position
- Ask about next steps
- Thank interviewer by name
- Shake hands upon leaving

---

## After the Interview

### Same Day
- Send thank-you email within 24 hours
- Personalize for each interviewer
- Reiterate interest and fit
- Reference something discussed

### Thank You Email Template

```
Subject: Thank You - [Position] Interview

Dear [Interviewer Name],

Thank you for taking the time to meet with me today about
the [Position] role at [Company]. I enjoyed learning about
[specific detail discussed] and am excited about the
opportunity to contribute to your team.

Our conversation reinforced my interest in this position,
particularly [mention something specific]. My experience
in [relevant skill/experience] would allow me to [contribute].

Please don't hesitate to contact me if you need any
additional information. I look forward to hearing from you.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
```

### Follow-Up
- Wait at least 1 week before following up
- One follow-up email is appropriate
- Continue applying to other positions

## Virtual Interview Tips

### Technical Preparation
- Test camera and microphone
- Ensure stable internet connection
- Download required software in advance
- Have phone number backup ready

### Environment
- Quiet, private location
- Professional background
- Good lighting (face the light source)
- Remove distractions

### During Virtual Interview
- Look at camera (not screen)
- Speak clearly, slight pause before responding
- Have notes nearby (but don't read from them)
- Dress professionally (full outfit, just in case)
