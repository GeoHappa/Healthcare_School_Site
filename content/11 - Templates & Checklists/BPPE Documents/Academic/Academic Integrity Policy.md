# Academic Integrity Policy

---

## Purpose

Academic integrity is fundamental to the educational mission of [SCHOOL NAME]. This policy establishes standards for honest academic conduct and outlines consequences for violations.

---

## Core Values

[SCHOOL NAME] expects all students to:
- Complete their own work
- Give proper credit to others' ideas
- Follow examination rules
- Report academic dishonesty
- Uphold professional standards

---

## Definitions

### Cheating
Using or attempting to use unauthorized materials, information, or assistance in any academic exercise.

**Examples:**
- Copying from another student during exams
- Using unauthorized notes or devices during tests
- Having someone else complete your work
- Sharing answers during individual assessments
- Using exam materials from previous terms

### Plagiarism
Presenting another's words, ideas, or work as your own without proper attribution.

**Examples:**
- Copying text without quotation marks and citation
- Paraphrasing without attribution
- Submitting purchased or downloaded papers
- Using AI-generated content without disclosure
- Copying from websites without citation

### Fabrication
Falsifying or inventing information or citations.

**Examples:**
- Making up data or research findings
- Creating fake clinical hours or signatures
- Falsifying attendance records
- Inventing sources or citations
- Altering documents or grades

### Facilitation
Helping another student commit academic dishonesty.

**Examples:**
- Allowing others to copy your work
- Sharing exam questions or answers
- Writing papers for others
- Taking exams for another student
- Covering for another's absence

### Other Violations
- Multiple submission of same work without permission
- Unauthorized collaboration
- Bribery or threats related to grades
- Theft of academic materials
- Sabotaging others' work

---

## Student Responsibilities

1. **Understand expectations** - Know what constitutes academic dishonesty
2. **Ask when uncertain** - Clarify with instructor before acting
3. **Complete own work** - Do not share or copy assignments
4. **Cite sources** - Give credit for others' ideas
5. **Secure your work** - Do not leave work accessible to others
6. **Report violations** - Report suspected dishonesty

---

## Instructor Responsibilities

1. Clearly communicate expectations
2. Design assessments that minimize cheating opportunities
3. Monitor examinations appropriately
4. Report all suspected violations
5. Maintain confidentiality during investigation
6. Apply consequences consistently

---

## Examination Rules

### During Exams
- No electronic devices (phones, watches, etc.)
- No notes unless specifically authorized
- No communication with other students
- Eyes on own paper
- Raise hand for questions
- Remain until dismissed

### Violations During Exams
- Exam may be collected immediately
- Student may be asked to leave
- Grade of zero may be assigned
- Additional sanctions may apply

---

## Reporting Violations

### Who May Report
- Instructors
- Staff members
- Students
- Clinical site personnel

### How to Report
1. Report to instructor or Program Director
2. Provide specific details and evidence
3. Maintain confidentiality
4. Cooperate with investigation

---

## Investigation Process

### Step 1: Initial Report
- Instructor documents suspected violation
- Evidence collected and preserved
- Student notified of allegation

### Step 2: Meeting with Student
- Student informed of specific allegation
- Student given opportunity to respond
- Student may bring support person (non-attorney)

### Step 3: Determination
- Program Director reviews evidence
- Determines if violation occurred
- Decides appropriate sanction

### Step 4: Notification
- Written decision provided to student
- Includes findings and sanctions
- Includes appeal information

---

## Sanctions

### First Offense (Minor)
- Written warning
- Zero on assignment
- Required integrity workshop
- Academic probation

### First Offense (Major) or Second Offense
- Failing grade in course/module
- Academic probation
- Suspension
- Dismissal from program

### Factors Considered
- Severity of violation
- Student's intent
- Prior violations
- Impact on others
- Student's response and honesty

---

## Sanction Guidelines

| Violation Type | Typical First Offense | Typical Repeat Offense |
|----------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Unauthorized collaboration | Warning + zero | Failing grade |
| Copying on quiz | Zero on quiz | Failing grade + probation |
| Cheating on exam | Failing grade | Dismissal |
| Plagiarism (minor) | Zero + rewrite | Failing grade |
| Plagiarism (major) | Failing grade | Dismissal |
| Fabrication | Failing grade + probation | Dismissal |
| Falsifying clinical hours | Dismissal | N/A |

---

## Appeal Process

### Grounds for Appeal
- Procedural error in investigation
- New evidence not previously available
- Sanction disproportionate to offense
- Factual error in determination

### Appeal Procedure
1. Submit written appeal within 5 business days
2. Include grounds for appeal and supporting evidence
3. School Director reviews appeal
4. Decision issued within 10 business days
5. Decision is final
