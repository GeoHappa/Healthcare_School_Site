## Site Supervisor Evaluation

### Evaluation Criteria

Supervisors evaluate students on:

| Category | Areas Assessed |
|----------|----------------|
| Clinical Skills | Technical competence, accuracy, efficiency |
| Patient Care | Communication, bedside manner, safety |
| Professionalism | Appearance, attitude, reliability |
| Work Habits | Organization, time management, initiative |
| Communication | With patients, staff, and supervisors |
| Adaptability | Flexibility, handling feedback, learning |

### Rating Scale

| Rating | Description | Points |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Excellent | Exceeds expectations consistently | 4 |
| Good | Meets expectations consistently | 3 |
| Satisfactory | Meets most expectations | 2 |
| Needs Improvement | Below expectations | 1 |
| Unsatisfactory | Does not meet expectations | 0 |

### Evaluation Schedule

| Evaluation | Timing |
|------------|--------|
| Midpoint | After 50% of hours completed |
| Final | At completion of all hours |

## Attendance Grading

### Requirements

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Attendance | 100% of scheduled hours |
| Punctuality | On time for all shifts |
| Notification | Absences reported immediately |

### Grading Impact

| Attendance | Grade Impact |
|------------|--------------|
| 100% | Full points |
| 95-99% | Slight deduction |
| 90-94% | Moderate deduction |
| Below 90% | May require extended placement |

### Absence Handling

- All absences must be made up
- Excused absences made up without penalty
- Unexcused absences affect grade
- Contact Clinical Coordinator immediately

## Incomplete Grades

### When Assigned

Incomplete (I) grade given when:
- Hours not completed due to documented circumstances
- Skills competencies not fully demonstrated
- Site removal requiring reassignment

### Resolving Incomplete

- Must complete requirements within [X] weeks
- Work with Clinical Coordinator
- Failure to complete = Failing grade

---

## Site Removal and Reassignment

### Reasons for Removal

Student may be removed from site for:
- Unprofessional conduct
- Attendance/punctuality issues
- Skills performance concerns
- Safety violations
- Site request

### Reassignment Process

1. Meet with Clinical Coordinator
2. Discuss reasons for removal
3. Determine eligibility for reassignment
4. Assign to new site (if eligible)
5. Previous hours may count (case by case)

### Impact on Grade

- First reassignment: May proceed with grade impact
- Second reassignment: Program Director review
- May result in failure of externship

- Supervisor signature required

## Feedback and Improvement

### Midpoint Review

At midpoint:
- Review supervisor evaluation
- Discuss performance
- Identify areas for improvement
- Set goals for remainder

### Addressing Concerns

If performance concerns arise:
1. Clinical Coordinator contacts site
2. Student meeting held
3. Improvement plan developed
4. Progress monitored
5. Additional support provided

## Externship as Extended Interview

### Site Hiring

Many students receive job offers from their externship sites.

**To Maximize Opportunity:**
- Treat externship as extended interview
- Demonstrate excellent work ethic
- Show willingness to learn
- Be reliable and punctual
- Express interest in employment

### No Guarantee

Completing externship at a site does not guarantee employment.

## Appeals

### Grade Appeals

If you disagree with your externship grade:
1. Review evaluation with Clinical Coordinator
2. Discuss concerns
3. Request meeting with supervisor (if appropriate)
4. File formal appeal if unresolved
