# Retake Policy

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes procedures for students who need to retake examinations or repeat courses/modules due to failing grades.

---

## Examination Retakes

### Eligibility
Students may retake an exam when:
- Original score is below 70% (failing)
- First or second attempt on the exam
- Retake scheduled within allowed timeframe

### Retake Limits

| Exam Type | Maximum Attempts | Timeframe |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| Quizzes | 2 total | Within 1 week |
| Module Exams | 2 total | Within 2 weeks |
| Final Exams | 2 total | Before program completion |
| Skills Competencies | 3 total | Before program completion |

### Retake Procedures

1. **Request retake** from instructor within 48 hours of receiving grade
2. **Complete remediation** (review material, tutoring, additional practice)
3. **Schedule retake** with instructor or Testing Center
4. **Take retake exam** within specified timeframe

### Retake Exam Format
- May be different version from original
- Same difficulty level and content coverage
- Same time limit
- Proctored environment required

### Grading of Retakes

| Policy Option | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Option A** | Higher of two scores recorded |
| **Option B** | Maximum retake score capped at 70% |
| **Option C** | Average of original and retake |

## Skills Competency Retakes

### Required Competencies
All students must demonstrate proficiency in required clinical skills.

### Failure to Pass
If a student fails a skills competency:

1. **First Failure**
   - Instructor provides feedback
   - Student schedules additional practice
   - Retake within 1 week

2. **Second Failure**
   - Meet with Program Director
   - Develop remediation plan
   - Additional lab practice required
   - Retake within 2 weeks

3. **Third Failure**
   - May result in course failure
   - Program Director determines next steps
   - May require repeating module

## Course/Module Repeats

### When Required
A student must repeat a course or module when:
- Final grade below 70%
- All exam retake attempts exhausted
- Skills competencies not passed
- Excessive absences requiring restart

### Repeat Procedures

1. Meet with Program Director
2. Determine appropriate re-entry point
3. Complete Course Repeat Form
4. Pay applicable fees (if any)
5. Re-enroll in next available session

### Repeat Fees

| Situation | Fee |
|-----------|-----|
| First repeat | [$ or No charge] |
| Second repeat of same course | $_______ |
| Repeating due to attendance | $_______ |

### Repeat Limits
- Maximum 2 attempts per course/module
- Second failure may result in program dismissal
- See [[Dismissal and Reinstatement Policy]]

---

## Impact on Progress

### Academic Standing
- Failed exams count toward SAP evaluation
- Retake scores may affect GPA
- Multiple failures lead to [[Academic Warning]] or [[Academic Probation]]

### Program Timeline
- Retakes should not extend program length
- Course repeats will extend completion time
- Maximum timeframe: 150% of program length

### Financial Impact
- No additional tuition for exam retakes
- Course repeats may incur fees
- WIOA: Contact workforce board about extensions

---

## Externship Readiness

Students must pass all coursework before externship:
- All exams passed (70% minimum)
- All skills competencies passed
- Attendance requirements met
- Good academic standing

**No retakes available during externship.**

---

## Special Circumstances

### Testing Accommodations
Students with documented disabilities:
- Same retake rights apply
- Accommodations provided per [[Disability Services (ADA)]]
- Extended time or modified format as approved

### Extenuating Circumstances
Students who fail due to documented emergencies:
- May petition for additional attempt
- Must provide documentation
- Program Director approval required

---

## Remediation Requirements

Before retaking exams, students should:

### For Written Exams
- Review lecture notes and textbook
- Complete practice questions
- Attend tutoring session
- Meet with instructor for review

### For Skills Competencies
- Additional lab practice (minimum 2 hours)
- Instructor demonstration review
- Peer practice (observed)
- Video review (if available)

---

## Documentation

All retakes are documented:
- Original and retake scores recorded
- Remediation activities noted
- Signed by student and instructor
- Maintained in student file

---

## Appeals

Students may appeal retake decisions by:
1. Submitting written appeal within 5 business days
2. Explaining circumstances and request
3. Meeting with Program Director
4. See [[Grade Appeals]] for complete process

---

## Best Practices for Success

1. **Don't wait** - Request retake promptly
2. **Get help** - Use tutoring and office hours
3. **Identify gaps** - Review missed questions
4. **Practice differently** - Try new study methods
5. **Ask questions** - Clarify confusing concepts
6. **Practice skills** - Extra lab time for competencies
