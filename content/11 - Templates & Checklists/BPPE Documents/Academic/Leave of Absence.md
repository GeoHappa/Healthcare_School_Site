# Leave of Absence Policy

---

## Purpose

A Leave of Absence (LOA) allows students to temporarily suspend their enrollment due to documented circumstances beyond their control, without being considered withdrawn from the program.

---

## Eligibility

Students may request a Leave of Absence for:

### Approved Reasons
- Medical emergency or serious illness (self or immediate family)
- Military duty
- Jury duty
- Death of immediate family member
- Other documented emergencies approved by the Director

### Requirements
- Student must be in good academic standing
- Student must be current on financial obligations
- Request must be submitted in writing
- Supporting documentation required

---

## LOA Limitations

| Limitation | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| Maximum Duration | 180 calendar days in a 12-month period |
| Number of LOAs | No more than 2 per enrollment period |
| Minimum Enrollment | Must have completed at least 25% of program |
| Return Date | Must be scheduled before LOA begins |

---

## Request Process

### Step 1: Submit Written Request
Complete the Leave of Absence Request Form including:
- Reason for leave
- Requested start date
- Expected return date
- Contact information during leave

### Step 2: Provide Documentation
Submit supporting documentation such as:
- Medical documentation (physician's note)
- Military orders
- Jury summons
- Death certificate or obituary
- Other relevant documentation

### Step 3: Meet with Program Director
- Review request and documentation
- Discuss impact on academic progress
- Confirm return date
- Sign LOA agreement

### Step 4: Receive Approval
- Written approval provided
- Return date confirmed
- Re-entry requirements outlined

---

## During Leave of Absence

### Student Responsibilities
- Maintain contact with school monthly
- Notify school of any changes to return date
- Notify school if circumstances change
- Return all borrowed materials (if applicable)

### School Responsibilities
- Hold student's place in program
- Maintain student records
- Not charge additional tuition during approved LOA
- Provide re-entry support

---

## Financial Implications

### During LOA
- No additional tuition charges during approved leave
- Payment plan payments may be paused (with approval)
- WIOA funding status maintained (contact workforce board)

### Failure to Return
If student does not return on scheduled date:
- Withdrawal date = last date of attendance
- Refund calculated per [[Refund Policy]]
- Balance due becomes immediately payable
- Re-enrollment may be required

---

## Returning from LOA

### Re-Entry Requirements
- Contact school 2 weeks before return date
- Complete re-entry paperwork
- Meet with instructor to review curriculum
- Update any expired health documentation
- Resume payment plan (if applicable)

### Academic Continuation
- Student continues from point of departure
- No re-enrollment fee
- Same program version (unless curriculum change)
- Previous grades and hours maintained

---

## Extending a Leave of Absence

Extensions may be granted if:
- Total LOA does not exceed 180 days
- Documentation supports continued need
- Request submitted before original return date
- Director approval obtained

**To Request Extension:**
1. Submit extension request in writing
2. Provide updated documentation
3. Propose new return date
4. Receive written approval

---

## Denial of LOA

A Leave of Absence may be denied if:
- Student is not in good academic standing
- Student has outstanding financial balance
- Maximum LOA time already used
- Insufficient documentation provided
- Request made after absence began

**Appeal Process:**
Students may appeal a denial by submitting a written appeal to the School Director within 5 business days.

---

## Withdrawal vs. Leave of Absence

| Factor | Leave of Absence | Withdrawal |
|--------|------------------|------------|
| Duration | Up to 180 days | Permanent |
| Return | Guaranteed spot | Must re-apply |
| Refund | None (paused) | Pro-rata calculation |
| Transcript | "LOA" notation | "W" notation |
| Re-entry | Same program | New enrollment |

---

## Emergency Leave

In cases of sudden emergency:
- Contact school immediately (within 48 hours if possible)
- Verbal request accepted initially
- Written request and documentation due within 10 days
- Retroactive approval may be granted

---

## Military Leave

Students called to active military duty:
- Entitled to LOA for duration of service
- 180-day limit may be extended for military
- Protected under federal law
- Must provide military orders
- Return within reasonable time after service ends
