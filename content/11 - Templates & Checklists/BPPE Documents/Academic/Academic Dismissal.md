# Academic Dismissal

---

## Overview

Academic dismissal is the termination of a student's enrollment due to failure to meet academic standards or serious policy violations. This page explains the dismissal process, consequences, and options available.

---

## Grounds for Dismissal

### Academic Grounds

| Reason | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| GPA failure | Cumulative GPA below 2.0 after probation |
| SAP failure | Failure to meet SAP after probation period |
| Course failure | Failing a required course twice |
| Attendance | Falling below 80% after warning/probation |
| Maximum time frame | Exceeding 150% of program length |
| Skills failure | Inability to pass required competencies |

### Conduct Grounds

| Reason | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Severe violation | Single serious policy violation |
| Repeated violations | Pattern of misconduct |
| Clinical removal | Dismissal from clinical site for cause |
| Safety concerns | Actions endangering others |
| Academic integrity | Cheating, plagiarism, falsification |

See [[Student Conduct Policy]] for conduct violations.

---

## Process Before Dismissal

### Academic Issues

Students typically progress through:

1. **Good Standing** - Meeting all requirements
2. **[[Academic Warning]]** - First evaluation period below standards
3. **[[Academic Probation]]** - Continued issues, improvement plan required
4. **Dismissal** - Failure to improve after probation

### Conduct Issues

For serious violations:
- Investigation conducted
- Student notified and given opportunity to respond
- Determination made
- Dismissal if warranted

## Notification

### How You'll Be Notified

- Written notification via:
  - Email to student email address
  - Certified mail to address on file
  - In-person meeting (when possible)

### What Notification Includes

- Reason for dismissal
- Effective date
- Impact on records and financials
- Appeal rights and deadline
- Contact information for questions

---

## Effective Date

### When Dismissal Takes Effect

- Typically effective immediately upon notification
- Or specified future date
- Academic work not accepted after effective date

### What Happens Immediately

- Access to facilities suspended
- Student portal access may continue briefly for records
- Must return school property
- Exit meeting may be scheduled

## Appealing Dismissal

### Right to Appeal

Students may appeal dismissal:
- Within 5 business days of notification
- Written appeal required
- Must state grounds for appeal

### Grounds for Appeal

- Procedural error in dismissal process
- New evidence not previously available
- Extenuating circumstances not considered
- Error in academic records

### Appeal Process

1. **Submit Appeal**
   - Written statement to School Director
   - Include all supporting documentation
   - Meet deadline

2. **Review**
   - Appeal reviewed by Director (or designee)
   - May include meeting with student
   - Review of all relevant records

3. **Decision**
   - Written decision within [TIMEFRAME]
   - May uphold, modify, or reverse dismissal
   - Decision is final

See [[Grievance Procedure]] for formal process.

## Reinstatement Eligibility

### May Apply for Reinstatement If

- Waiting period completed (typically one semester/term)
- Circumstances that led to dismissal resolved
- Demonstrated readiness to succeed
- Space available in program

### Not Eligible If

- Dismissed for severe conduct violation
- Already reinstated once before
- Cannot complete within maximum timeframe
- Other disqualifying factors

See [[Reinstatement Process]] for complete requirements.
