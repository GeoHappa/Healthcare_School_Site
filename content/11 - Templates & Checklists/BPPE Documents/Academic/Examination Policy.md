# Examination Policy

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes rules and procedures for all examinations to ensure fair, consistent, and secure testing conditions.

---

## Types of Examinations

| Exam Type | Description | Typical Length |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Quiz | Brief assessment of recent material | 15-30 minutes |
| Module Exam | Comprehensive assessment of module | 60-90 minutes |
| Midterm Exam | Mid-program cumulative assessment | 90-120 minutes |
| Final Exam | Program-ending comprehensive exam | 120-180 minutes |
| Skills Practical | Hands-on demonstration of competencies | 30-60 minutes |

---

## Exam Schedule

### Notification
- Exam dates listed in course syllabus
- Reminder given at least 1 week in advance
- Final exam schedule provided at start of program

### Timing
- Exams given during scheduled class time
- Start and end times strictly enforced
- Late arrivals may not receive extra time

---

## Before the Exam

### Student Responsibilities

**Preparation:**
- Attend all classes and complete assignments
- Review study materials
- Get adequate rest before exam day
- Arrive on time

**What to Bring:**
- Government-issued photo ID
- Two sharpened #2 pencils
- Approved calculator (if allowed)
- Required materials specified by instructor

**Leave at Home/in Locker:**
- Cell phones and smart watches
- Notes, textbooks (unless open-book)
- Bags, purses, backpacks
- Food and drinks (except water in clear container)

---

## During the Exam

### General Rules

1. **No Talking**
   - No communication with other students
   - Raise hand for questions to instructor

2. **No Electronic Devices**
   - Cell phones must be OFF and stored away
   - Smart watches, earbuds, etc. prohibited
   - Calculators only if specifically allowed

3. **Eyes on Your Paper**
   - Looking at others' papers is cheating
   - Cover your answers

4. **No Unauthorized Materials**
   - Only approved materials at desk
   - Desks cleared before exam starts

5. **Remain Seated**
   - Raise hand for restroom (at instructor discretion)
   - Submit exam if leaving room
   - Return promptly if break allowed

6. **Time Management**
   - Note time limits
   - Pace yourself
   - No extra time for late starts

### Restroom Policy
- Attempt before exam starts
- Limited breaks during exam
- May not be allowed during final portion
- Instructor may accompany or limit access

---

## Exam Security

### Exam Materials
- Exams are confidential documents
- Do not copy, photograph, or remove exams
- All materials returned at end of exam
- Scratch paper collected

### Multiple Versions
- Instructors may use multiple exam versions
- Random seating may be assigned
- Do not discuss exam content after testing

### Computer-Based Testing
- No screenshots or photos
- No use of other applications
- Browser lockdown may be used
- Activity may be monitored

---

## Prohibited Conduct

The following are prohibited during exams:

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Talking to other students | Warning or exam confiscated |
| Using unauthorized materials | Zero on exam |
| Looking at another's paper | Zero on exam |
| Cell phone use | Zero on exam |
| Leaving without permission | Zero on exam |
| Removing exam materials | Zero + academic integrity violation |
| Sharing exam content | Academic integrity violation |
| Having someone else take exam | Dismissal from program |

See [[Academic Integrity Policy]] for complete consequences.

---

## Exam Day Procedures

### Arrival
1. Arrive 5-10 minutes early
2. Store belongings in designated area
3. Use restroom before exam
4. Take assigned seat (if applicable)
5. Clear desk of all materials

### Distribution
1. Wait for instructions
2. Do not open exam until instructed
3. Write name on exam immediately
4. Listen to any verbal instructions

### During Exam
1. Read all instructions carefully
2. Manage time wisely
3. Answer questions you know first
4. Review answers if time permits
5. Raise hand for questions

### Submission
1. Stop writing when time is called
2. Remain seated until dismissed
3. Submit exam to instructor
4. Ensure all materials returned
5. Leave quietly

---

## Late Arrivals

### Policy
- Late students may be admitted at instructor discretion
- No extra time given for late arrival
- May be denied entry after certain point
- Must take exam during make-up period if denied

### Chronic Lateness
- Pattern of late arrivals noted
- May affect participation grade
- Discuss concerns with instructor

---

## Absences

### Excused Absence
If you cannot attend an exam due to documented emergency:
1. Notify instructor BEFORE exam if possible
2. Provide documentation within 48 hours
3. Schedule make-up exam within 1 week
4. See [[Make-Up Policy]]

### Unexcused Absence
- Zero grade assigned
- Make-up at instructor discretion
- May impact course grade significantly

---

## Make-Up Exams

### Eligibility
Make-up exams available for:
- Documented illness
- Family emergency
- Court appearance/jury duty
- Other documented circumstances

### Procedure
1. Contact instructor immediately
2. Provide documentation
3. Schedule make-up within 1 week
4. Exam may be different version

See [[Make-Up Policy]] for complete details.

---

## Retakes

### When Available
- Score below 70%
- Within specified timeframe
- After completing remediation

### Limits
- Maximum attempts per [[Retake Policy]]
- Remediation required between attempts
- May be different version

---

## Accommodations

### Available Accommodations
- Extended time (typically 1.5x or 2x)
- Separate testing location
- Reader or scribe
- Modified format (large print, etc.)
- Assistive technology

### Request Process
1. Provide documentation to Student Services
2. Complete accommodation request form
3. Receive accommodation letter
4. Present letter to instructor at least 3 days before exam
5. Coordinate arrangements

See [[Disability Services (ADA)]] for complete process.

---

## Grading

### Turnaround Time
- Quizzes: Within 24-48 hours
- Module exams: Within 1 week
- Final exams: Within 2 weeks

### Reviewing Exams
- May review exam with instructor
- Schedule during office hours
- Cannot keep or copy exam
- Discuss incorrect answers

### Grade Appeals
If you believe exam was graded unfairly:
1. Review with instructor first
2. File formal appeal if unresolved
3. See [[Grade Appeals]]
