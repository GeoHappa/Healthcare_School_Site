# Cheating Consequences

---

## Overview

Academic dishonesty undermines the integrity of education and violates the trust between students and the institution. This document outlines the consequences for cheating and other forms of academic dishonesty.

---

## What Constitutes Cheating

See [[Academic Integrity Policy]] for complete definitions. Examples include:

- Copying from another student's exam
- Using unauthorized materials during tests
- Sharing answers during individual assessments
- Having someone else complete your work
- Submitting purchased or downloaded assignments
- Using prohibited electronic devices
- Looking at another's paper during exams
- Communicating with others during exams

---

## Range of Consequences

### Factors Considered

Consequences depend on:
- Severity of the violation
- First offense vs. repeat offense
- Intent (deliberate vs. accidental)
- Impact on others
- Student's response and honesty
- Nature of the assignment

### Consequence Levels

| Level | Applies To | Typical Consequences |
|-------|------------|---------------------|
| Minor | First offense, small assignment | Zero + warning |
| Moderate | First offense, major assignment | Failing grade + probation |
| Severe | Repeat offense or exam cheating | Suspension or dismissal |

---

## Specific Consequences

### First Offense - Minor Violation

**Examples:** Unauthorized collaboration on homework, minor copying

| Consequence | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Zero on assignment | No credit for the work |
| Written warning | Documented in file |
| Educational requirement | Academic integrity tutorial |
| Meeting with instructor | Discuss expectations |

### First Offense - Major Violation

**Examples:** Cheating on exam, extensive plagiarism, impersonation

| Consequence | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Failing grade (F) | For course or module |
| Academic probation | Monitoring status |
| Educational requirement | Academic integrity workshop |
| Notation in file | Permanent record of violation |

### Second Offense (Any Level)

**Any repeat violation:**

| Consequence | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Failing grade (F) | For course or module |
| Suspension | Temporary removal (may be permanent) |
| Possible dismissal | Reviewed by Director |
| Permanent record | Violation documented |

### Severe Violations (Any Offense)

**Examples:** Stealing exam, impersonation, selling exams, repeated cheating

| Consequence | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Immediate dismissal | Removal from program |
| No refund | Per refund policy |
| Notation on transcript | May be permanent |
| Report to authorities | If criminal activity involved |

## Process

### When Cheating is Suspected

1. **Instructor observes or suspects violation**
2. **Evidence documented**
3. **Student notified of allegation**
4. **Meeting held with student**
5. **Determination made**
6. **Consequences applied**
7. **Appeal available**

### Student Rights During Process

- Presumption of innocence until proven
- Opportunity to respond
- Review of evidence
- Bring support person to meetings
- Appeal adverse decisions

---

## Academic Probation for Cheating

Students placed on probation for academic integrity violations:

- Must meet with advisor regularly
- May have restricted privileges
- Any subsequent violation results in dismissal
- Probation remains until completion of program
- Documented on internal records

---

## Impact on Academic Standing

### Grades

- Zero on assignment or exam
- F in course affects GPA
- May impact SAP status
- May delay graduation

### Progression

- May not advance to externship with integrity violation
- May be ineligible for certifications
- May affect transfer to other programs

### Records

- Violation documented in student file
- Maintained per retention policy
- May be disclosed per [[FERPA Policy]]

---

## Clinical/Externship Cheating

### Higher Standards

Academic dishonesty in clinical settings is treated severely:

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Falsifying patient encounters | Dismissal |
| Forging supervisor signatures | Dismissal |
| Fabricating clinical hours | Dismissal |
| Falsifying competency checkoffs | Dismissal |

### Professional Implications

Clinical dishonesty may:
- Be reported to licensing boards
- Affect future licensure
- Result in employer notification
- Have legal consequences

---

## Impact on Certification

### Certifying Organizations

Some certifying bodies:
- Require disclosure of academic misconduct
- May deny certification
- May revoke existing certifications
- Maintain their own ethics standards

### Your Responsibility

You may be required to disclose academic integrity violations when:
- Applying for certification
- Applying for licensure
- Applying for employment
- Transferring to another school

---

## Rehabilitation

### After First Offense

Students who complete requirements may:
- Continue in program
- Learn from the experience
- Demonstrate integrity going forward
- Complete program successfully

### Requirements May Include

- Academic integrity workshop
- Reflective essay
- Meeting with advisor
- Additional monitoring

---

## Appeals

### Grounds for Appeal

You may appeal if you believe:
- Procedural error occurred
- Evidence is insufficient
- Consequence is disproportionate
- Factual error in findings

### Appeal Process

1. Submit written appeal within 5 business days
2. Include basis for appeal
3. Provide supporting evidence
4. Program Director reviews
5. Decision within 10 business days
6. Further appeal to School Director if needed

---

## Prevention

### How to Avoid Cheating

1. **Prepare adequately** for exams
2. **Start assignments early** to avoid panic
3. **Ask for help** when struggling
4. **Clarify expectations** with instructor
5. **Keep your work secure** from others
6. **When in doubt, ask**

- [[Tutoring Services]]
- Instructor office hours

- Study groups (when permitted)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I didn't know it was cheating?**
A: Ignorance is not an excuse, but intent may affect consequences.

**Q: Can I appeal a cheating finding?**
A: Yes, within 5 business days.

**Q: Will this go on my transcript?**
A: Typically internal records only, unless resulting in failing grade.

**Q: Can I still graduate with a cheating violation?**
A: Possibly, depending on consequences and completion of requirements.

**Q: What if someone cheated off my paper?**
A: Protect your work. If reported, explain the situation. You may not be at fault.
