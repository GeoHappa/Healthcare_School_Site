# Academic Probation Policy

---

## Purpose

Academic probation is an intervention designed to alert students who are not meeting satisfactory academic progress standards and provide support to help them succeed.

---

## SAP Standards

Students must maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) including:

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Cumulative GPA | 2.0 minimum |
| Attendance | 80% minimum |
| Pace | Complete program within 150% of scheduled time |
| Skills Competencies | Pass all required skills |

See [[Satisfactory Academic Progress]] for complete policy.

---

## Academic Status Progression

```
Good Standing → Academic Warning → Academic Probation → Academic Dismissal
```

| Status | Condition | Duration |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Good Standing | Meeting all SAP standards | Ongoing |
| Academic Warning | First SAP failure | One evaluation period |
| Academic Probation | Continued SAP failure after warning | One evaluation period |
| Academic Dismissal | Failure to meet SAP while on probation | Immediate |

---

## Academic Warning

### Trigger
- GPA falls below 2.0 at evaluation point
- Attendance falls below 80%
- Pace falls below minimum requirement

### Actions
1. Student notified in writing
2. Meeting with instructor/advisor
3. Academic improvement plan developed
4. Additional support offered

### Duration
- One evaluation period (typically 25% of program)
- Progress reviewed at next evaluation point

### Outcome
- Meet SAP standards → Return to Good Standing
- Fail to meet SAP → Academic Probation

---

## Academic Probation

### Trigger
- Failure to meet SAP standards during warning period
- Appeal of dismissal granted with probation conditions

### Notification
Within 5 business days of determination:
- Written notification sent
- Meeting scheduled with Program Director
- Probation terms documented

### Probation Requirements

**Mandatory:**
- Sign Academic Probation Agreement
- Meet weekly with instructor/advisor
- Complete Academic Improvement Plan
- Attend all classes (no unexcused absences)
- Submit all work on time

**Support Services:**
- Tutoring sessions (minimum 2 hours/week)
- Study skills workshop
- Additional lab practice time
- Progress monitoring

### Duration
- Maximum one evaluation period
- Weekly progress reviews
- Formal evaluation at period end

---

## Academic Improvement Plan

Students on probation must develop and follow an improvement plan including:

### Required Components
1. **Root cause analysis** - Identify factors affecting performance
2. **Specific goals** - Measurable academic targets
3. **Action steps** - Concrete steps to improve
4. **Timeline** - Weekly milestones
5. **Support resources** - Tutoring, study groups, etc.
6. **Monitoring** - Weekly check-ins with advisor

### Sample Goals
- Raise GPA from 1.8 to 2.0 by [date]
- Improve attendance from 75% to 85%
- Pass all quizzes with 70% or higher
- Complete all assignments on time

---

## Probation Outcomes

### Successful Completion
If student meets SAP standards by end of probation:
- Return to Good Standing
- Probation removed from active status
- Continue program normally

### Unsuccessful
If student fails to meet SAP standards:
- Academic Dismissal
- Withdrawal processed
- Refund calculated per policy
- May appeal dismissal

---

## Academic Dismissal

### Grounds for Dismissal
- Failure to meet SAP after probation period
- GPA below 1.5 at any evaluation point
- Attendance below 70%
- Academic integrity violation
- Failure of same course/module twice

### Dismissal Process
1. Written notification of dismissal
2. 5 business days to appeal
3. Exit interview scheduled
4. Final grade/transcript issued
5. Refund processed per [[Refund Policy]]

---

## Appeal Process

### Eligibility to Appeal
Students may appeal dismissal if:
- Extenuating circumstances existed
- Documentation supports circumstances
- Reasonable expectation of improvement exists

### How to Appeal
1. Submit written appeal within 5 business days
2. Include documentation of circumstances
3. Explain how circumstances have changed
4. Propose academic improvement plan

### Appeal Review
- Director reviews within 5 business days
- May request additional documentation
- May conduct interview
- Decision is final

### Possible Outcomes
- **Denied** - Dismissal stands
- **Granted with probation** - Return on strict probation
- **Granted with conditions** - Return with specific requirements

---

## Reinstatement

### After Dismissal
Students dismissed for academic reasons may apply for reinstatement:

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Waiting period | Minimum 1 term (or 90 days) |
| Application | Submit reinstatement request |
| Interview | Meet with Director |
| Plan | Submit Academic Success Plan |
| Approval | Director decision |

### Reinstatement Conditions
- Start on Academic Probation
- May need to repeat failed modules
- Additional fees may apply
- One reinstatement opportunity only

---

## Student Rights

Students on probation or facing dismissal have the right to:
- Written notification of status
- Review their academic records
- Meet with instructor and/or advisor
- Access support services
- Appeal adverse decisions
- Receive fair treatment

---

## Confidentiality

Academic status information is:
- Protected under FERPA
- Shared only with need-to-know staff
- Not disclosed to third parties without consent
- Maintained in student file
