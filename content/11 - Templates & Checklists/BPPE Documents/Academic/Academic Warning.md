# Academic Warning Policy

---

## Purpose

Academic Warning is an early intervention designed to notify students when their academic performance falls below satisfactory standards, before more serious consequences occur.

---

## When Academic Warning is Issued

A student is placed on Academic Warning when any of the following occur at an evaluation point:

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---------|-----------|
| Cumulative GPA | Below 2.0 |
| Attendance Rate | Below 80% |
| Completion Pace | Below minimum requirement |
| Failed Competency | Required skill not passed |

---

## Evaluation Points

Academic progress is evaluated at these intervals:

| Program | Evaluation Points |
|---------|-------------------|
| Medical Assistant (800 hrs) | 200, 400, 600, 800 hours |
| Phlebotomy (94 hrs) | 24, 47, 70, 94 hours |
| Medical Coding (460 hrs) | 115, 230, 345, 460 hours |
| EKG (50 hrs) | 13, 25, 38, 50 hours |

*Approximately 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% of program*

---

## Academic Warning Process

### Step 1: Notification
Within 5 business days of evaluation:
- Written notification sent to student
- Specific deficiencies identified
- Meeting scheduled with instructor/advisor

### Step 2: Meeting
Student meets with instructor/Program Director to:
- Review academic record
- Identify factors affecting performance
- Discuss available support resources
- Develop improvement plan

### Step 3: Improvement Plan
Student creates written plan including:
- Specific goals to achieve
- Steps to improve performance
- Timeline for improvement
- Support resources to utilize
- Weekly check-in schedule

### Step 4: Monitoring
During warning period:
- Weekly progress reviews
- Attendance tracking
- Assignment completion monitoring
- Support service utilization

---

## Warning Period Duration

- **Length:** One evaluation period (25% of program hours)
- **Review:** At next evaluation point
- **Extension:** Not available - must meet standards

---

## Possible Outcomes

### At End of Warning Period

| Outcome | Condition | Next Status |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Success | Meets all SAP standards | Good Standing |
| Partial Improvement | Some improvement, not meeting SAP | Academic Probation |
| No Improvement | No significant improvement | Academic Probation |
| Significant Decline | Performance worsened | May proceed to Dismissal |

---

## Student Responsibilities During Warning

1. **Attend all classes** - No unexcused absences
2. **Complete all work** - Submit assignments on time
3. **Seek help early** - Use tutoring and support services
4. **Communicate** - Keep instructor informed of challenges
5. **Follow improvement plan** - Complete all agreed-upon actions
6. **Attend check-ins** - Meet weekly with advisor

## Impact on Financial Aid/WIOA

### WIOA-Funded Students
- Warning status reported to workforce board
- Continued funding may depend on improvement
- Contact career counselor immediately

### Payment Plan Students
- Warning does not affect payment plan
- Must remain current on payments

---

## Difference: Warning vs. Probation

| Factor | Warning | Probation |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Severity | First intervention | Second intervention |
| Requirements | Improvement plan | Strict requirements |
| Monitoring | Weekly check-ins | Intensive monitoring |
| Next step if failed | Probation | Dismissal |

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Does warning appear on my transcript?**
A: No, warning status is internal and does not appear on official transcripts.

**Q: Can I continue to externship while on warning?**
A: Students must be in good academic standing before beginning externship. Resolve warning status first.

**Q: What if I have extenuating circumstances?**
A: Document circumstances and discuss with Program Director. A Leave of Absence may be appropriate.

**Q: Can I appeal being placed on warning?**
A: Warning status is based on objective criteria. If you believe there's an error, meet with the Program Director.

---

## Removing Warning Status

To return to Good Standing:
1. Raise GPA to 2.0 or above
2. Improve attendance to 80% or above
3. Meet completion pace requirements
4. Pass all required competencies
5. Complete improvement plan activities
