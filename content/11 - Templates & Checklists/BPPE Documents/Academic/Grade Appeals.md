# Grade Appeals Policy

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes a fair and consistent process for students to appeal grades they believe were assigned in error or do not accurately reflect their academic performance.

---

## Grounds for Appeal

A grade appeal may be filed when a student believes:

### Valid Grounds
- **Calculation error** - Mathematical error in grade computation
- **Recording error** - Grade incorrectly entered or reported
- **Policy deviation** - Grading criteria not applied consistently
- **Missing work** - Submitted work not included in grade
- **Unfair treatment** - Discriminatory or arbitrary grading

### Not Valid Grounds
- Disagreement with instructor's judgment
- Desire for a higher grade
- Comparison to other students' grades
- Personal circumstances not previously disclosed
- Late submission of excused absence documentation

---

## Grade Appeal Process

### Informal Resolution (Step 1)

**Timeline:** Within 5 business days of receiving grade

1. **Review graded work** with instructor
2. **Discuss concerns** respectfully
3. **Request explanation** of grade determination
4. **Provide evidence** if applicable (missing work, etc.)

Most grade concerns are resolved at this level.

### Formal Appeal (Step 2)

**Timeline:** Within 10 business days of informal resolution attempt

If informal resolution fails:

1. **Complete Grade Appeal Form**
2. **Attach documentation:**
   - Copy of graded work
   - Course syllabus/grading criteria
   - Evidence supporting appeal
   - Record of informal resolution attempt
3. **Submit to Program Director**

### Director Review (Step 3)

**Timeline:** Within 10 business days of receiving appeal

The Program Director will:
1. Review submitted documentation
2. Meet with student
3. Meet with instructor
4. Review relevant policies
5. Issue written decision

### Final Appeal (Step 4)

**Timeline:** Within 5 business days of Director decision

If dissatisfied with Director decision:
1. Submit written appeal to School Director
2. Include original appeal and Director response
3. Explain basis for continued appeal
4. School Director decision is final

---

## Appeal Timeline Summary

| Step | Action | Deadline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Informal resolution with instructor | 5 days after grade |
| 2 | Formal appeal to Program Director | 10 days after Step 1 |
| 3 | Director issues decision | 10 days after Step 2 |
| 4 | Final appeal to School Director | 5 days after Step 3 |
| 5 | Final decision issued | 10 days after Step 4 |

*All days are business days. Extensions may be granted for documented reasons.*

## Documentation Required

### For Formal Appeal

| Document | Required | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Grade Appeal Form | Yes | Available from Student Services |
| Copy of graded work | Yes | If applicable |
| Course syllabus | Yes | Including grading criteria |
| Evidence of concern | Yes | Calculations, communications, etc. |
| Record of Step 1 attempt | Yes | Date, participants, outcome |

---

## Possible Outcomes

### Grade Change
- Original grade replaced with corrected grade
- Transcript updated
- GPA recalculated

### Grade Stands
- Original grade upheld
- Documentation placed in file
- No change to transcript

### Incomplete
- Additional time to complete work
- Grade changed upon completion
- Must meet deadline

### Course Retake
- In rare cases, student may retake course
- Both grades may appear on transcript
- Per [[Retake Policy]]

---

## Committee Review

For complex appeals, a Grade Appeal Committee may be convened:

### Committee Composition
- Program Director (Chair)
- One faculty member (not the instructor)
- One staff member

### Committee Process
1. Review all documentation
2. Interview student and instructor (separately)
3. Deliberate in closed session
4. Issue majority decision
5. Provide written rationale

---

## Student Rights During Appeal

Students have the right to:
- Receive written explanation of grade
- Access their graded work
- Have appeal heard fairly
- Receive timely responses
- Be free from retaliation
- Confidential handling of appeal

---

## Academic Integrity Cases

Grade appeals involving academic integrity allegations follow a different process:
- See [[Academic Integrity Policy]]
- Appeals go through conduct process
- Different timeline and procedures apply

---

## Impact on Academic Standing

### During Appeal
- Original grade remains in effect
- SAP evaluated with original grade
- Probation/warning based on original grade

### After Successful Appeal
- New grade replaces original
- Academic standing recalculated
- Any adverse actions may be reversed

---

## Record Keeping

- All appeal documentation maintained for 5 years
- Outcomes tracked for institutional improvement
- FERPA protections apply

---

## Tips for Successful Appeals

1. **Act quickly** - Meet deadlines
2. **Be specific** - Identify exact concern
3. **Provide evidence** - Support claims with documentation
4. **Stay professional** - Respectful communication
5. **Follow process** - Complete each step in order
6. **Keep copies** - Document all communications
