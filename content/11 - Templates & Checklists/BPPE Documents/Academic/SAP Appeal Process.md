# SAP Appeal Process

---

## Overview

Students who fail to meet Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) standards have the right to appeal. This page explains when and how to submit a SAP appeal.

---

## When You Can Appeal

### Eligible Situations

You may appeal if you failed to meet SAP due to:

| Circumstance | Examples |
|--------------|----------|
| Medical emergency | Serious illness, hospitalization, surgery |
| Family emergency | Death or serious illness of immediate family |
| Personal trauma | Accident, victim of crime, natural disaster |
| Mental health | Documented mental health crisis |
| Military service | Deployment or service obligations |
| Other | Documented extraordinary circumstances |

### Requirements

Your appeal must show:
1. **What happened** - Explain the circumstances
2. **Why it affected you** - How it impacted your academic performance
3. **What changed** - The circumstances have been resolved or mitigated
4. **How you'll succeed** - Plan for meeting SAP going forward

## How to Appeal

### Step 1: Obtain Appeal Form

Get the SAP Appeal Form from:
- Student Services office
- School website: [URL]
- Financial Aid office

### Step 2: Complete the Form

Form includes:
- Personal information
- Program information
- Reason for appeal (check all that apply)
- Personal statement (detailed explanation)
- Academic improvement plan
- Signature and date

### Step 3: Write Personal Statement

Your statement should explain:

**What happened:**
- Describe the circumstances
- Be specific about dates and events
- Explain how it affected your academics

**What has changed:**
- How situation is resolved
- What's different now
- Why it won't happen again

**Your plan for success:**
- Specific steps you'll take
- Resources you'll use
- How you'll meet SAP requirements

### Step 4: Gather Documentation

Attach supporting documents:

| Circumstance | Documentation Needed |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Medical | Doctor's letter, hospital records |
| Family death | Death certificate, obituary |
| Family illness | Doctor's letter for family member |
| Mental health | Counselor/therapist letter |
| Other | Relevant official documentation |

### Step 5: Submit Complete Appeal

Submit to:
- Financial Aid Office / Student Services
- Address: [ADDRESS]
- Email: [EMAIL]
- In person or by mail

**Keep copies of everything you submit.**

## What Makes a Strong Appeal

### Do Include

- Specific details about circumstances
- Clear timeline of events
- How circumstances affected your academics specifically
- Evidence that situation is resolved
- Realistic plan for improvement
- All required documentation

### Don't Include

- Vague explanations ("I was going through a lot")
- Blame without taking responsibility
- Unrealistic promises
- Missing documentation
- Incomplete form

### Example Statement (Excerpt)

> "During the fall term, I was hospitalized for [condition] from [date] to [date]. This caused me to miss [X] weeks of class and fall behind in my coursework. As documented by my physician's letter, I have completed treatment and am cleared to return to full-time studies. I have already met with my academic advisor to develop a plan for catching up, and I will attend tutoring twice weekly..."

## Review Process

### Who Reviews

The SAP Appeal Committee, which may include:
- Financial Aid Director
- Academic Advisor
- Program Director

### What They Consider

- Validity of circumstances
- Quality of documentation
- Student's prior history
- Feasibility of academic plan
- Likelihood of future success

## Possible Outcomes

### Appeal Approved

If approved:
- Placed on SAP Probation
- Must follow Academic Improvement Plan
- Financial aid eligibility restored
- Progress monitored each term
- Must meet plan requirements to continue

### Appeal Denied

If denied:
- May not receive financial aid
- May continue at own expense (if allowed)
- May reapply for [[Reinstatement Process|reinstatement]] later
- May request reconsideration with new information

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I appeal more than once?**
A: Additional appeals are possible but must show new circumstances or significant change.

**Q: Will my appeal be automatically approved if I have documentation?**
A: No. Documentation is required but the committee considers all factors.

**Q: Can I still attend while my appeal is pending?**
A: Check with Student Services about interim enrollment options.

**Q: What if I don't have documentation?**
A: Your appeal may be weaker without documentation. Get what documentation you can.
