# Reinstatement Process

---

## Overview

Students who have been academically dismissed or who withdrew from [SCHOOL NAME] may apply for reinstatement to continue their education. This page explains the eligibility requirements and application process.

---

## Who Can Apply

### Eligible for Reinstatement

| Previous Status | Eligible? | Waiting Period |
|-----------------|-----------|----------------|
| Academic dismissal (SAP) | Yes | [X] weeks/months |
| Academic dismissal (Attendance) | Yes | [X] weeks/months |
| Voluntary withdrawal | Yes | None required |
| Leave of absence expired | Yes | None required |
| Conduct dismissal (minor) | Maybe | Case by case |
| Conduct dismissal (severe) | No | Not eligible |

### Not Eligible

Reinstatement is generally not available if:
- Dismissed for severe misconduct
- Dismissed twice previously
- Cannot complete program within maximum timeframe
- Owe balance and cannot make payment arrangements
- Other disqualifying circumstances

## Application Process

### Step 1: Request Reinstatement Package

Contact:
- Student Services: [EMAIL]
- Phone: [PHONE]

Request:
- Reinstatement Application Form
- Academic Improvement Plan template
- Current program schedule
- Financial worksheet

### Step 2: Complete Application

Application includes:
- Personal information
- Program and enrollment history
- Reason for previous dismissal/withdrawal
- Reinstatement personal statement
- Proposed academic improvement plan
- Supporting documentation

### Step 3: Write Personal Statement

Your statement should address:

**What happened:**
- Why you left/were dismissed
- Be honest and specific
- Take responsibility where appropriate

**What's changed:**
- How you addressed the issues
- What's different in your circumstances
- Personal growth or development

**Your plan for success:**
- Specific strategies for succeeding
- Resources you'll use
- How you'll handle challenges

**Your commitment:**
- Why you want to return
- Career goals
- Dedication to completion

### Step 4: Develop Academic Improvement Plan

Work with advisor (if available) to create plan including:
- Course schedule for remaining program
- GPA goals for each term
- Attendance commitment
- Study schedule
- Support services you'll use
- Check-in schedule with advisor

### Step 5: Gather Documentation

| If Dismissed For... | Provide... |
|--------------------|------------|
| Medical reasons | Clearance from physician |
| Mental health | Clearance from provider |
| Family emergency | Documentation of resolution |
| Work conflicts | New schedule/letter from employer |
| Other | Relevant documentation |

### Step 6: Submit Complete Application

Submit to:
- Student Services
- Address: [ADDRESS]
- Email: [EMAIL]
- Deadline: [DEADLINE before desired start]

## Possible Outcomes

### Approved

If reinstated:
- Placed on Academic Probation
- Must follow Academic Improvement Plan
- Required check-ins with advisor
- May have conditions attached
- Previous coursework applies (if current)

### Approved with Conditions

Common conditions:
- Reduced course load first term
- Required tutoring
- Frequent progress checks
- Specific GPA requirements
- Other requirements

### Denied

If denied:
- Written explanation provided
- May reapply after additional waiting period
- May explore other options

### Deferred

Application may be deferred if:
- Space not available
- Need additional documentation
- Issues not fully resolved

## If Reinstated

### Your Obligations

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Probation status | Automatically on probation |
| Improvement plan | Must follow exactly |
| Check-ins | Meet with advisor as scheduled |
| Performance | Must meet agreed standards |
| Participation | Full engagement expected |

### First Steps

1. Complete any required orientation
2. Meet with academic advisor
3. Finalize course schedule
4. Set up payment plan (if needed)
5. Begin classes

### Probation Period

- Typically first term after return
- Must meet improvement plan goals
- Progress evaluated at end of term
- Success = continue (may remain on probation)
- Failure = dismissal (typically not eligible for another reinstatement)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long do I have to wait before applying?**
A: Depends on reason for dismissal. See waiting period table above.

**Q: Will my previous grades count?**
A: Courses completed with C or higher typically count if curriculum unchanged.

**Q: Can I start at any time?**
A: Usually must start at beginning of a term when your courses are offered.

**Q: What if I can't complete within the maximum timeframe?**
A: You may not be eligible for reinstatement. Discuss options with advisor.

**Q: Is financial aid available?**
A: Possibly, but depends on your SAP status and aid history.
