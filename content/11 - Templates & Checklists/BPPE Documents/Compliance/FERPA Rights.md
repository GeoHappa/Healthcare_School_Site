# FERPA Rights

---

## What is FERPA?

The **Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)** is a federal law that protects the privacy of student education records. FERPA applies to all schools that receive federal funding.

---

## Your Rights Under FERPA

As a student, you have the following rights:

### 1. Right to Inspect Records

You have the right to:
- Review your education records
- Request access within 45 days
- View records maintained by the school

### 2. Right to Request Amendment

You have the right to:
- Request correction of inaccurate information
- Request deletion of misleading information
- Add a statement to disputed records

### 3. Right to Consent to Disclosure

You have the right to:
- Control who sees your records
- Provide written consent before disclosure
- Know what information is shared

### 4. Right to File Complaints

You have the right to:
- File a complaint if FERPA is violated
- Report violations to U.S. Department of Education

---

## What Are Education Records?

### Included in Education Records

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Academic records | Grades, transcripts, GPA |
| Enrollment records | Admission application, enrollment dates |
| Financial records | Account balance, payment history |
| Discipline records | Conduct violations, outcomes |
| Attendance records | Attendance tracking |
| Externship records | Clinical evaluations, hours |

### NOT Education Records

| Type | Examples |
|------|----------|
| Personal notes | Instructor's private notes |
| Law enforcement records | Campus security records |
| Employment records | If employed by school (in that capacity) |
| Medical records | Health center records (separate protections) |
| Alumni records | Created after you leave |

## Accessing Your Records

### How to Request Access

1. Submit written request to Registrar
2. Identify records you wish to view
3. School has 45 days to comply
4. Schedule appointment to review

### What You Can Do

- View records in person
- Request copies (fee may apply)
- Receive explanations of records
- Have representative present

### What You Cannot Do

- Remove records from file
- Alter or destroy records
- Access other students' records
- Access confidential recommendations (if you waived access)

---

## Amending Records

### When You Can Request Amendment

You may request amendment if records are:
- Inaccurate
- Misleading
- In violation of your privacy

### Process

1. Submit written request to Registrar
2. Identify specific record and concern
3. Explain why it should be changed
4. School reviews and responds

### If Request is Denied

- You may request a hearing
- Present your case
- If still denied, add a statement to your file
- Statement remains with disputed record

### What Cannot Be Challenged

- Grades (use academic appeal process)
- Substantive judgments
- Opinions in records
- Content you simply disagree with

---

## Disclosure of Records

### When Consent is Required

Written consent is needed to release records to:
- Employers (non-verification)
- Other schools (some exceptions)
- Insurance companies
- Family members (including parents)
- General public

### Consent Form Requirements

Valid consent must include:
- Specify records to be released
- Purpose of disclosure
- Parties receiving information
- Your signature and date

---

## Exceptions to Consent

Records may be released WITHOUT your consent to:

| Recipient | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|
| School officials | Legitimate educational interest |
| Other schools | Transfer evaluation |
| Auditors/evaluators | Authorized education audits |
| Financial aid officials | Financial aid determination |
| Accrediting organizations | Accreditation functions |
| Court orders/subpoenas | Legal compliance |
| Health/safety emergencies | Protect health or safety |
| State/local authorities | Juvenile justice system |

### School Officials

Includes employees with legitimate educational interest:
- Instructors and administrators
- Student Services staff
- Financial aid personnel
- Board members
- Contractors performing school functions

---

## Directory Information

### What is Directory Information?

Basic information the school may disclose without consent:
- Name
- Address
- Phone number
- Email address
- Program of study
- Enrollment status
- Dates of attendance
- Credentials earned
- Honors and awards

### Opting Out

You have the right to:
- Restrict disclosure of directory information
- Submit written opt-out request
- Must be done by specified deadline
- Remains in effect until you change it

### How to Opt Out

1. Complete FERPA Opt-Out Form
2. Submit to Registrar
3. Confirm receipt
4. Update as needed

---

## Parent Access

### General Rule

FERPA rights belong to students, not parents:
- Students control their records
- Parents do not have automatic access
- Even if parents pay tuition

### Exceptions

Parents may access records if:
- Student provides written consent
- Student is claimed as dependent (tax purposes)
- Health/safety emergency exists
- Required by court order

---

## Third Party Requests

### Employment Verification

Employers may receive verification of:
- Dates of attendance
- Program completed
- Credential earned
- With your written consent OR if directory information

### Background Checks

Background check companies:
- Must have your written consent
- Limited to what you authorize
- May verify enrollment/completion

## Complaints

### Filing a FERPA Complaint

If you believe your rights have been violated:

**U.S. Department of Education**
Student Privacy Policy Office
400 Maryland Avenue, SW
Washington, DC 20202-8520

- File within 180 days of violation
- Include specific details
- Provide documentation

### Internal Complaints

First try to resolve internally:
1. Contact Registrar
2. Escalate to School Director
3. File formal grievance if unresolved

---

## Protecting Your Privacy

### Best Practices

1. **Don't share login credentials**
2. **Review your records regularly**
3. **Know who has access**
4. **Understand directory opt-out**
5. **Report concerns promptly**

### Identity Verification

School will verify identity before:
- Releasing records
- Discussing information by phone
- Providing copies
- Allowing record access
