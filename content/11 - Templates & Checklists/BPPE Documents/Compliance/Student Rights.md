# Student Rights

---

## Overview

As a student at [SCHOOL NAME], you have specific rights protected by California law, federal regulations, and institutional policy. This document outlines your rights and how to exercise them.

---

## Right to Information

### Before Enrollment
You have the right to receive:
- School Catalog (current version)
- School Performance Fact Sheet
- Enrollment Agreement (before signing)
- Total cost of program (itemized)
- Refund policy disclosure
- STRF disclosure

### During Enrollment
You have the right to:
- Access your student records
- Request copies of your records
- Review your grades and attendance
- Receive progress reports
- Know your instructor's qualifications

---

## Consumer Protection Rights

### Cancellation Rights
- Cancel enrollment within 7 days of signing for full refund
- Cancel before first class for full refund (minus registration fee up to $250)
- Written cancellation notice required

### Refund Rights
- Receive pro-rata refund if withdrawing during program
- Refund issued within 45 days of withdrawal
- Clear disclosure of refund calculation method
- See [[Refund Policy]] for complete details

### STRF Rights
- Protection through Student Tuition Recovery Fund
- May recover losses if school closes
- May recover if school fails to pay judgment
- See [[STRF Disclosure]] for eligibility

## Academic Rights

### Fair Treatment
- Be graded according to stated criteria
- Receive timely feedback on work
- Access to syllabus and grading policies
- Equal treatment regardless of protected characteristics

### Due Process
- Written notice of academic concerns
- Opportunity to respond to allegations
- Fair investigation of complaints
- Right to appeal academic decisions
- See [[Grade Appeals]] and [[Grievance Procedure]]

### Academic Support
- Access to tutoring services
- Reasonable make-up opportunities for excused absences
- Academic advising
- See [[Tutoring Services]]

## Privacy Rights (FERPA)

Under the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act:

### Right to Access
- Inspect your education records
- Request copies of records
- Receive records within 45 days of request

### Right to Amend
- Request correction of inaccurate records
- Add statement to disputed records
- Appeal denial of amendment request

### Right to Consent
- Control disclosure of your records
- Provide written consent before release
- Exceptions: school officials, legal requirements, emergencies

### Right to Complain
- File complaints with U.S. Department of Education
- Report FERPA violations

See [[FERPA Policy]] for complete details.

## Disability Rights

### Under ADA and Section 504
- Equal access to programs and facilities
- Reasonable accommodations for documented disabilities
- Accessible facilities
- Modified testing arrangements (when appropriate)
- Freedom from discrimination

### To Request Accommodations
1. Self-identify to Student Services
2. Provide documentation of disability
3. Participate in interactive process
4. Receive reasonable accommodations

See [[Disability Services (ADA)]] for procedures.

---

## Non-Discrimination Rights

You have the right to be free from discrimination based on:
- Race, color, or ethnicity
- National origin
- Sex or gender
- Age
- Disability
- Religion
- Sexual orientation
- Gender identity
- Veteran status
- Pregnancy or parental status
- Any other protected characteristic

### If You Experience Discrimination
1. Report to School Director immediately
2. File written complaint
3. Receive investigation of complaint
4. File external complaint (BPPE, OCR, DFEH)

## Student Responsibilities

Rights come with responsibilities:
- Follow school policies
- Maintain academic integrity
- Respect others
- Pay tuition as agreed
- Attend classes
- Complete assignments
- Maintain professional conduct

## How to Exercise Your Rights

### Step 1: Know Your Rights
- Read this document
- Review School Catalog
- Ask questions

### Step 2: Communicate
- Talk to instructor or staff
- Put requests in writing
- Keep copies of communications

### Step 3: Escalate if Needed
- Use [[Grievance Procedure]]
- Contact School Director
- File external complaints if unresolved

---

## Protection from Retaliation

You are protected from retaliation for:
- Filing complaints
- Reporting violations
- Participating in investigations
- Exercising your rights

**Report retaliation immediately to the School Director.**
