# CDPH Phlebotomy Requirements

---

## Overview

The California Department of Public Health (CDPH) Laboratory Field Services regulates phlebotomy training and certification in California. All phlebotomy training programs must meet CDPH requirements for graduates to be eligible for the Certified Phlebotomy Technician I (CPT I) license.

## CPT I Training Requirements

### Minimum Hours

| Component | Minimum Hours |
|-----------|---------------|
| Didactic (Classroom) | 40 hours |
| Clinical Practical | 40 hours |
| **Total Minimum** | **80 hours** |

*Note: [SCHOOL NAME] Phlebotomy Program exceeds minimums at 94 hours total.*

### Clinical Requirements

| Requirement | Minimum |
|-------------|---------|
| Successful Venipunctures | 50 |
| Successful Skin Punctures | 10 |

All draws must be:
- Performed on live patients
- Supervised by qualified personnel
- Documented and signed

## Required Curriculum Content

CDPH mandates training in these areas:

### Didactic Topics
1. **Anatomy and Physiology**
   - Circulatory system
   - Blood composition
   - Vein anatomy and location

2. **Medical Terminology**
   - Common terms
   - Abbreviations
   - Laboratory terminology

3. **Specimen Collection**
   - Venipuncture techniques
   - Capillary puncture
   - Order of draw
   - Equipment and supplies

4. **Safety and Infection Control**
   - Standard precautions
   - Bloodborne pathogens
   - Sharps safety
   - Personal protective equipment

5. **Quality Assurance**
   - Patient identification
   - Specimen labeling
   - Rejection criteria
   - Chain of custody

6. **Legal and Ethical Issues**
   - Patient rights
   - Confidentiality
   - Scope of practice
   - Professional conduct

7. **Special Populations**
   - Pediatric patients
   - Geriatric patients
   - Difficult draws
   - Special needs patients

## Clinical Training Requirements

### Approved Clinical Settings
- Hospital laboratories
- Outpatient laboratories
- Blood donation centers
- Physician office laboratories

### Supervision Requirements
Clinical training must be supervised by:
- Licensed clinical laboratory scientist (CLS)
- Licensed medical laboratory technician (MLT)
- Certified phlebotomy technician (CPT I or II)
- Licensed physician

### Documentation Required
For each venipuncture/skin puncture:
- Date and time
- Patient identifier (not name)
- Type of collection
- Supervisor initials
- Student signature

---

## Program Approval

### CDPH Program Requirements
Training programs must:
- Meet minimum hour requirements
- Cover required curriculum content
- Provide supervised clinical experience
- Maintain student records
- Use qualified instructors

### Instructor Qualifications
Phlebotomy instructors must have:
- Current CPT I or higher license, OR
- CLS, MLT, or physician license
- Minimum 3 years phlebotomy experience
- Training in instructional methods

---

## Student Eligibility

### Prerequisites for CPT I Exam
Graduates must have:
- Completed approved training program
- Minimum 40 hours didactic
- Minimum 40 hours clinical
- 50 successful venipunctures documented
- 10 successful skin punctures documented

### Age Requirement
- No minimum age for training
- Must be 18+ to work unsupervised

### Background Check
- No state background check required for certification
- Employers may require background checks
- Criminal history may affect employment

## CPT License Levels

| Level | Requirements | Scope |
|-------|--------------|-------|
| CPT I | 80+ hours training | Routine venipuncture and skin puncture |
| CPT II | CPT I + 1,040 hours experience | Arterial puncture, pediatric, special procedures |

---

## Maintaining Certification

### Renewal
- CPT I license must be renewed
- Renewal period: Every 2 years
- Continuing education may be required
- Renewal fee applies

### Address Changes
- Notify CDPH of address changes
- Update within 30 days

---

## Scope of Practice

### CPT I May Perform
- Routine venipuncture
- Capillary/skin puncture
- Specimen collection and handling
- Basic specimen processing
- Patient identification verification

### CPT I May NOT Perform
- Arterial puncture (requires CPT II)
- IV starts
- Blood transfusions
- Any procedures outside training

---

## Employment Requirements

### California Law
- Must hold valid CPT I to perform phlebotomy for hire
- License must be current (not expired)
- Must work under laboratory director supervision

### Exempt Settings
Some settings may be exempt from CPT requirements:
- Certain physician offices
- Blood donation centers (separate requirements)
- Check current regulations

## Common Compliance Issues

| Issue | How to Avoid |
|-------|--------------|
| Insufficient clinical hours | Ensure 40+ hours documented |
| Incomplete draw logs | Document every attempt |
| Missing supervisor signatures | Get signatures same day |
| Expired certification | Renew before expiration |
