## Important Notice

> **NOTICE CONCERNING TRANSFERABILITY OF CREDITS AND CREDENTIALS EARNED AT OUR INSTITUTION**
>
> The transferability of credits you earn at [SCHOOL NAME] is at the complete discretion of an institution to which you may seek to transfer. Acceptance of the diploma or certificate you earn in your program is also at the complete discretion of the institution to which you may seek to transfer. If the credits or diploma/certificate that you earn at this institution are not accepted at the institution to which you seek to transfer, you may be required to repeat some or all of your coursework at that institution. For this reason, you should make certain that your attendance at this institution will meet your educational goals. This may include contacting an institution to which you may seek to transfer after attending [SCHOOL NAME] to determine if your credits, diploma, or certificate will transfer.

## Understanding This Disclosure

### What This Means

[SCHOOL NAME] is a vocational training institution providing career-focused healthcare education. Our programs are designed to prepare you for immediate employment in healthcare, not for transfer to colleges or universities.

### Key Points

1. **Credits earned here may not transfer** to other educational institutions
2. **Diploma/certificate may not be accepted** for advanced standing at other schools
3. **Transfer decisions are made by the receiving institution**, not by us
4. **You should research transfer policies** BEFORE enrolling if transfer is your goal

## Transfer Credit Policy

### Credits TO [SCHOOL NAME]

We have a limited policy for accepting transfer credits:

**Requirements:**
- From BPPE-approved or accredited institution
- Coursework comparable in content and hours
- Grade of C or better
- Completed within [5] years
- Official transcripts required

**Evaluation:**
- Program Director reviews transcripts
- Determines course equivalency
- Maximum transfer credit: [25%] of program

See [[Transfer Credit Policy]] for complete details.

### Credits FROM [SCHOOL NAME]

**Reality:**
- Most community colleges and universities do NOT accept transfer credit from vocational schools
- Our programs are not designed for college transfer
- Credits are clock-hour based, not semester units

**If Transfer is Your Goal:**
- Contact receiving institution before enrolling here
- Get transfer policy in writing
- Consider starting at a transferable institution

### Diploma/Certificate Value

- Portable across states (with some variations)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I transfer my credits to a community college?**
A: Unlikely. Community colleges typically do not accept credits from vocational schools. Contact the specific college to confirm.

**Q: Will my MA diploma help me become a nurse?**
A: Our diploma does not transfer as nursing credits. However, your healthcare experience may be valuable when applying to nursing programs.

**Q: If I complete phlebotomy here, can I skip phlebotomy in an MA program elsewhere?**
A: Possibly. Contact the receiving school directly. They may grant credit or waive requirements based on your certification.

**Q: Why don't more schools accept transfer credit?**
A: Accreditation differences, credit type (clock hours vs. semester units), and curriculum variations affect transferability.
