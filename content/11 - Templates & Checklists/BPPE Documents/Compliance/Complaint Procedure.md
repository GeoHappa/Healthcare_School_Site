## Overview

[SCHOOL NAME] is committed to resolving student concerns fairly and promptly. This document outlines the procedures for filing complaints and the resources available to students.

## Internal Complaint Process

### When to File a Complaint

You may file a complaint when:
- School policies are not being followed
- You experience unfair treatment
- Services are not as described
- You have concerns about safety
- Other issues affecting your education

### Step 1: Informal Resolution

**Try to resolve the issue directly first:**

| Issue Type | First Contact |
|------------|---------------|
| Classroom/instructor issues | Speak with instructor |
| Administrative issues | Speak with office staff |
| Student services issues | Student Services Coordinator |
| General concerns | Program Director |

**Timeline:** Within 5 business days of issue arising

### Step 2: Formal Written Complaint

If informal resolution fails:

1. **Complete Complaint Form**
   - Available from Student Services
   - Include detailed description of issue
   - Attach supporting documentation
   - State desired resolution

2. **Submit to Program Director**
   - In person or via email
   - Keep copy for your records
   - Request written acknowledgment

3. **Acknowledgment**
   - Complaint acknowledged within 2 business days
   - Investigation begins

### Step 3: Investigation

**What Happens:**
- Relevant parties interviewed
- Documentation reviewed
- Facts gathered
- Typically completed within 10 business days

**Your Rights During Investigation:**
- Provide additional information
- Be free from retaliation
- Receive updates on status

### Step 4: Resolution

**Written Response:**
- Decision provided within 15 business days
- Includes findings and actions taken
- Explains appeal rights

### Step 5: Appeal (If Needed)

If dissatisfied with resolution:
1. Submit written appeal within 5 business days
2. Address to School Director
3. Include basis for appeal
4. Final decision within 10 business days

## Complaint Form

A complete complaint should include:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Your name and contact | How to reach you |
| Date of complaint | When you're filing |
| Date(s) of incident | When issue occurred |
| Description | Detailed account of what happened |
| Witnesses | Names of others involved |
| Documentation | Any supporting evidence |
| Prior attempts to resolve | Who you've already spoken to |
| Desired resolution | What outcome you're seeking |
| Signature | Your signature and date |

## External Complaint Options

### Bureau for Private Postsecondary Education (BPPE)

If you are not satisfied with the school's response, or at any time, you may file a complaint with BPPE:

**Bureau for Private Postsecondary Education**
1747 N. Market Blvd., Suite 225
Sacramento, CA 95834

- **Phone:** (916) 574-8900
- **Toll-Free:** (888) 370-7589
- **Fax:** (916) 263-1897
- **Website:** www.bppe.ca.gov

**Online Complaint Form:**
www.bppe.ca.gov/enforcement/complaint.shtml

### When to Contact BPPE

You may contact BPPE:
- At any time (no requirement to complete internal process first)
- If school does not respond within stated timeframes
- If you disagree with school's resolution
- For issues involving regulatory violations

### Information to Include in BPPE Complaint

1. Your name and contact information
2. School name and address
3. Dates you attended
4. Detailed description of complaint
5. Copies of enrollment agreement and other relevant documents
6. Documentation of attempts to resolve with school
7. Any other supporting evidence

## Protection from Retaliation

### Your Rights
You are protected from retaliation for:
- Filing a complaint
- Participating in an investigation
- Reporting violations
- Exercising your rights

### What Constitutes Retaliation
- Adverse academic actions
- Harassment or intimidation
- Denial of services
- Any negative treatment due to complaint

### Report Retaliation
Report suspected retaliation immediately to:
- School Director
- BPPE

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I have to use the internal process first?**
A: No. You may contact BPPE at any time, though attempting internal resolution first is encouraged.

**Q: Will filing a complaint affect my grades?**
A: No. Retaliation is prohibited. Report any suspected retaliation immediately.

**Q: Can I file anonymously?**
A: Internal complaints cannot be fully investigated anonymously. External complaints to BPPE may have different policies.

**Q: How long do I have to file a complaint?**
A: File as soon as possible. BPPE has specific timeframes; check their website.

**Q: What if I'm no longer enrolled?**
A: Former students may still file complaints.
