# Grievance Rights

---

## Overview

You have the right to file grievances when you believe school policies have not been followed or you have been treated unfairly. This document explains your grievance rights and how to exercise them.

---

## Your Rights

### Right to File a Grievance

You have the right to:
- File a complaint about school policies or treatment
- Have your complaint investigated fairly
- Receive a written response
- Appeal decisions you disagree with
- Be free from retaliation

### Right to Fair Treatment

You have the right to:
- Be treated with respect during the process
- Have your grievance kept confidential (to extent possible)
- Present evidence and witnesses
- Receive a timely response

### Right to External Recourse

You have the right to:
- File complaints with external agencies
- Contact BPPE at any time
- Seek legal counsel if desired

## Grievance Process Overview

### Step 1: Informal Resolution
- Attempt to resolve directly with involved party
- Document the conversation
- Timeline: Within 5 business days of issue

### Step 2: Formal Grievance
- Submit written grievance form
- Include documentation
- Timeline: Within 10 business days of informal attempt

### Step 3: Investigation
- School investigates complaint
- Both parties interviewed
- Timeline: Within 15 business days

### Step 4: Resolution
- Written decision provided
- Actions taken (if any)
- Appeal rights explained

### Step 5: Appeal (if needed)
- Submit written appeal
- School Director reviews
- Final decision issued

## Confidentiality

### Your Privacy is Protected

- Grievances handled confidentially
- Information shared only with those who need to know
- Outcome not publicly disclosed
- Records maintained securely

### Limits on Confidentiality

Information may be shared when:
- Required to investigate (with relevant parties)
- Required by law
- Safety concerns exist
- Required for legal proceedings

---

## Retaliation Protection

### Retaliation is Prohibited

You are protected from retaliation for:
- Filing a grievance
- Participating in investigation
- Supporting another's grievance
- Reporting violations

### Examples of Retaliation

- Adverse academic actions
- Hostile treatment
- Exclusion from activities
- Harassment
- Reduced grades

### Reporting Retaliation

If you experience retaliation:
1. Report immediately to School Director
2. Document the retaliation
3. File additional grievance
4. Contact BPPE if internal process fails

## Documentation

### What to Keep

Maintain records of:
- All written communications
- Dates and times of incidents
- Names of witnesses
- Any evidence
- Meeting notes
- Responses received

### Why Documentation Matters

Good documentation:
- Supports your case
- Helps establish timeline
- Provides evidence for appeal
- May be needed for external complaints

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I file anonymously?**
A: Anonymous complaints are difficult to investigate. Confidential complaints are preferred.

**Q: What if I'm worried about retaliation?**
A: Retaliation is prohibited. Report any retaliation immediately.

**Q: How long do I have to file?**
A: File as soon as possible. Generally within 30 days of incident.

**Q: Can I go directly to BPPE?**
A: Yes, you can contact BPPE at any time without using internal process.

**Q: What if I disagree with the outcome?**
A: You can appeal internally, then file with BPPE if still unsatisfied.
