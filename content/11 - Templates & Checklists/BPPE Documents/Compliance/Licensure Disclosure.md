## California Requirements

### Phlebotomy Technician

**State Licensure: REQUIRED**

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Credential | Certified Phlebotomy Technician I (CPT I) |
| Issuing Agency | California Department of Public Health (CDPH) |
| Required? | **Yes** - Cannot work as phlebotomist in CA without license |
| Application | Submit to CDPH after completing approved training |
| Fee | Approximately $100 |
| Processing | 4-8 weeks |

**Requirements for CPT I:**
- Complete approved training program (80+ hours)
- 40 hours didactic, 40 hours clinical
- 50 venipunctures, 10 skin punctures documented
- Submit application with documentation
- Pay fee

**CDPH Contact:**
(510) 620-3800
www.cdph.ca.gov/Programs/OSPHLD/LFS

## Out-of-State Employment

### Important Warning

> **If you plan to work in a state other than California, licensure requirements may differ. You must verify requirements in that state before seeking employment.**

### What May Differ

| Factor | May Vary By State |
|--------|-------------------|
| License required | Some states require licenses CA does not |
| Training requirements | Hours or content may differ |
| Exam requirements | Different exams may be required |
| Application process | Each state has own process |
| Reciprocity | CA license may or may not transfer |

### How to Verify

1. Contact the state licensing board in your target state
2. Research online requirements
3. Contact employers in that state
4. Consult professional associations

## Disclosures by Program

### Medical Assistant

This program prepares you for entry-level medical assistant positions. California does not require a license to work as a medical assistant. Voluntary national certification is available through multiple organizations. Most certifications require passing an exam after completing an approved training program.

### Phlebotomy Technician

This program prepares you to apply for the California CPT I license issued by CDPH. California requires a license to work as a phlebotomist. Our program meets CDPH training requirements. You must apply separately to CDPH after completing the program.

### Medical Coding Specialist

This program prepares you for entry-level medical coding positions. No state license is required for medical coding. Voluntary national certification is available through AAPC and AHIMA. While not legally required, most employers expect certification.

### EKG Technician

This program prepares you for entry-level EKG technician positions. California does not require a license for EKG technicians. Voluntary national certification is available.
