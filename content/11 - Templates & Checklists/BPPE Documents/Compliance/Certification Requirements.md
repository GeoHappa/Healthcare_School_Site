## Phlebotomy Technician Certification

### Certification Status
- **STATE LICENSE REQUIRED** for employment in California
- Must obtain CPT I from California Department of Public Health

### Licensure Process

**Step 1: Complete Training**
- 40 hours didactic minimum
- 40 hours clinical minimum
- 50 venipunctures + 10 skin punctures

**Step 2: Apply to CDPH**
- Submit application and fee (~$100)
- Include training documentation
- Processing: 4-8 weeks

**Step 3: Receive License**
- CPT I license mailed
- Valid for 2 years
- Must renew before expiration

### National Certification (Optional)
In addition to state license, graduates may pursue:

| Certification | Organization | Notes |
|---------------|--------------|-------|
| CPT (NHA) | NHA | National certification |
| NCPT | NCCT | National certification |
| PBT | ASCP | Requires additional experience |

### CDPH Contact
California Department of Public Health
Laboratory Field Services
(510) 620-3800
See [[CDPH Requirements]] for details

## Medical Coding Certification

### Certification Status
- **Not required** for employment
- **Strongly recommended** - most employers require certification
- Entry-level positions may hire pending certification

### Available Certifications

| Certification | Organization | Exam Fee | Focus |
|---------------|--------------|----------|-------|
| CPC | AAPC | ~$399 | Physician-based coding |
| CPC-A | AAPC | ~$399 | Entry-level (apprentice) |
| CCS | AHIMA | ~$399 | Hospital coding |
| CCA | AHIMA | ~$299 | Entry-level coding |

### Recommended Path
1. Graduate from training program
2. Take CPC or CCA exam
3. Gain experience
4. Remove apprentice designation (CPC-A)
5. Pursue specialty certifications

### Exam Preparation
- Program includes certification exam prep
- Practice exams provided
- Open-book exam (bring coding manuals)

### Maintaining Certification
- AAPC: 36 CEUs every 2 years
- AHIMA: 20 CEUs every 2 years
- Renewal fees required
