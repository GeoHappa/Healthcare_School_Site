## 3. Attendance Policy

### Attendance Requirements

Regular attendance is essential for success. Students are expected to:
- Attend all scheduled classes
- Arrive on time
- Remain for the entire class period
- Sign in and out on the daily attendance log

### Minimum Attendance Standard

- **Required:** 80% attendance to remain in good standing
- **Graduation requirement:** Minimum 80% overall attendance
- Externship attendance: 100% of assigned hours required

### Tardiness

- Arriving more than 10 minutes late = tardy
- Leaving more than 10 minutes early = tardy
- Three (3) tardies = one (1) absence

### Absence Reporting

Students must:
- Notify the school BEFORE the start of class
- Call: [PHONE NUMBER]
- Email: [EMAIL ADDRESS]
- Failure to notify = unexcused absence

### Excused vs. Unexcused Absences

**Excused absences** (documentation required):
- Illness with doctor's note
- Medical emergency
- Death in immediate family
- Court appearance with documentation
- Military duty with orders

**Unexcused absences:**
- No call/no show
- Personal appointments
- Transportation issues
- Work schedule conflicts

### Make-Up Policy

- Students may make up missed work within one week
- Make-up time must be scheduled with instructor
- Maximum make-up hours: 10% of program hours
- Externship hours cannot be made up and must be completed

### Attendance Warning and Probation

| Attendance Level | Action |
|------------------|--------|
| Below 85% | Verbal warning |
| Below 80% | Written warning and attendance plan |
| Below 75% | Academic probation |
| Below 70% | Subject to dismissal |

## 5. Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)

### SAP Requirements

To maintain Satisfactory Academic Progress, students must:
- Maintain minimum 70% cumulative grade average
- Maintain minimum 80% attendance
- Complete program within 150% of scheduled time

### SAP Evaluation Points

Progress is evaluated at:
- 25% of program completion
- 50% of program completion
- 75% of program completion
- Program completion

### Academic Warning

Students falling below SAP standards will:
- Receive written notification
- Meet with Program Director
- Develop an academic improvement plan
- Have one evaluation period to meet standards

### Academic Probation

If standards are not met during warning period:
- Placed on academic probation
- Continued enrollment with conditions
- Weekly progress monitoring
- Must meet standards by next evaluation

### Academic Dismissal

Dismissal may occur if student:
- Fails to meet SAP after probation period
- Earns below 60% in any course twice
- Falls below 70% attendance after warning
- Violates academic integrity policy

### Appeal Process

Students may appeal dismissal by:
1. Submitting written appeal within 5 business days
2. Explaining extenuating circumstances
3. Providing supporting documentation
4. Presenting plan for improvement

Students are expected to:
- Treat all persons with respect and dignity
- Maintain professional behavior at all times
- Come to class prepared and ready to learn
- Follow all school policies and procedures
- Represent the school professionally

### Professional Standards

As future healthcare professionals, students must:
- Dress appropriately (see dress code)
- Maintain personal hygiene
- Use appropriate language
- Demonstrate ethical behavior
- Respect patient confidentiality

**Classroom:**
- Scrubs or professional attire

- Possession or use of drugs/alcohol

- HIPAA violations
- Falsification of records

### Cell Phone and Electronic Device Policy

- Devices must be silenced during class
- No phone calls or texting during instruction
- Devices may be used during breaks
- Recording requires instructor permission
- No devices during examinations

The externship/clinical training component provides real-world experience:
- **Medical Assistant:** 160 hours

- Follow all site policies and procedures

Students may be removed from externship for:
- Unexcused absences
- Unprofessional conduct
- HIPAA violations
- Safety violations
- Site request

Removal may result in:
- Reassignment to new site (if available)
- Program dismissal
- Incomplete grade

## 9. Safety and Emergency Procedures

### General Safety

- Know the location of exits
- Know the location of first aid kit
- Know the location of fire extinguisher
- Report unsafe conditions immediately
- Follow all safety protocols in lab

### Emergency Procedures

#### Fire
1. Activate nearest fire alarm
2. Evacuate using nearest exit
3. Meet at designated assembly area
4. Do not re-enter building
5. Call 911 if not already done

#### Medical Emergency
1. Call 911
2. Notify staff member
3. Do not move injured person (unless danger)
4. Provide first aid if trained
5. Complete incident report

#### Earthquake
1. DROP, COVER, and HOLD ON
2. Stay away from windows
3. After shaking stops, evacuate if safe
4. Meet at designated assembly area
5. Do not re-enter building until cleared

#### Active Shooter
1. RUN if safe path exists
2. HIDE if escape not possible
3. FIGHT as last resort
4. Call 911 when safe

### Incident Reporting

All incidents must be reported immediately:
- Injuries (student, staff, visitor)
- Near-misses
- Property damage
- Safety hazards
- Emergencies

Complete incident report form within 24 hours.

## 11. Grievance Procedure

### Internal Grievance Process

**Step 1: Informal Resolution**
- Discuss concern with instructor or staff member
- Attempt to resolve informally
- Timeline: Within 5 business days of incident

**Step 2: Written Complaint**
- If unresolved, submit written complaint to Program Director
- Include: Nature of complaint, dates, persons involved, desired resolution
- Timeline: Within 10 business days of Step 1

**Step 3: Director Review**
- Program Director investigates and responds in writing
- Timeline: Within 10 business days of receiving complaint

**Step 4: Appeal**
- If unsatisfied, appeal to School Director
- Submit written appeal with supporting documentation
- Timeline: Within 5 business days of Step 3 response

**Step 5: Final Decision**
- School Director reviews and issues final decision
- Timeline: Within 10 business days of appeal
- Decision is final for internal process

### External Complaint Process

A student or any member of the public may file a complaint with:

**Bureau for Private Postsecondary Education**
1747 N. Market Blvd., Suite 225
Sacramento, CA 95834
Phone: (888) 370-7589
Website: www.bppe.ca.gov

Students must exhaust the school's internal grievance procedure before filing with BPPE, unless the complaint alleges violations of state law.

## 12. FERPA Rights

### Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act

Under FERPA, students have the right to:

1. **Inspect and review** education records within 45 days of request

2. **Request amendment** of records believed to be inaccurate

3. **Consent to disclosure** of personally identifiable information

4. **File a complaint** with the U.S. Department of Education

### Directory Information

The following may be disclosed without consent:
- Student name
- Program of study
- Enrollment status
- Dates of attendance
- Degrees/certificates awarded
- Honors and awards

Students may request non-disclosure of directory information.

### Disclosure Without Consent

Records may be disclosed without consent to:
- School officials with legitimate educational interest
- Accrediting agencies
- Appropriate parties in health/safety emergencies
- In response to lawful subpoena (with notice)
- Other institutions where student seeks enrollment
