# Clinical Partnerships

## Overview

Clinical partnerships are **critical to success**. Without externship sites, you cannot graduate students from clinical programs like Medical Assistant and Phlebotomy. Building these relationships is one of the most important pre-launch activities.

---

## Why Clinical Partnerships Matter

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Externship Sites** | Required for MA and Phlebotomy programs |
| **Job Placement** | Partners often hire graduates |
| **Credibility** | Partner names enhance marketing |
| **Advisory Input** | Employers shape curriculum relevance |
| **Accreditation** | ABHES requires documented clinical agreements |

---

### For Phlebotomy Clinical Training
- Hospital laboratories

- Reference laboratories
- Large medical group practices

### For Job Placement
- All of the above, plus:
- Medical billing companies
- Insurance companies
- Health systems (Kaiser, Dignity, Sutter)

## Partnership Development Process

### Step 1: Identify Contacts (Months 1-3)
- [ ] Research local healthcare facilities
- [ ] Identify key contacts (HR, Education, Clinical Managers)
- [ ] Leverage instructor networks
- [ ] Attend healthcare networking events
- [ ] Join local healthcare associations

### Step 2: Initial Outreach (Months 3-5)
- [ ] Send introduction letter on school letterhead
- [ ] Follow up with phone call
- [ ] Request meeting to discuss partnership
- [ ] Prepare partnership presentation

### Step 3: Partnership Meeting
**Agenda:**
1. Introduce school and mission
2. Explain programs and student qualifications
3. Present partnership benefits
4. Address concerns (liability, supervision)
5. Discuss next steps

### Step 4: Formalize Agreement (Months 5-8)
- [ ] Draft affiliation agreement
- [ ] Legal review (both parties)
- [ ] Execute agreement
- [ ] Establish operational procedures

### Step 5: Maintain Relationship
- [ ] Regular check-ins (quarterly)
- [ ] Address any issues promptly
- [ ] Recognize partners publicly
- [ ] Invite to advisory board
- [ ] Provide student performance feedback

## Affiliation Agreement Essentials

### Key Terms
| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Purpose** | Educational externship, not employment |
| **Duration** | 1-3 years, renewable |
| **Student Requirements** | Background check, immunizations, CPR |
| **Supervision** | Site provides qualified supervisor |
| **Liability** | School maintains liability insurance |
| **Indemnification** | Mutual hold-harmless clause |
| **Confidentiality** | HIPAA compliance requirements |
| **Termination** | Notice period, process |

### School Responsibilities
- Screen and prepare students
- Provide liability insurance
- Maintain student records
- Remove problematic students promptly
- Communicate regularly

### Site Responsibilities
- Provide qualified supervision
- Orient students to facility
- Allow sufficient patient interaction
- Evaluate student performance
- Notify school of any issues

## Value Proposition to Partners

### What We Offer
1. **Pre-screened candidates** - Background checked, trained, professional
2. **Free labor** - Externs work under supervision at no cost
3. **Future hires** - Evaluate before committing to employment
4. **Reduced turnover** - Externs who become employees stay longer
5. **Community goodwill** - Supporting local workforce development
6. **Flexible scheduling** - Work with partner's needs
7. **Recognition** - Public acknowledgment of partnership

### Common Objections & Responses

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We're too busy" | "Students come ready to help. We'll match schedules to your slow periods initially." |
| "Liability concerns" | "We carry $1M+ liability insurance. Agreement includes indemnification." |
| "We've had bad students before" | "We carefully screen students. You can dismiss any student not meeting expectations." |
| "No one to supervise" | "Any licensed healthcare professional can supervise. We provide evaluation forms." |
| "We already work with another school" | "Consider adding us for more candidate options. No exclusivity required." |

## Externship Site Requirements

### Medical Assistant Sites
- Must be clinical healthcare setting
- Must have licensed supervision (MD, DO, PA, NP, RN)
- Appropriate patient volume for skill development
- Willing to allow student to perform:
  - Vital signs
  - Patient intake
  - Injections (under supervision)
  - EKG
  - Phlebotomy (if trained)
  - Administrative tasks

### Phlebotomy Sites
- CLIA-certified laboratory
- Licensed laboratory director oversight
- Qualified supervisor (CPT I minimum, MLT/MLS preferred)
- Minimum 50+ draws available for student completion
- Documented training verification process

## Student Requirements for Externship

### Documentation
- [ ] Completed background check (cleared)
- [ ] Drug screen (negative)
- [ ] Immunization records (Hep B series, MMR, Varicella, Tdap)
- [ ] TB test (negative, within 1 year)
- [ ] CPR/BLS certification (current)
- [ ] Professional liability insurance (via school policy)
- [ ] Signed confidentiality agreement

### Preparation
- [ ] Completed required didactic coursework
- [ ] Passed lab competency assessments
- [ ] Professional dress code review
- [ ] Site orientation completed
- [ ] Emergency contact information provided

|----------|-----------|

## Advisory Board

### Purpose
- Provide industry input on curriculum
- Enhance employer partnerships
- Support job placement
- Meet accreditation requirements

### Composition (Target: 5-7 members)
- 2-3 healthcare employers (HR or clinical managers)
- 1 current student representative
- 1 graduate representative
- 1 community representative
- School leadership (ex officio)

### Meeting Schedule
- Twice annually (Fall and Spring)
- Additional as needed for curriculum review

### Advisory Board Benefits
- Deepens partner commitment
- Provides valuable feedback
- Meets ABHES requirements
- Enhances credibility
