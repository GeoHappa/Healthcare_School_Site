# Clinical Site Development Process

---

## Overview

This document outlines the process for identifying, approaching, and securing clinical partnership sites for student externships and clinical practicum placements.

---

## Site Development Pipeline

```
Identify → Research → Outreach → Meeting → Agreement → Onboarding → Active
```

### Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Activities | Timeline |
|-------|------------|----------|
| Identify | Find potential sites | Ongoing |
| Research | Gather information | 1-2 weeks |
| Outreach | Initial contact | 1-2 weeks |
| Meeting | Site visit and discussion | 1-2 weeks |
| Agreement | Negotiate and sign | 2-4 weeks |
| Onboarding | Setup processes | 1-2 weeks |
| Active | Place students | Ongoing |

---

## Step 1: Identify Potential Sites

### Target Facilities

| Program | Ideal Site Types |
|---------|------------------|
| Medical Assistant | Physician offices, urgent care, clinics |
| Phlebotomy | Hospitals, labs, blood banks |
| EKG | Cardiology offices, hospitals |

### Sources for Site Leads

- Online directories (Yelp, Google Maps)
- Healthcare association memberships
- Professional networking
- Graduate referrals
- Advisory board connections
- Job posting analysis
- Local business listings

### Initial Criteria

Sites should ideally have:
- Adequate patient volume
- Qualified supervision available
- Willingness to train students
- Safe environment
- Accessible location
- Professional reputation

---

## Step 2: Research Prospective Sites

### Information to Gather

| Information | Source |
|-------------|--------|
| Facility type and size | Website, observation |
| Services offered | Website, marketing materials |
| Key contacts | Website, phone inquiry |
| Ownership/management | Business filings, website |
| Reputation | Reviews, word of mouth |
| Existing student programs | Direct inquiry |

### Research Documentation

Create site profile including:
- Facility name and address
- Contact information
- Service types
- Size/patient volume (estimate)
- Notes on potential fit

---

## Step 3: Initial Outreach

### Outreach Methods

| Method | Best For |
|--------|----------|
| Phone call | Initial contact |
| Email | Follow-up, details |
| In-person visit | Relationship building |
| Mail | Introduction packet |

### Who to Contact

1. **Practice Manager/Office Manager** - First contact
2. **HR/Education Department** - Larger facilities
3. **Physician/Owner** - Small practices
4. **Clinical Supervisor** - Department heads

### Initial Outreach Script

```
"Hello, my name is [NAME] from [SCHOOL NAME]. We train
Medical Assistants/Phlebotomists in the [AREA] area.

We're looking for quality clinical sites where our
students can complete their externship hours under
professional supervision.

Would you have a few minutes to discuss whether a
partnership might work for your practice?"
```

- [[Introduction Letter]]

## Step 4: Site Meeting

### Meeting Goals

1. Present partnership opportunity
2. Understand site needs and concerns
3. Tour facility
4. Discuss logistics
5. Answer questions
6. Gauge interest level

### Presentation Topics

- About [SCHOOL NAME]
- Program overview
- Student preparation level
- Externship structure
- Site responsibilities
- School support provided
- Benefits to the site

### Questions to Ask Site

| Area | Questions |
|------|-----------|
| Capacity | How many students can you accommodate? |
| Supervision | Who would supervise students? |
| Schedule | What schedules work best? |
| Requirements | Do you have specific requirements? |
| Past experience | Have you worked with students before? |
| Concerns | What concerns do you have? |

### Site Evaluation

Assess during visit:
- Professional environment
- Adequate supervision potential
- Patient volume
- Learning opportunities
- Safety conditions
- Staff receptiveness

---

## Step 5: Agreement Negotiation

### Clinical Affiliation Agreement

Key terms to address:

| Term | Considerations |
|------|----------------|
| Term length | 1-3 years typical |
| Student numbers | Per rotation limits |
| Supervision | Qualifications required |
| Insurance | Liability coverage |
| Termination | Notice requirements |
| Compliance | HIPAA, OSHA, etc. |

### Site Requirements

Sites typically require:
- Student background checks
- Drug screens
- Immunization records
- CPR certification
- Health clearance

### School Responsibilities

School typically provides:
- Student selection and screening
- Pre-externship training
- Supervision/coordination
- Insurance coverage
- Problem resolution
- Outcome tracking

### See [[Clinical Affiliation Agreement]] for template

## Step 6: Site Onboarding

### After Agreement Signed

| Task | Responsible | Timeline |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Add to site database | Clinical Coordinator | Immediate |
| Collect site policies | Clinical Coordinator | 1 week |
| Train staff on process | School | Before first student |
| Develop site orientation | School | Before first student |
| Create evaluation forms | School | Before first student |

### Site Orientation Materials

Provide to site:
- Program competencies
- Student evaluation forms
- Timesheet/attendance forms
- Emergency contact list
- Supervision guidelines
- School contact information

---

## Step 7: Ongoing Management

### Relationship Maintenance

| Activity | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|
| Site visits | Each rotation |
| Phone check-ins | Weekly during placements |
| Appreciation communication | Annually |
| Agreement renewal | Per agreement terms |
| Performance review | Annually |

### Issue Resolution

When problems arise:
1. Contact site promptly
2. Listen to concerns
3. Investigate thoroughly
4. Take appropriate action
5. Follow up to confirm resolution
6. Document everything

### Student Feedback

Collect from students:
- Site evaluation after externship
- Learning experience rating
- Supervision quality
- Recommendation for future students

## Site Tracking

### Database Fields

Track for each site:
- Contact information
- Agreement status and dates
- Capacity (students per rotation)
- Programs accepted
- Special requirements
- Supervisor names
- Student placements (history)
- Performance notes

### See [[Site Contact Tracker]] for template

---

## Rejecting or Removing Sites

### Reasons to Decline Site

- Inadequate supervision
- Safety concerns
- Poor learning environment
- Unprofessional treatment of students
- Non-compliance with requirements
- Excessive issues

### Site Removal Process

1. Document concerns
2. Attempt resolution
3. Make termination decision
4. Provide notice per agreement
5. Relocate any current students
6. Update database

---

## Quality Standards

### Site Must Maintain

- Appropriate supervision at all times
- Safe working environment
- Adequate patient volume for learning
- Professional treatment of students
- Compliance with affiliation agreement
- Timely communication with school

### School Monitors

- Student experiences and feedback
- Completion of competencies
- Site responsiveness
- Any concerns or complaints
