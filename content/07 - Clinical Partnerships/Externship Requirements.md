# Externship Requirements

---

## Overview

The externship/clinical component provides hands-on experience in real healthcare settings. Students must complete all requirements before placement at a clinical site.

---

## Programs with Externship Component

| Program | Clinical Hours | Type |
|---------|----------------|------|
| Medical Assistant | 160 hours | Externship |
| Phlebotomy | 50 hours | Clinical Practicum |
| EKG Technician | 10 hours | Clinical Observation |

*Medical Coding does not have a clinical component.*

---

## Pre-Externship Requirements

All students must complete the following **before** beginning externship:

### Academic Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Classroom Hours | Complete all didactic/lab hours |
| GPA | Minimum 2.0 cumulative |
| Skills Competencies | All skills signed off |
| Attendance | 80% minimum |
| Financial | Current on payment plan |

### Health & Safety Requirements

| Requirement | Details | Valid Period |
|-------------|---------|--------------|
| TB Test | Negative result (2-step or QuantiFERON) | Within 12 months |
| Physical Exam | Cleared for clinical work | Within 12 months |
| Immunizations | See immunization checklist below | Current |
| Drug Screen | Negative 10-panel result | Within 30 days |
| Background Check | Clear or site-approved | Within 12 months |

### Immunizations Required

| Vaccine | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Hepatitis B | Series complete or signed declination |
| MMR | 2 doses or titer proving immunity |
| Varicella | 2 doses or titer proving immunity |
| Tdap | Within 10 years |
| Influenza | Current season (or signed declination) |
| COVID-19 | Per site requirements |

### Documentation Required

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| CPR/BLS Certification | American Heart Association preferred |
| Government ID | Valid, unexpired |
| Social Security Card | For site onboarding |
| Proof of Health Insurance | Some sites require |

## Background Check Policy

### What is Screened
- Criminal history (7 years)
- Sex offender registry
- Healthcare sanctions/exclusions
- OIG/GSA exclusion lists

### Disqualifying Offenses
The following may disqualify students from externship placement:
- Felony convictions
- Drug-related offenses
- Theft or fraud convictions
- Violent crimes
- Offenses involving vulnerable populations

### Disclosure Process
Students with criminal history must:
1. Disclose at admission interview
2. Provide court documentation
3. Meet with Program Director
4. Receive site-by-site approval determination

*A criminal record does not automatically disqualify you. Disclosure and transparency are required.*

---

## Drug Screening Policy

### Standard Panel
10-panel urine drug screen testing for:
- Amphetamines
- Barbiturates
- Benzodiazepines
- Cocaine
- Marijuana (THC)
- Methadone
- Methamphetamines
- Opiates
- Phencyclidine (PCP)
- Propoxyphene

### Positive Results
- Students with positive results cannot begin externship
- Prescription medications must be verified with MRO
- Marijuana is NOT permitted (regardless of CA law)
- Retest may be available per policy

## Site Placement Process

### Step 1: Eligibility Verification
- Academic requirements confirmed
- All health/safety documents submitted
- Background check and drug screen cleared

### Step 2: Site Matching
- Student availability reviewed
- Site requirements compared
- Geographic preferences considered
- Assignment made by Clinical Coordinator

### Step 3: Orientation
- Site-specific orientation (if required)
- Review of site policies
- Emergency contact information
- First day logistics

### Step 4: Externship Begins
- Report to site as scheduled
- Follow all site policies
- Complete required hours
- Submit timesheets weekly

---

## During Externship

### Student Responsibilities
- Arrive on time, in proper attire
- Follow site policies and procedures
- Maintain professional conduct
- Complete all assigned tasks
- Submit timesheets and evaluations
- Report absences immediately

### Attendance Requirements
- 100% of scheduled hours required
- No unexcused absences
- Missed time must be made up
- Excessive absences = removal from site

### Professional Standards
- Dress code compliance
- No cell phone use
- HIPAA compliance
- Respectful communication
- No disclosure of patient information

## Site Requirements by Program

### Medical Assistant Externship (160 hours)
**Typical Settings:**
- Physician offices
- Urgent care centers
- Specialty clinics
- Community health centers

**Expected Competencies:**
- Patient intake and vitals
- Injections and phlebotomy
- EKG administration
- Administrative tasks
- Assisting with procedures

### Phlebotomy Practicum (50 hours)
**Typical Settings:**
- Hospital laboratories
- Blood banks
- Outpatient draw stations
- Clinical laboratories

**Required Competencies:**
- Minimum 50 successful venipunctures
- Minimum 10 capillary sticks
- Specimen processing
- Order verification

### EKG Clinical (10 hours)
**Typical Settings:**
- Cardiology offices
- Hospital cardiac units
- Outpatient clinics

**Expected Competencies:**
- 12-lead EKG application
- Patient preparation
- Artifact recognition
- Documentation

## Failure to Complete Externship

Students who cannot complete externship may face:
- Program extension
- Additional fees for extended enrollment
- Loss of site placement
- Dismissal from program

### Common Issues
- Attendance problems
- Unprofessional conduct
- Site removal by facility
- Failure to meet competencies
- Health/safety non-compliance
