# Enrollment Process

## Overview

A clear, professional enrollment process ensures prospective students understand expectations and make informed decisions. This process must comply with BPPE requirements for disclosures and student protection.

---

## Enrollment Funnel

```
Inquiry → Tour → Application → Acceptance → Enrollment → Start
   ↓         ↓         ↓            ↓           ↓         ↓
  24hr    Same day   48hr       24hr       72hr     Orientation
response  or next   review     notify    complete
```

---

## Step 1: Inquiry (Lead Generation)

### Inquiry Sources
- Website form submission
- Phone call
- Walk-in visitor
- Referral
- Community event
- Social media message

### Initial Response (Within 24 hours)
- [ ] Acknowledge inquiry
- [ ] Answer immediate questions
- [ ] Schedule campus tour / info session
- [ ] Send program information packet (email)
- [ ] Enter into CRM system

### Information to Collect
- Name, phone, email
- Program of interest
- Desired start date
- How did you hear about us?
- Current situation (employed, student, etc.)

---

## Step 2: Campus Tour / Information Session

### Tour Components (45-60 minutes)
1. **Welcome** (5 min)
   - Meet admissions representative
   - Overview of visit agenda

2. **School Overview** (10 min)
   - Mission and history
   - Programs offered
   - Accreditation status

3. **Facility Tour** (15 min)
   - Classrooms
   - Clinical skills lab
   - Computer lab
   - Student areas

4. **Program Details** (15 min)
   - Curriculum overview
   - Schedule options
   - Externship process
   - Career outcomes

5. **Financial Discussion** (10 min)
   - Tuition and fees
   - Payment options
   - Financial assistance

6. **Q&A and Next Steps** (10 min)
   - Answer questions
   - Explain application process
   - Schedule follow-up if needed

### Virtual Tour Option
- Video walkthrough for distant inquiries
- Live video call with admissions
- Program presentation via Zoom

---

## Step 3: Application

### Application Requirements

#### All Programs
- [ ] Completed application form
- [ ] Application fee ($50-$100, may be waived)
- [ ] High school diploma/GED (or transcript)
- [ ] Valid government-issued ID
- [ ] Entrance assessment (if applicable)

#### Clinical Programs (MA, Phlebotomy)
- [ ] Background check authorization
- [ ] Immunization records (or plan to obtain)
- [ ] TB test (or plan to obtain)
- [ ] CPR certification (or plan to obtain)

### Entrance Assessment
- Basic reading comprehension
- Basic math skills
- No minimum score required (diagnostic)
- Used to identify students needing support

### Application Review (Within 48 hours)
- Verify documentation completeness
- Review entrance assessment
- Check background (clinical programs)
- Make acceptance decision

---

## Step 4: Acceptance Decision

### Acceptance Criteria
- High school diploma or GED
- 18 years of age or older
- Clear background check (clinical programs)
- Demonstrated interest and commitment
- Ability to meet program demands

### Communication
- **Accepted:** Phone call + acceptance letter
- **Conditional:** Letter with conditions to meet
- **Denied:** Respectful letter with reason, alternatives

### Acceptance Package
- Congratulations letter
- Enrollment checklist
- Enrollment agreement (for review)
- School catalog
- School Performance Fact Sheet (SPFS)
- Financial worksheet

---

## Step 5: Enrollment

### Required Documents (BPPE Compliance)

#### Enrollment Agreement
Must include:
- [ ] School name and address
- [ ] Student name and address
- [ ] Program title and objective
- [ ] Schedule (start date, hours, duration)
- [ ] Total charges (tuition, fees, books)
- [ ] Payment terms
- [ ] Refund policy (pro-rata)
- [ ] Student's right to cancel
- [ ] BPPE contact information
- [ ] Student signature and date
- [ ] School representative signature

#### School Performance Fact Sheet (SPFS)
Must disclose (per program):
- [ ] Completion rate
- [ ] Placement rate
- [ ] License exam pass rate
- [ ] Median loan debt
- [ ] Median earnings
- [ ] Student acknowledgment signature

#### Catalog Acknowledgment
- [ ] Student confirms receipt of catalog
- [ ] Student confirms understanding of policies

#### Additional Disclosures
- [ ] Drug-free school policy
- [ ] FERPA rights
- [ ] Grievance procedure
- [ ] Arbitration agreement (if any)

### Enrollment Meeting (60-90 minutes)
1. Review enrollment agreement in detail
2. Explain refund and cancellation policy
3. Review School Performance Fact Sheet
4. Discuss payment options and finalize plan
5. Complete all required signatures
6. Provide copies of all documents to student
7. Schedule orientation

### Cooling-Off Period
Per California law:
- Student has right to cancel within 7 days
- Full refund if cancelled before instruction begins
- Pro-rata refund after instruction begins

## Step 6: Pre-Start Requirements

### Administrative
- [ ] Enrollment agreement signed
- [ ] Payment arrangement confirmed
- [ ] Student ID created
- [ ] Contact information verified
- [ ] Emergency contact obtained

### Clinical Programs Only
- [ ] Background check completed (cleared)
- [ ] Immunizations documented or scheduled
- [ ] TB test documented
- [ ] CPR certification obtained/scheduled
- [ ] Scrubs/uniform ordered

### All Students
- [ ] Textbooks ordered/distributed
- [ ] Login credentials provided (LMS, email)
- [ ] Orientation date confirmed
- [ ] Class schedule provided

---

## Step 7: Orientation

### Orientation Agenda (Half day)
1. **Welcome and Introductions** (30 min)
   - Meet staff and instructors
   - Icebreaker activity

2. **Facility Orientation** (30 min)
   - Tour and logistics
   - Safety procedures
   - Emergency exits

3. **Academic Policies** (45 min)
   - Attendance requirements
   - Grading policy
   - Academic probation
   - Grievance procedure

4. **Program Overview** (30 min)
   - Curriculum overview
   - Expectations
   - Success strategies

5. **Student Services** (30 min)
   - Career services introduction
   - Tutoring availability
   - Student resources

6. **Administrative** (30 min)
   - Final paperwork
   - Payment reminders
   - Questions

7. **First Day Prep** (15 min)
   - What to bring
   - What to expect
   - Contact information

## Enrollment Policies

### Start Dates
- Rolling enrollment (monthly starts preferred)
- Minimum class size: 5 students
- Maximum class size: 20-25 students

### Late Enrollment
- May join within first week of class
- Must make up missed content
- Program Director approval required

### Deferral
- May defer start up to 6 months
- One deferral allowed
- Must reconfirm 30 days before new start

### Transfer Students
- Case-by-case evaluation
- Transcript review required
- Maximum 25% transfer credit

---

## Financial Options

### Payment Options
1. **Pay in Full** - 5% discount
2. **Payment Plan** - Monthly payments over program duration
3. **Third-Party Financing** - Partner with Climb Credit, Meritize
4. **Employer Tuition Reimbursement** - Coordinate with employer
5. **Workforce Development** - WIOA, EDD programs

### Payment Plan Terms
- Minimum 20% down payment
- Balance divided over program duration
- No interest (if paid on time)
- Late fee: $25 per occurrence

### Workforce Agency Partnerships
- Sacramento Employment & Training Agency
- Golden Sierra Workforce Development
- California EDD programs

---

## Refund Policy (BPPE Compliant)

### Before Classes Begin
- Full refund minus $250 administrative fee
- Refund within 45 days

### After Classes Begin
Pro-rata refund based on:
- Percentage of program completed
- Calculated to nearest 10%
- Minus non-refundable fees (application, registration)

### Refund Schedule Example (MA Program - $15,000)
| % Completed | Refund |
|-------------|--------|
| 0-10% | 90% ($13,500) |
| 11-20% | 80% ($12,000) |
| 21-30% | 70% ($10,500) |
| 31-40% | 60% ($9,000) |
| 41-50% | 50% ($7,500) |
| 51-60% | 40% ($6,000) |
| >60% | No refund |

## Compliance Checklist

### BPPE Requirements
- [ ] Enrollment agreement meets all requirements
- [ ] SPFS provided and acknowledged
- [ ] Catalog provided
- [ ] Cancellation rights explained
- [ ] Refund policy compliant
- [ ] Student retains copies of all documents
- [ ] Records maintained for 5+ years

### Best Practices
- [ ] Never pressure students to enroll
- [ ] Allow time to review documents at home
- [ ] Encourage questions
- [ ] Document all communications
- [ ] Follow up on incomplete enrollments
