# Marketing Strategy

## Overview

A comprehensive marketing strategy is essential to achieve enrollment targets. This plan covers brand positioning, target audiences, marketing channels, budget allocation, and key metrics.

---

## Brand Positioning

### Brand Promise
> "Fast-track healthcare careers with hands-on training and local job placement support."

### Unique Value Proposition
1. **Only healthcare training center in Folsom** - No local competition
2. **Local externships** - Train where you'll work
3. **Affordable** - Competitive pricing vs. Sacramento schools
4. **Fast** - Graduate in months, not years
5. **Job-focused** - Career services from day one

### Brand Personality
- Professional but approachable
- Supportive and encouraging
- Practical and results-oriented
- Community-focused

### Key Messages
- "Start your healthcare career in [X] months"
- "Hands-on training with real clinical experience"
- "Local externships, local jobs"
- "Affordable tuition with payment plans"
- "Join Folsom's growing healthcare workforce"

## Target Audiences

### Primary Audience: Career Changers (25-45)
**Profile:**
- Currently employed but unfulfilled
- Seeking stable, meaningful career
- Can afford tuition (or has savings)
- Values work-life balance
- Attracted to healthcare's job security

**Pain Points:**
- "I'm stuck in a dead-end job"
- "I want to help people"
- "I need a career, not just a job"
- "I can't afford to go back to school for years"

**Messaging:**
- "It's never too late to start a healthcare career"
- "Graduate in months, not years"
- "Real training for real careers"

### Secondary Audience: Recent High School Graduates (18-22)
**Profile:**
- Not pursuing 4-year college
- Parents may assist with tuition
- Wants to start working quickly
- Prefers hands-on learning

**Pain Points:**
- "I don't want to be stuck in retail"
- "College isn't for me"
- "I want to make real money"

**Messaging:**
- "Skip the debt, start your career"
- "Hands-on training beats lectures"
- "Earn while your friends are in debt"

### Tertiary Audience: Displaced Workers
**Profile:**
- Recent layoff (Intel, tech sector)
- Has severance or savings
- Needs career pivot
- May be eligible for workforce programs

**Pain Points:**
- "My industry is dying"
- "I need to reinvent myself"
- "I want job security"

**Messaging:**
- "Healthcare is hiring"
- "Your next career starts here"
- "Workforce training programs available"

### Quaternary Audience: Workforce Development Referrals
**Profile:**
- Unemployed/underemployed
- Eligible for WIOA or other funding
- May have barriers to employment
- Referred by workforce agencies

**Pain Points:**
- "I can't afford training"
- "I need help finding a job"

**Messaging:**
- "Financial assistance available"
- "We'll help you every step of the way"

## Marketing Channels

### Digital Marketing (60% of budget)

#### Search Engine Marketing (SEM/PPC)
**Budget:** 25% of total
**Platforms:** Google Ads
**Strategy:**
- Target keywords: "medical assistant training Folsom," "phlebotomy classes Sacramento," "healthcare career training near me"
- Geo-target: 25-mile radius of Folsom
- Use call extensions and location extensions
- Landing pages for each program

**Expected Metrics:**
- Cost per click: $3-$8
- Conversion rate: 5-10%
- Cost per lead: $30-$80

#### Social Media Advertising
**Budget:** 20% of total
**Platforms:** Facebook, Instagram
**Strategy:**
- Target demographics matching student profiles
- Interest targeting: healthcare, career change, job search
- Retargeting website visitors
- Video content performing well

**Ad Types:**
- Carousel ads (program highlights)
- Video testimonials
- Lead generation ads
- Event promotion (open houses)

#### Search Engine Optimization (SEO)
**Budget:** 10% of total (primarily labor)
**Strategy:**
- Optimize website for local search
- Create program-specific landing pages
- Blog content (career guides, industry news)
- Build local citations (directories)
- Encourage Google reviews

**Target Keywords:**
- "medical assistant school Folsom"
- "phlebotomy training Sacramento"
- "healthcare careers California"
- "CMA certification program"

#### Email Marketing
**Budget:** 5% of total
**Strategy:**
- Nurture leads not ready to enroll
- Monthly newsletter with career tips
- Program announcements
- Re-engagement campaigns
- Graduate success stories

### Traditional/Local Marketing (25% of budget)

#### Community Events
**Budget:** 10% of total
**Activities:**
- Career fairs (high schools, colleges, workforce centers)
- Health fairs and community events
- Open house events (monthly)
- Employer lunch-and-learns

#### Print Materials
**Budget:** 5% of total
**Materials:**
- Program brochures
- Business cards
- Posters/flyers for community boards
- Direct mail (targeted neighborhoods)

#### Local Media
**Budget:** 5% of total
**Channels:**
- Local newspaper ads (Folsom Telegraph)
- Community magazines
- Local radio (if budget allows)
- Press releases for milestones

#### Partnerships
**Budget:** 5% of total
**Strategy:**
- High school counselor outreach
- Workforce development agency partnerships
- Employer tuition reimbursement programs
- Community organization relationships

### Referral Marketing (15% of budget)

#### Student Referrals
**Program:**
- $250-$500 referral bonus for enrolled referral
- Paid after referred student completes first month

#### Graduate Network
**Strategy:**
- Stay connected with graduates
- Share job opportunities
- Encourage reviews and testimonials
- Alumni events

#### Employer Referrals
**Strategy:**
- Ask partner employers to recommend training
- Tuition assistance coordination
- Customized training opportunities

## Lead Response Protocol

### Speed-to-Lead Standards

| Lead Source | Response Time Target | Method |
|-------------|---------------------|--------|
| Website form | <5 minutes (business hours) | Phone call |
| Phone inquiry | Immediate | Live answer |
| Email inquiry | <1 hour | Email + phone |
| Walk-in | Immediate | Meet with admissions |
| After hours | <30 min next business day | Text + email |

### Lead Scoring Model

| Action | Points | Interpretation |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Website visit | 5 | Awareness |
| Program page view | 10 | Interest |
| Form submission | 25 | High interest |
| Tour scheduled | 50 | Strong consideration |
| Tour attended | 75 | Ready to decide |
| Application started | 90 | Intent to enroll |

**Hot Lead:** 75+ points → Immediate admissions follow-up
**Warm Lead:** 40-74 points → Nurture sequence
**Cold Lead:** <40 points → General marketing

## Open House Event Strategy

### Monthly Open House Format

**Frequency:** 2nd Saturday of each month, 10am-12pm
**Format:** 2-hour event with structured flow

```
10:00 - 10:15  Welcome & Refreshments
10:15 - 10:30  School Overview Presentation
10:30 - 11:00  Program Deep Dives (breakout sessions)
11:00 - 11:30  Facility Tour & Skills Lab Demo
11:30 - 11:50  Financial Aid & Enrollment Info
11:50 - 12:00  Q&A, One-on-One Consultations
```

### Open House Checklist

**Pre-Event (1 week before):**
- [ ] Email all leads in database
- [ ] Social media promotion
- [ ] Facebook event created
- [ ] Eventbrite registration
- [ ] Reminder calls to registrants
- [ ] Refreshments ordered
- [ ] Materials printed
- [ ] Name tags prepared

**Day-Of:**
- [ ] Sign-in sheet ready
- [ ] Presentation tested
- [ ] Skills lab equipment ready for demo
- [ ] Staff assignments confirmed
- [ ] Enrollment paperwork available
- [ ] Follow-up system ready

**Post-Event (within 48 hours):**
- [ ] Thank you emails sent
- [ ] Follow-up calls to attendees
- [ ] Schedule tours for no-shows
- [ ] Post photos on social media
- [ ] Track conversions

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Registrations | 25+ |
| Attendance rate | 60%+ |
| Tour-to-enrollment rate | 30% |

## Crisis Marketing Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Low Enrollment

**Triggers:** <10 enrollments for 2 consecutive months

**Response Plan:**
1. Increase digital ad spend by 50%
2. Launch limited-time discount (10% off)
3. Host additional open house events
4. Activate referral bonus increase
5. Direct outreach to waitlist/past inquiries

### Scenario 2: Negative Reviews

**Triggers:** <4.0 star average on Google

**Response Plan:**
1. Respond professionally to all reviews
2. Implement review request program for satisfied students
3. Address underlying issues immediately
4. Request satisfied graduates post reviews
5. Monitor daily until resolved

### Scenario 3: Competitor Entry

**Triggers:** New school opens in Folsom area

**Response Plan:**
1. Emphasize established reputation
2. Highlight graduate outcomes
3. Strengthen employer relationships
4. Consider loyalty pricing
5. Double down on referral program
